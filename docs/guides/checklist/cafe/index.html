30-point Google Business Profile checklist for cafes

This 30-point Google Business Profile checklist for cafes isn't just general advice; it's a meticulously crafted action plan designed for immediate implementation. While generic tips on optimizing your GBP are plentiful, they often lack the specificity needed to make a tangible impact on a cafe's local search presence. This checklist provides a precise, actionable sequence, ensuring every critical element is addressed, rather than merely acknowledged.

Imagine a quiet afternoon at your cafe, post-lunch rush but pre-evening lull. This is the exact sequence your team, or you, should follow. Each item is a concrete, measurable step towards enhancing your cafe's visibility, attracting more local customers, and ultimately boosting your bottom line. By methodically working through these points, you transform passive optimization knowledge into active, results-driven improvements, directly impacting how potential customers find and choose your establishment.

The three fixes on 80% of audited cafe profiles

Business Info

  • ☐ Verify business name exactly as it appears on signage and branding materials.
  • ☐ Ensure accurate street address, suite number, and postal code are consistently entered.
  • ☐ Confirm precise operating hours for every day, including specific holiday schedules.
  • ☐ Add the primary phone number for direct customer calls and reservation inquiries.
  • ☐ Include a direct, functional link to your cafe's official website or online ordering platform.

Categories

  • ☐ Set "Coffee Shop" as the primary business category for optimal local search visibility.
  • ☐ Add "Brunch Restaurant" if you offer a significant, dedicated morning meal service.
  • ☐ Include "Bakery" if you consistently bake and sell pastries, cakes, or artisan breads onsite.
  • ☐ Select "Cafe" as a relevant secondary category if it accurately describes your overall offering.
  • ☐ Review all suggested categories, adding only those genuinely descriptive of your cafe's services.

Photos

  • ☐ Upload at least 5 high-quality, inviting photos of your cafe's interior ambiance and seating.
  • ☐ Feature 5 professional shots of your most popular coffee beverages, including latte art.
  • ☐ Add 5 appealing images of your signature brunch dishes and freshly baked goods.
  • ☐ Include 3 clear exterior shots, showcasing your cafe's storefront, signage, and entrance.
  • ☐ Ensure your logo is uploaded as the profile picture and is high-resolution for clarity.

Services

  • ☐ List specific coffee drinks like espresso, latte, cappuccino, and various pour-over options.
  • ☐ Detail brunch menu items such as avocado toast, pancakes, breakfast sandwiches, and specials.
  • ☐ Add bakery offerings like croissants, muffins, cookies, specialty cakes, and custom orders.
  • ☐ Specify any catering services for events or bulk coffee and pastry orders.
  • ☐ Clearly indicate "Dine-in," "Takeout," and "Delivery" options, linking to relevant platforms.

Reviews

  • ☐ Respond promptly and courteously to all new customer reviews, both positive and critical.
  • ☐ Address any negative feedback professionally, offering genuine solutions or apologies.
  • ☐ Encourage satisfied customers to leave reviews using QR codes or gentle verbal prompts.
  • ☐ Thank customers for positive feedback, perhaps mentioning specific items they enjoyed.
  • ☐ Regularly monitor competitor reviews to identify service improvement opportunities or gaps.

Posts

  • ☐ Create a "What's New" post highlighting a seasonal coffee special or new pastry item.
  • ☐ Announce upcoming cafe events like live music, poetry nights, or tasting sessions.
  • ☐ Share an "Offer" post with a discount code for coffee, brunch, or bakery purchases.
  • ☐ Publish an "Update" post about temporary holiday hours or service changes.
  • ☐ Post a "Product" update showcasing a new menu item with an enticing photo and description.

Why these matter specifically for cafes in Checklist

After meticulously checking off all 30 items on this Google Business Profile checklist, the work isn't entirely done. Now, the crucial step is to monitor the impact of your efforts. Utilize the "Insights" section within your GBP dashboard to track key metrics like search views, map views, website clicks, and phone calls over the next 14 days. This allows sufficient time for Google's algorithm to re-index your updated profile and for changes to reflect in user behavior. After this period, review the data, identify areas for further improvement, and then repeat the process, making incremental adjustments for continuous optimization.
