The three fixes on 80% of audited photographer profiles

  • Shortchanged Business Descriptions

    Many Edinburgh photographers use a description that is too brief, missing valuable keyword opportunities. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet most profiles barely scratch 200, simply stating "Edinburgh photographer." This omits crucial details about your specialisms, style, and unique selling points, preventing Google from fully understanding and ranking your photography business for relevant searches beyond the basic.

  • Neglected Secondary Categories

    Photographers often only select "Photographer" as their primary category, overlooking vital secondary options. For example, if you specialise in particular niches, failing to add "Wedding Photographer," "Portrait Studio," or "Real Estate Photography" means you are invisible to those specific search queries. This significantly narrows your reach to potential clients actively looking for your specialised services in Edinburgh and surrounding areas.

  • Insufficient Visual Storytelling

    Many photography profiles fall short of the ideal 30+ owner-uploaded photos, hindering visual engagement and SEO. Beyond portfolio shots, missing photos often include interior/exterior studio views, behind-the-scenes glimpses, headshots of the team, and even client interaction shots. These images build trust and give potential clients a comprehensive view of your brand and what to expect from your Edinburgh photography services.

Why these matter specifically for photographers in Edinburgh, UK

  • Edinburgh is a highly competitive market for photographers. With a high density of talented professionals, standing out on Google Maps requires every possible advantage. Neglecting these GBP details means your profile gets lost amidst hundreds of others when locals search for "wedding photographer Edinburgh" or "family portraits Old Town," directly impacting your booking calendar.

  • Customer search behavior for photographers in Edinburgh is often location-specific. People frequently search for services within their immediate neighbourhood, or specific parts of the city like Stockbridge, Morningside, or Leith. An optimised GBP helps Google match you with these hyper-local searches, ensuring you are found by clients who are genuinely close by and ready to book.

  • Commute patterns also play a significant role. Clients usually prefer photographers who are easily accessible, especially for studio sessions or local outdoor shoots. A thoroughly completed Google Business Profile, with accurate service areas and rich descriptions, signals to potential clients that you are a convenient and reliable local choice, reducing their perceived travel burden.

  • The visual nature of photography means high-quality, diverse images on your GBP are non-negotiable. In a city like Edinburgh, where visual appeal is paramount, a profile lacking engaging photos will quickly be overlooked. Customers are not just looking for a photographer; they are looking for a visual artist whose work resonates, and your GBP is their first impression.
