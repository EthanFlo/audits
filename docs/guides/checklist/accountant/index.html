30-point Google Business Profile checklist for accountants

Here is your "30-point Google Business Profile checklist for accountants":

When it comes to boosting your online presence, generic advice like "optimize your profile" often falls short. What an accountant truly needs are precise, actionable steps they can systematically follow to ensure every aspect of their Google Business Profile (GBP) is working as hard as they do. This isn't a vague suggestion; it's a focused blueprint designed to yield tangible improvements in local search visibility.

Imagine a slow afternoon in the office, client meetings are done, and the books are balanced. Instead of aimlessly browsing, you can open this checklist and systematically work through each item. Each point represents a direct, impactful action that, once completed, contributes to a more robust, client-attracting GBP. This is your definitive sequence for transforming your accounting firm's digital footprint.

The three fixes on 80% of audited accountant profiles

Business Info

  • ☐ Verify your exact accounting firm name matches official documents (e.g., "Smith & Co. CPA").
  • ☐ Ensure your physical office address is accurate and consistent across all online listings.
  • ☐ Confirm your primary business phone number is correct and directly reachable by clients.
  • ☐ Update your website URL to the current, mobile-friendly site for your accounting practice.
  • ☐ Specify precise opening hours for all service days, including any seasonal tax season extensions.

Categories

  • ☐ Set "Accountant" as your primary business category for maximum relevance to searchers.
  • ☐ Add "Tax Preparation Service" as a secondary category to capture specific client needs.
  • ☐ Include "Bookkeeping Service" if offered, as another key secondary category.
  • ☐ Consider adding "CPA" or "Certified Public Accountant" if available and applicable to your firm.
  • ☐ Add "Financial Advisor" if your practice provides relevant advisory or planning services.

Photos

  • ☐ Upload a high-quality exterior photo of your accounting office for easy recognition.
  • ☐ Include interior photos showcasing your professional workspace and client waiting area.
  • ☐ Add professional team photos, featuring your accountants, to build trust and rapport.
  • ☐ Upload your accounting firm's professional logo as the primary profile picture.
  • ☐ Feature photos of local landmarks near your office to enhance local context and appeal.

Services

  • ☐ List "Tax Preparation" with a detailed description of individual and business tax services.
  • ☐ Add "Bookkeeping" outlining ongoing reconciliation, financial reporting, and compliance.
  • ☐ Include "Payroll Services" with specifics on processing, direct deposit, and tax filings.
  • ☐ Detail "Small Business CPA Services," covering setup, consulting, and growth strategies.
  • ☐ Create custom services for specialized offerings like "Audit Representation" or "Forensic Accounting."

Reviews

  • ☐ Respond promptly and professionally to all new client reviews, positive or negative.
  • ☐ Actively encourage satisfied clients to leave a Google review after a successful engagement.
  • ☐ Address any negative reviews constructively, offering solutions or clarifications where appropriate.
  • ☐ Highlight positive reviews by sharing excerpts on your website or social media (with permission).
  • ☐ Use a direct link to your GBP review form in email signatures and client communications.

Posts

  • ☐ Create a "What's New" post about upcoming tax deadlines (e.g., 1099, W2 filing reminders).
  • ☐ Publish an "Offer" post for a free initial consultation for new business clients.
  • ☐ Share an "Event" post about a webinar on new tax law changes or small business finance.
  • ☐ Use a "Product" post to feature a specific service package (e.g., "Startup Business Accounting Package").
  • ☐ Write a general informational post relevant to accountants, like year-end financial planning tips.

Why these matter specifically for accountants in Checklist

After diligently working through all 30 points on this checklist, resist the urge to immediately tweak or change things. Give Google's algorithms at least 14 days to process and index your updated information. Following this waiting period, dive into your Google Business Profile Insights to measure the tangible impact on views, calls, direction requests, and website clicks. This data-driven approach will inform your next optimization cycle, ensuring your accounting practice continues to improve its online visibility and client acquisition strategy.
