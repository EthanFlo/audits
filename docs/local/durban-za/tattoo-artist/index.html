The three fixes on 80% of audited tattoo artist profiles

  • Generic Business Descriptions

    Many Durban tattoo artists overlook the power of their Google Business Profile description. I frequently find profiles with descriptions under 400 characters, failing to utilise Google's generous 750-character limit. This means you're missing a vital opportunity to highlight your signature styles—be it Polynesian, neo-traditional, custom script, or cover-up expertise—and distinguish your studio from the competition, leaving potential clients unclear about your specialisations.

  • Missing Specific Secondary Categories

    Your Google Business Profile should do more than just list "Tattoo Shop." I often see profiles missing crucial secondary categories. Adding specific descriptors like "Fine Line Tattoo Artist," "Traditional Tattoo Artist," or "Cover-Up Tattoo Service" can dramatically improve visibility for niche searches. Without these, your studio might not appear when clients in Durban are specifically looking for the unique style you master, limiting your reach to highly targeted clientele.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Clients choose tattoo artists visually, yet many profiles feature fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. This often means missing critical elements like high-quality, diverse portfolio shots showcasing healed work, detailed close-ups of specific styles, the clean, inviting ambiance of your studio, or even behind-the-scenes glimpses of your artistic process. A lack of rich visual content leaves potential clients unable to connect with your artistry and build trust.
