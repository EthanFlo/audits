The three fixes on 80% of audited locksmith profiles

*

Underutilised Business Description

Many Glasgow locksmiths leave their Google Business Profile description brief, often under 400 characters. This wastes valuable space that could be used to detail your 24/7 emergency service, specific lock types you handle (e.g., UPVC, mortice), residential and commercial specialisms, and areas served within Glasgow. A comprehensive 750-character description helps Google understand your full service offering and matches you to more specific customer searches.

*

Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

Often, locksmith profiles are set to just 'Locksmith' as their primary category, neglecting vital secondary categories. For a Glasgow locksmith, adding categories like 'Emergency Locksmith', 'Auto Locksmith', and 'Safe Opening Service' is essential. These categories signal to Google the breadth of your expertise, allowing you to appear in a wider range of specific searches beyond just a general 'locksmith near me' query in Glasgow.

*

Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

With fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, many Glasgow locksmiths miss a massive opportunity to showcase their work and professionalism. Missing images often include team photos (building trust), vans (local presence), various lock repairs (demonstrating expertise), tools, and 'before and after' shots of completed jobs. High-quality, diverse photos significantly boost engagement and trust, crucial for customers seeking urgent services.
