The three fixes on 80% of audited dentist profiles

*

Underutilized Business Description (Under 400 Characters)

Many dental practices in Kansas City, MO, are leaving hundreds of characters on the table in their Google Business Profile description. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet most dentists only use a fraction of that. This often means missing vital keywords about your family-friendly atmosphere, advanced cosmetic treatments like veneers and whitening, or specialized care for anxious patients, preventing you from ranking for crucial patient searches. *

Missing Key Secondary Categories

Your primary category as a "Dentist" is just the starting point. I frequently find local dental profiles missing crucial secondary categories that significantly broaden their reach. By adding relevant specific categories like "Cosmetic Dentist," "Pediatric Dentist," and "Emergency Dental Service," you ensure your practice appears for a wider range of high-intent patient searches in Kansas City seeking specialized care. *

Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

Google favors robust, visually engaging profiles, yet many KC dental practices have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. Patients want to see your modern office, a welcoming staff, and even before-and-after treatment examples. Missing photos of your waiting area, treatment rooms, smiling team, specific equipment, or even patient testimonials leaves your profile looking sparse and less trustworthy compared to photo-rich competitors.
