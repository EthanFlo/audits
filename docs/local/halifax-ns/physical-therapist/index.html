The three fixes on 80% of audited physical therapist profiles

  • Underutilized Business Description

    Many Halifax physical therapists miss a prime opportunity by keeping their Google Business Profile description under 400 characters, well short of the allowed 750. This brief text often provides generic information instead of highlighting unique specializations like vestibular rehabilitation, advanced manual therapy techniques, or specific sports injury recovery programs. A sparse description fails to capture the attention of prospective patients searching for very specific needs in the Dartmouth or Bedford areas, leaving them without a clear understanding of your clinic's distinct offerings and expertise.

  • Missing Secondary Categories

    A common oversight is relying solely on "Physical Therapist" as the primary category, neglecting crucial secondary categories that significantly broaden reach. For physical therapists in Halifax, I often find profiles missing key additions like "Sports injury clinic," "Rehabilitation center," and "Back pain treatment." These categories allow your clinic to appear in more diverse and specific local searches, connecting you with patients who might be looking for a particular service rather than just a general physical therapist, especially important in a competitive urban environment.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Your Google Business Profile should visually immerse potential patients in your clinic's environment, yet many Halifax physical therapy profiles feature fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. This often means missing critical visuals like detailed shots of your modern treatment rooms, a diverse team demonstrating professionalism, specialized equipment in use (e.g., shockwave therapy, traction tables), clear exterior shots for easy navigation around downtown or Clayton Park, and patient testimonials or success stories depicted visually. Such visual gaps prevent clinics from building trust and showcasing their unique atmosphere.
