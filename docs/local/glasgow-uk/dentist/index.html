The three fixes on 80% of audited dentist profiles

  • Underutilised Business Description

    Many Glasgow dental practices only use a fraction of the 750 characters available for their Google Business Profile description, often leaving out crucial details. This means missing vital opportunities to highlight your clinic's specialisations like cosmetic dentistry or advanced oral surgery, your commitment to patient comfort, or the cutting-edge technology you employ. A concise yet comprehensive description is key to setting your practice apart and attracting the right patients actively seeking your specific expertise in the city.

  • Missing Secondary Categories

    Beyond the primary 'Dentist' category, many Glasgow dental practices neglect to add relevant secondary categories. This is a huge oversight, as Google allows you to specify up to nine. Common missed categories include 'Cosmetic Dentist,' 'Pediatric Dentist,' and 'Emergency Dental Service.' Adding these ensures your practice appears in more targeted searches, capturing a wider range of patients looking for specific dental solutions, from a child's first check-up to urgent weekend care.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Google Business Profiles with limited owner-uploaded photos often fail to engage potential patients. We frequently find practices with fewer than 30 images, missing critical visuals that build trust and familiarity. This often means no photos of your welcoming reception area, modern treatment rooms, friendly team members, or patient testimonials. High-quality, diverse images of your facilities and staff are essential for showcasing your practice's professional, approachable, and hygienic environment, crucial for attracting new Glasgow patients.
