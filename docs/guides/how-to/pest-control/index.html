How to optimize a pest control's Google Business Profile in 2026

Is your pest control business struggling to get calls from Google Maps? Are competitors with fewer reviews outranking you, leaving your profile buried on page two? I know the frustration of seeing your prime service areas dominated by others while your phones stay silent. It feels like you're invisible when potential customers search for "pest control near me."

I will show you how to dominate your local search results in 2026. This guide will transform your Google Business Profile from an afterthought into a lead-generating machine. Follow these steps, and you will consistently attract more high-quality leads and book more appointments directly from local Google searches.

The three fixes on 80% of audited pest control profiles

  1. Craft a Compelling Description: Write a 750-character description. Clearly state your service areas and specialty treatments like termite, bed bug, or rodent control. Use keywords naturally that potential customers search for, describing your expertise and commitment to effective, safe pest solutions.
  2. Select the Precise Primary Category: Your primary category is crucial. For most pest control businesses, "Pest control service" is the correct choice. Do not choose overly niche categories like "Termite exterminator" here, as it limits your broad reach for general pest issues. This defines your core business to Google.
  3. Add Relevant Secondary Categories: Expand your visibility by adding up to nine secondary categories. Include "Termite control service," "Rodent exterminator," "Bed bug exterminator," and "Ant exterminator." Add others like "Mosquito control service" or "Wildlife removal service" if applicable to your offerings.
  4. Detail Your Service List: Populate the "Services" section comprehensively. List every specific service you offer, such as "Termite Inspection," "Bed Bug Heat Treatment," "Cockroach Extermination," "Rodent Trapping," and "Mosquito Misting." This helps Google match you to precise user queries.
  5. Upload High-Quality Photos and Videos: Regularly add geolocated photos and short videos of your team in action, service vehicles, equipment, and before-and-after pest situations. Aim for at least 3-5 new photos monthly. Visuals build trust and demonstrate professionalism to potential clients.
  6. Maintain Consistent Google Posts: Publish a Google Post at least weekly. Announce special offers on seasonal pests like mosquitos or ants, share success stories, or highlight new services. These posts keep your profile active and visible, encouraging direct customer engagement.
  7. Prioritize Review Velocity: Actively solicit new 5-star reviews from every satisfied customer. Implement a simple process for requesting reviews immediately after service completion. High review volume, especially recent reviews, significantly boosts your local ranking and customer trust.
  8. Seed Your Q&A Section: Proactively answer common questions potential customers might have. Ask colleagues or staff to post questions like "Do you offer emergency bed bug service?" or "Are your treatments pet-safe?" and then provide thorough, keyword-rich answers yourself.

Why these matter specifically for pest controls in How To

  • Not responding to all reviews: Ignoring negative feedback or failing to thank positive reviewers makes your business appear disengaged. Always respond professionally to every review, good or bad, demonstrating excellent customer service.
  • Stuffing keywords in your business name: Adding "Best Termite & Rodent Control Experts 24/7" to your official business name violates Google's guidelines. Use only your legal business name to avoid penalties and improve trustworthiness.
  • Failing to update hours for holidays: Leaving incorrect holiday hours leads to frustrated customers and negative experiences. Regularly update your operating hours, especially for special events or holidays, to manage customer expectations.
  • Using stock photos for your profile: Generic images do not build trust or showcase your unique team. Always use authentic, high-quality photos of your actual technicians, vehicles, and real-world service situations.
