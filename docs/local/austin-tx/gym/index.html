The three fixes on 80% of audited gym profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many Austin gyms neglect their Google Business Profile description, leaving it under 400 characters when Google allows 750. This means you’re missing a huge opportunity to highlight what makes your gym unique. You need to elaborate on your specialized equipment, the expertise of your personal trainers, the range of classes offered, and the welcoming community atmosphere that sets your gym apart from the rest. This isn't just text; it's your digital storefront's sales pitch.

  • Missing Key Secondary Categories

    A common oversight for gyms is failing to add relevant secondary categories beyond just "Gym." In Austin, members search for very specific fitness niches. Many profiles are missing crucial additions like "CrossFit Gym," "Yoga Studio," or "Personal Trainer." Without these, you're invisible to people actively seeking those exact services, effectively narrowing your potential member pool and handing those leads directly to more accurately categorized competitors.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Google Business Profiles with less than 30 owner-uploaded photos rarely capture attention. Prospective members want to visualize your gym before visiting. Many profiles lack high-quality images of specific workout areas, group class in action, the welcoming front desk, sparkling clean locker rooms, or even the gym’s exterior from the street. Without a rich visual story, your profile feels incomplete and less inviting, losing out to gyms that show their vibrant environment.
