The three fixes on 80% of audited dermatologist profiles

  • Your Google Business Profile Description is Undersized

    Many dermatologists in Albuquerque leave their Google Business Profile description severely underutilized, often well under the 750-character limit. This oversight means you're missing a prime opportunity to include vital keywords like "acne treatment," "skin cancer screening," "cosmetic dermatology," or "pediatric dermatology Albuquerque." A comprehensive description helps Google understand your specializations, making your practice more visible to patients actively searching for those exact services.

  • Critical Secondary Categories Are Missing

    Google Business Profile allows you to list multiple categories, but I often find Albuquerque dermatology profiles only using "Dermatologist." This significantly limits your reach. To capture a wider audience, you should include specific secondary categories such as "Cosmetic Dermatology," "Skin Cancer Screening Service," and "Pediatric Dermatology." These help your practice appear in more precise searches, connecting you with patients looking for very particular types of dermatological care.

  • A Sparse Photo Gallery Detracts from Your Practice

    A common finding is that dermatology practices in Albuquerque have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos on their Google Business Profile. Patients want to see your office environment, meet your staff (with permission), and visualize their visit before they book. Missing photos like your welcoming waiting room, treatment areas, exterior signage, doctor headshots, and patient education materials means you're not building the trust and familiarity that potential patients seek online.
