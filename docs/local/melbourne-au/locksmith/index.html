The three fixes on 80% of audited locksmith profiles

  • Underutilized Business Description

    Many Melbourne locksmiths leave significant visibility on the table by using only a fraction of the available 750 characters for their Google Business Profile description. Often, I find profiles with less than 400 characters, missing vital keywords and detailed service explanations. This squanders a prime opportunity to inform potential clients about your full range of services, from residential lock changes to advanced commercial security systems, hindering your ability to stand out from local competitors who are optimizing this space effectively.

  • Missing Secondary Categories

    A common oversight for locksmiths in Melbourne is failing to add crucial secondary categories beyond just "Locksmith." This severely limits how many different types of searches you can rank for. Profiles frequently lack important additions like "Emergency Locksmith," "Auto Locksmith," or "Safe Opening Service," which directly match what potential customers are typing into Google. Expanding these categories ensures your business appears for a wider, more relevant array of specific locksmith needs.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Your Google Business Profile should be a visual showcase, yet I often see Melbourne locksmith profiles with fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. This means you're likely missing crucial imagery like your branded service vehicles, your team in uniform, specific lock hardware, before-and-after security upgrades, or photos of your workshop. Visuals build trust and demonstrate professionalism, and a lack of them makes your profile less engaging and less likely to convert lookers into callers.
