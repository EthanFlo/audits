Duplicate GBP Listings Explained

A duplicate Google Business Profile (GBP) listing occurs when Google's systems, or a business owner, creates multiple entries for the exact same physical business location. These are redundant profiles that point to the same address, phone number, or core business entity, often causing confusion for both Google's ranking algorithms and potential customers. Such duplicates can arise accidentally, often due to historical management issues, changes in business name, or simply oversight.

The presence of duplicate GBP listings significantly hinders a business's ability to rank effectively in local search results, including the crucial Google Map Pack. Google strives to provide the most accurate and authoritative information, and duplicate profiles fragment a business's online presence. Instead of consolidating positive signals like reviews and website authority into one strong profile, duplicates dilute these signals across multiple entries. This can lead to lower visibility, reduced customer trust, and even profile suspensions if Google perceives manipulative intent, making a single, verified, and robust profile per unique location essential for local SEO success.

Consider the example of a Chicago dentist, Dr. Maya Patel, who operates a single practice in the Loop. Several years ago, an administrative assistant set up a Google Business Profile under the name "Maya Patel Dentistry." Later, Dr. Patel, unaware of the existing profile, created a new one herself, perhaps using a slightly different business name like "Dr. Patel's Chicago Dental Clinic." Both profiles list the identical street address, phone number, and website. This scenario creates two distinct GBP listings for what is, in reality, a single dental office, serving as a classic example of duplicate GBP listings.

A common misconception is that having multiple listings somehow increases a business's visibility; in reality, it does the opposite by confusing Google and customers. Business owners sometimes mistakenly believe that a slight variation in the business name or a different contact person warrants a new listing, even for the same physical location. Another frequent mistake is neglecting to audit and remove old, defunct profiles from previous business owners or locations, which can resurface as unwanted duplicates. It is important not to confuse a legitimate service area business (which serves customers at their location rather than a storefront) with a duplicate profile for a brick-and-mortar store.

Resolving duplicate GBP listings is a critical step for improving local search performance. Here is actionable advice:

  • Identify Duplicates: Thoroughly search Google Maps and Google Search using your business name, address, and phone number (including variations) to uncover all existing profiles.
  • Choose Primary Profile: Select the most complete, active, and well-optimized profile as your primary listing. This profile should have the most reviews and accurate information.
  • Report or Remove Duplicates:
    • If you have access to the duplicate profile within your Google Business Profile manager, you can often mark it as "Permanently Closed" or, in some cases, request a merge.
    • For profiles you do not manage, use the "Suggest an edit" feature on Google Maps to mark the duplicate as "Does not exist" or "Permanently closed" (if appropriate).
  • Maintain NAP Consistency: Ensure your Name, Address, and Phone number (NAP) are identical across your chosen primary GBP listing and all other online directories.
  • Seek Google Support: If you encounter difficulties or are unsure how to proceed, contact Google Business Profile support for assistance.
