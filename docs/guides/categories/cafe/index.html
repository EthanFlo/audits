Best Google Business categories for a cafe

Optimizing your Google Business Profile is a critical, yet often underestimated, strategy for local SEO success. Many cafe owners focus solely on appealing photos and accurate opening hours, overlooking the powerful impact of properly chosen business categories. Google allows businesses to select one primary category and up to nine secondary categories, effectively creating ten distinct ranking "lanes" for potential customers to find you.

The vast majority of cafes make the mistake of selecting only a primary category, immediately putting them at a disadvantage. By failing to utilize the additional nine secondary category slots, these businesses miss out on a massive opportunity to rank for diverse, high-intent searches. Each category serves as a signal to Google, telling the algorithm exactly what types of services or products you offer, ensuring you appear in relevant searches beyond just your core offering.

The three fixes on 80% of audited cafe profiles

Coffee shop

"Coffee shop" is the optimal primary category for most cafes due to its direct relevance to the core offering and high search volume. While "Cafe" is also a strong contender, "Coffee shop" often implies a stronger emphasis on artisanal coffee and espresso drinks, aligning with consumer expectations for quality brews. Choosing this category prioritizes searches from users specifically seeking coffee, espresso, and related beverages, which is typically the main draw for this type of establishment, differentiating it from a broader "Restaurant" or "Bakery" that might not prioritize coffee as much.

Why these matter specifically for cafes in Categories

  1. Cafe: Unlocks broader searches for "cafe near me" or general places to relax and grab a drink or light meal.
  2. Bakery: Captures customers actively searching for fresh pastries, bread, cakes, and other baked goods.
  3. Brunch restaurant: Attracts weekend crowds specifically looking for popular brunch menus and experiences.
  4. Breakfast restaurant: Targets early morning patrons searching for breakfast options, from pastries to full meals.
  5. Dessert shop: Appeals to those seeking sweet treats, ice cream, or cakes after a meal or as a standalone indulgence.
  6. Sandwich shop: Positions your business for lunchtime searches from users specifically craving sandwiches, wraps, or paninis.
  7. Lunch restaurant: Broadens your reach for general lunchtime dining queries beyond just sandwiches.
  8. Tea house: If you offer a significant and varied selection of loose-leaf teas or specialty tea drinks.
  9. Restaurant: A crucial catch-all to ensure visibility for general dining searches if your cafe offers a more extensive food menu.
