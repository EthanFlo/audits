The three fixes on 80% of audited gym profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many Bristol gyms only use a few sentences to describe their unique offerings. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet most profiles barely hit 400. You're missing a huge opportunity to showcase your specialist equipment, unique class styles, and the welcoming atmosphere that makes your gym stand out in a crowded market like Bristol.

  • Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    While you might have "Gym" as your primary category, many profiles stop there. To capture specific searches, you need to add relevant secondary categories. We often find Bristol gyms missing vital additions like "CrossFit Gym," "Yoga Studio," "Personal Trainer," or even "Health Club" if applicable, narrowing their reach significantly.

  • Too Few Photos (Under 30)

    A picture is worth a thousand words, especially for a visual business like a gym. Most Bristol gym profiles have fewer than 15 owner-uploaded photos. You should be showcasing high-quality images of your cardio areas, free weights, group class in action, changing rooms, and even your friendly front desk staff, creating a comprehensive visual tour for potential members.
