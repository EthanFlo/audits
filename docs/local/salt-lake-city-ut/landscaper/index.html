The three fixes on 80% of audited landscaper profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many landscapers in Salt Lake City miss a crucial opportunity by keeping their Google Business Profile description under 400 characters, when Google allows 750. This means you're likely omitting valuable keywords like "xeriscaping design," "sprinkler repair," "retaining walls," or "paver installation" that local clients are actively searching for. A robust, keyword-rich description helps Google understand your full range of services and match you with more relevant searches across the Salt Lake Valley.

  • Missing Out on Key Service Categories

    Beyond your primary category, neglecting secondary categories severely limits your visibility. For a Salt Lake City landscaper, this could mean not showing up for specialized searches. I often find profiles missing critical additions such as "Lawn Care Service," "Hardscape Contractor," or "Tree Service." These specific categories directly inform Google about your expertise, making it far more likely you’ll appear for targeted local searches, driving more qualified leads for your specific offerings.

  • Not Enough Visual Proof of Your Work

    With fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, your Google Business Profile isn't showcasing the full breadth of your landscaping talent. Potential clients in Salt Lake City want to see your projects come to life. This often means missing crucial "before & after" shots of garden makeovers, detailed photos of completed patios or walkways, examples of your winter snow removal, or even inviting team photos. High-quality visuals are essential for trust and conversion in a visual industry like landscaping.
