How Many Categories Can You Have on Google Business Profile?

You can have one primary category and up to nine additional categories on your Google Business Profile, for a total of ten categories.

Understanding Google Business Profile Categories

Google Business Profile (GBP) categories serve as the fundamental classification system that tells Google and potential customers exactly what your business does. These categories are far more than mere tags; they are crucial determinants of your online visibility, influencing which search queries your business appears for and the specific features displayed on your profile (for instance, a "Restaurant" category might enable menu listings, while a "Hair Salon" might offer booking options).

By accurately defining your business type, categories help Google match your profile with relevant local searches. For consumers, categories provide immediate context, allowing them to quickly understand if your business offers the products or services they are seeking. They are a cornerstone of effective local SEO, helping your business stand out in a competitive digital landscape.

The Specifics: Primary and Additional Category Limits

Google Business Profile allows you to assign up to ten categories to your business, structured as one primary category and up to nine additional categories.

  • Primary Category: This is the single most important category you will choose. It should represent the core function or main service of your business. The primary category significantly impacts what Google understands your business to be, affecting your ranking for broad searches and dictating the specific features and attributes that become available on your profile. For example, a business listed as a "Coffee Shop" will have different display options than one listed as a "Hardware Store."
  • Additional Categories: After selecting your primary category, you have the option to add up to nine more categories. These additional categories should reflect secondary services, specialized offerings, or other relevant aspects of your business. While less influential than the primary category, they still play a vital role in broadening your reach, allowing your business to appear for more specific or tangential search terms. It is important that all chosen categories accurately describe services offered at your physical location.

Choosing your categories carefully is paramount. While you have a generous allowance of ten, quality and relevance far outweigh quantity. Each category should genuinely represent a distinct aspect of your business operations.

Strategic Category Selection for Maximum Impact

Selecting the right categories for your Google Business Profile is a strategic decision that directly impacts your visibility and customer engagement. Here are best practices for making effective choices:

  1. Prioritize Your Core Offering: Your primary category must unequivocally state what your business primarily does. If you run a bakery that also serves coffee, and baking is your main revenue stream, "Bakery" should be your primary category, not "Coffee Shop."
  2. Be Specific, Yet Broad Enough: Utilize Google's predefined category list. Avoid trying to invent categories. Search for terms that describe your business and see what Google offers. For example, if you are a "Yoga Studio," you might find that as a direct category, rather than trying to use something like "Mindfulness Center," which might be too broad or not available.
  3. Think Like a Customer: What terms would a potential customer use to search for your services? If someone needs their brakes repaired, they might search "Auto Repair Shop" or "Brake Shop," not necessarily "Vehicle Maintenance Service."
  4. Utilize All Relevant Additional Categories: Don't leave slots empty if you genuinely offer more services. For "The Savory Spoon," a local eatery, the categories might look like this:
    • Primary: Restaurant
    • Additional: American Restaurant, Brunch Restaurant, Lunch Restaurant, Catering Service, Bar

    This comprehensive approach ensures "The Savory Spoon" is discoverable for various related searches, from those looking for a specific type of cuisine to those needing event catering.

  5. Research Competitors: While you should not simply copy, observing the categories chosen by successful, relevant competitors can provide insights into effective strategies in your specific industry.

How to Add or Edit Categories on Your Google Business Profile

Managing your Google Business Profile categories is a straightforward process. Here are the steps to add or adjust them:

  1. Access Your Profile: Log in to the Google account associated with your Google Business Profile. You can either search for your business name directly on Google (if managing directly on Search/Maps) or go to business.google.com.
  2. Select Your Business: If you manage multiple profiles, ensure you select the correct business location.
  3. Navigate to 'Info' or 'Edit Profile': Depending on whether you are using the new Google Search/Maps interface or the traditional Business Profile Manager dashboard, look for an "Edit profile" button or an "Info" tab on the left-hand menu.
  4. Locate the Categories Section: Within the edit or info section, find the field labeled "Categories" or "Business category." Your current categories will be listed there.
  5. Edit Primary Category: To change your primary category, click on it and start typing. Google will suggest relevant predefined categories. Select the one that best suits your core business.
  6. Add Additional Categories: Below your primary category, there should be an option to "Add another category" or a similar button. Click this and begin typing to see Google's suggestions. Select all relevant additional categories, up to the maximum of nine.
  7. Save Your Changes: After making all your adjustments, click "Save" (or "Apply," depending on the interface) to ensure your updates are submitted.

It may take some time, typically a few hours to a few days, for your updated categories to be fully reflected across Google Search and Maps.

Common Category Mistakes to Avoid

While the process of selecting categories seems simple, several common pitfalls can hinder your business's online performance:

  • Using Too Few Categories: Not utilizing the full allowance of ten categories means you might be missing opportunities to rank for secondary services that customers are actively searching for.
  • Choosing Irrelevant Categories: Adding categories that do not accurately describe your services can confuse Google's algorithm and potential customers. This can lead to your business appearing in irrelevant searches, resulting in poor click-through rates and potentially even penalization for misleading information.
  • Selecting Overly Broad Categories: While a broad category might seem inclusive, it can make it harder for your business to stand out. For example, a specialized "Vegan Bakery" might gain more specific visibility with that category rather than just "Bakery," if "Vegan Bakery" is an available and accurate option.
  • Ignoring Google's Predefined List: You cannot create custom categories. Only choose from Google's existing list of hundreds of options. Attempting to enter a non-existent category will simply not work.
  • Focusing on Products Instead of Business Type: Categories should describe what your business is (e.g., "Clothing Store"), not every individual product you sell (e.g., "Men's Jeans," "Women's Dresses"). Specific products are best listed in your products or services section.
  • Not Reviewing Periodically: Business offerings evolve, and so do Google's category lists. Periodically review your chosen categories to ensure they remain accurate and optimal for your current services.
