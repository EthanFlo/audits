The three fixes on 80% of audited restaurant profiles

  • 1. Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many Edmonton restaurants overlook the powerful 750-character Google Business Profile description. Often, I find profiles with only a brief sentence or two, barely scratching the surface of what makes your eatery unique. This valuable space is perfect for highlighting your signature dishes, unique ambiance, or commitment to local ingredients, telling potential diners exactly why they should choose you over the competition. Maximizing this helps your restaurant stand out.

  • 2. Missing Specific Secondary Categories

    Google allows you to use up to nine additional categories beyond your primary one, yet many Edmonton restaurants only list "Restaurant." This is a huge missed opportunity. If you're an "Italian Restaurant," "Sushi Restaurant," or a "Steakhouse," adding these specific categories helps you appear in highly targeted searches. Without them, you're invisible to potential patrons actively seeking your exact cuisine.

  • 3. Too Few High-Quality Owner Photos

    A visually appealing Google Business Profile is crucial for Edmonton restaurants, yet many profiles feature fewer than 30 owner-uploaded images. This means diners aren't seeing your beautifully plated entrees, inviting interior, bustling kitchen, or happy customers. High-resolution photos of your menu items, signature cocktails, and vibrant atmosphere are essential for enticing hungry locals and conveying the full experience you offer.
