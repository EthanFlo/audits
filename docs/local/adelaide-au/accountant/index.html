The three fixes on 80% of audited accountant profiles

1. Your Business Description is Too Short

Many Adelaide accountants are missing a huge opportunity by only using a few sentences in their Google Business Profile description. Google allows up to 750 characters, but I often see profiles using less than 400. This is your chance to clearly articulate your specialisations – whether it's tax planning for small businesses, SMSF audits, or detailed financial consulting for local startups. A detailed description helps Google understand exactly what you offer and matches you with the right clients searching for specific accounting solutions in Adelaide.

2. Missing Key Secondary Categories

I consistently find accountant profiles with only "Accountant" as their primary category. This seriously limits your visibility. To truly stand out, you need to add specific secondary categories like "Tax Preparation", "Bookkeeping Service", and "Financial Planner". These categories help potential clients find you when they're searching for specific services, not just a generic accountant. Broadening your categories accurately tells Google and your Adelaide prospects the full scope of your valuable offerings.

3. Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

A surprising number of local accountant profiles are starved for photos, often having less than 10, when Google recommends 30+. You're missing out on showcasing your professional yet approachable team, your inviting Adelaide office interior, client meeting spaces, and even local landmarks near your premises. High-quality photos build trust and give clients a real feel for your practice before they even call. This visual connection is crucial for attracting clients in the Adelaide market.
