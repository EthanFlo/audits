The three fixes on 80% of audited hair salon profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many Halifax hair salons underutilize the Google Business Profile description, often leaving it under 400 characters when 750 are allowed. This critical space is your chance to highlight your unique atmosphere, signature cutting techniques, colour expertise, or premium product lines. A brief description fails to capture what truly sets your salon apart from others, missing opportunities to entice potential clients searching for specific styles or experiences.

  • Missing Vital Secondary Categories

    Beyond "Hair Salon," Google offers numerous specific secondary categories that dramatically improve search visibility for niche services. I frequently find Halifax salons missing crucial ones like "Colorist," "Men's Barber," or "Bridal Hair Salon." Without these, clients specifically searching for these specialized services often won't find you, even if you excel in those areas. Adding them broadens your reach significantly to a highly targeted audience.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Hair is a visual business, yet many local salons lack a robust photo gallery on their Google Business Profile. I often see profiles with fewer than 30 owner-uploaded images, missing critical visuals like stunning before-and-after client transformations, diverse hairstyle portfolios, inviting salon interiors, and professional team portraits. A limited gallery fails to showcase your artistry and can deter clients who rely on visual evidence when choosing a salon.
