The three fixes on 80% of audited accountant profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Brief

    Many Sydney accountants use fewer than 400 characters for their Google Business Profile description, missing out on valuable SEO opportunities. Google allows up to 750 characters. This ample space should be used to detail your specializations, like tax planning for medical professionals, SMSF auditing, or cloud accounting expertise. A well-crafted, keyword-rich description helps Google understand your specific services, allowing your profile to appear for a wider range of relevant local searches and capture more targeted clients.

  • You're Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Many accounting practices in Sydney only use one or two categories beyond "Accountant." Google Business Profile allows up to 9. By not leveraging categories like "Tax Preparation Service," "Bookkeeping Service," and "Financial Planner," you're implicitly telling Google your service range is narrow. This omission prevents your profile from appearing for specific client searches, ceding visibility and potential leads to competitors who have fully optimised their categories.

  • Insufficient or Generic Photos

    Many Sydney accountants upload fewer than 30 owner-specific photos, often generic stock images or just a logo. Google prioritises authentic, high-quality visuals. You need to showcase your professional team in action, inviting client meeting areas, and clear exterior shots that help clients recognise your premises. Missing these visual cues makes your profile less engaging and trustworthy. Google often favours profiles with a richer, diverse photo gallery, impacting your local search ranking potential.
