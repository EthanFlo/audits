30-point Google Business Profile checklist for auto repair shops

Here's your "30-point Google Business Profile checklist for auto repair shops":

When the shop floor is quiet and the waiting room is empty, that's your cue to invest in your digital storefront: your Google Business Profile (GBP). Instead of sifting through generic advice on "how to improve your online presence," this checklist provides a concrete, step-by-step sequence. It's designed to be tackled in one focused session, transforming your slow afternoon into a powerful growth opportunity.

This isn't about vague recommendations; it's about specific, actionable items tailored for auto repair shops. Each point is a direct task, ensuring you cover every critical aspect of your GBP. By methodically checking off these 30 items, you're not just "optimizing"; you're building a robust, high-visibility profile that actively attracts more local customers needing everything from an oil change to complex engine diagnostics.

The three fixes on 80% of audited auto repair shop profiles

Business Info

  • ☐ Confirm your official business name is exact, including any DBA.
  • ☐ Verify your shop's physical street address is precise and matches USPS records.
  • ☐ Ensure your primary phone number is current and consistently listed across all platforms.
  • ☐ Double-check your website URL is correct and leads directly to your auto repair site.
  • ☐ Accurately update operating hours, including any holiday exceptions or service bay closure times.

Categories

  • ☐ Set "Auto repair shop" as your primary business category for maximum relevance.
  • ☐ Add "Oil change service" to capture specific maintenance searches.
  • ☐ Include "Brake shop" if you specialize in brake repair and replacement.
  • ☐ Select "Tire shop" if you offer tire sales, rotation, and balancing.
  • ☐ Add another relevant service category like "Auto transmission shop" or "Car inspection station."

Photos

  • ☐ Upload a high-resolution exterior photo showcasing your inviting auto repair bay entrance.
  • ☐ Feature a clean, well-lit photo of your customer waiting area or service desk.
  • ☐ Include a friendly team photo of your certified mechanics in uniform.
  • ☐ Post a photo demonstrating a common service, like a car on a lift during a tire rotation.
  • ☐ Add a specific image highlighting a service, such as new brake pads or diagnostic equipment.

Services

  • ☐ List "Oil Change Service" with a clear description and pricing if applicable.
  • ☐ Detail "Brake Repair & Replacement" including types of brakes serviced.
  • ☐ Add "Tire Sales, Mounting & Balancing" specifying tire brands offered.
  • ☐ Include "Engine Diagnostics & Repair" outlining common issues addressed.
  • ☐ List "AC Service & Repair" to attract customers needing climate control fixes.

Reviews

  • ☐ Respond promptly and professionally to every new customer review, positive or negative.
  • ☐ Actively encourage satisfied customers, perhaps after a successful brake repair, to leave a review.
  • ☐ Address any negative reviews constructively, offering to resolve issues offline.
  • ☐ Utilize the "Request a review" link from your GBP dashboard for easy sharing.
  • ☐ Analyze recurring themes in reviews to identify areas for service improvement or marketing.

Posts

  • ☐ Create a "What's New" post announcing a special offer, like 15% off your next oil change.
  • ☐ Publish an "Offer" post promoting a specific service package, such as a seasonal tire check.
  • ☐ Share an "Event" post if you're hosting a free car care clinic or safety inspection day.
  • ☐ Use a "Product" post to highlight a new line of tires or synthetic motor oil.
  • ☐ Post timely updates about holiday hours, COVID-19 safety protocols, or staff achievements.

Why these matter specifically for auto repair shops in Checklist

After you've diligently checked off all 30 items on this list, your work isn't entirely done. The next crucial step is to measure the impact of your efforts. Wait approximately 14 days for Google's algorithm to fully process and reflect your updates, then dive into your Google Business Profile Insights. Analyze metrics like search queries, map views, website clicks, and phone calls. This data will reveal what's working and where further refinement might be needed. Remember, GBP optimization is an ongoing process, so commit to repeating this checklist quarterly to maintain peak performance and continuously attract new auto repair clients.
