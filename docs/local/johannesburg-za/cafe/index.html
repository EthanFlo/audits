The three fixes on 80% of audited cafe profiles

  • Your Cafe's Story is Incomplete (Under 400 Characters)

    Many Johannesburg cafes miss a golden opportunity by using a bare-bones business description. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet most profiles barely scratch 400. This means you're not showcasing your ethically sourced beans, signature Rooibos lattes, artisan pastries, or that inviting patio space that makes your cafe unique. A detailed description tells potential customers exactly what experience awaits them, setting you apart from the cafe next door.

  • Overlooking Crucial Secondary Categories

    Simply listing "Cafe" as your primary category isn't enough in a city like Joburg, where diverse dining experiences are sought. Failing to add relevant secondary categories means you're invisible to customers searching for specific offerings. Common omissions include "Coffee Shop," "Brunch Restaurant," or even "Bakery" if you bake on-site. Optimizing these categories ensures your cafe appears in a wider range of targeted local searches, driving more specific and high-intent traffic.

  • A Visual Feast is Missing: Fewer Than 30 Photos

    Cafes are inherently visual businesses; customers want to see the vibe before they visit. Many profiles neglect to upload a comprehensive visual story. We often find fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. This means missing enticing shots of your latte art, signature breakfast spreads, the cozy interior decor, or your inviting shop front. A robust photo gallery showcasing your ambiance, food, and drinks is critical for attracting Johannesburg locals and tourists alike.
