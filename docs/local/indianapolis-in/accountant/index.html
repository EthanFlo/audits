The three fixes on 80% of audited accountant profiles

  • Your Google Business Profile Description is Too Short

    Most Indianapolis accountants only scratch the surface with their business description, using less than 400 characters. Google allows 750 characters, a crucial space to detail your firm's specialties like tax planning for small businesses, audit support, or specialized industry accounting. This missed opportunity means potential clients don't fully understand your firm's unique value and comprehensive services before moving on to a competitor.

  • Missing Key Secondary Categories

    Many accounting firms in Indianapolis only select "Accountant" as their primary category and overlook vital secondary categories that significantly broaden their reach. Essential secondary categories for accountants include "Tax Preparation," "Bookkeeping Service," and "Small Business CPA." Without these specific service categories, your firm remains invisible to potential clients searching specifically for those particular services, missing out on targeted traffic.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Most Indianapolis accountants have a sparse photo gallery on their Google Business Profile, often falling far below the recommended 30 owner-uploaded images. This lack of visual content makes your profile feel impersonal and less trustworthy. Clients want to see professional headshots of your team, welcoming interior photos of your office space, pictures of your team interacting, or even community involvement photos. Visuals build crucial trust and demonstrate your firm's professionalism.
