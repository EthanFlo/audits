The three fixes on 80% of audited photographer profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many Durban photographers' profiles use a scant 100-200 characters for their business description. Google allows up to 750 characters, a critical space to elaborate on your unique photographic style, the types of services you offer (e.g., weddings, family portraits, corporate events), and what makes your studio stand out. This often-missed opportunity limits your ability to include relevant keywords that help local clients find you.

  • You're Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Simply listing "Photographer" as your primary category isn't enough in a city like Durban. Your profile should leverage secondary categories to capture specific client searches. Common omissions include "Wedding Photographer," "Portrait Studio," and "Real Estate Photography Service." Adding these accurately tells Google and potential clients exactly what specific services you excel at, broadening your visibility for niche searches.

  • Your Photo Gallery is Underperforming

    Most photographer profiles I review have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. A vibrant, comprehensive gallery is vital. Clients often miss seeing diverse portfolio shots, behind-the-scenes glimpses of your process, photos of your studio space if applicable, or even images depicting your team. An underpopulated gallery fails to showcase your range, build trust, and truly capture the visual interest of a potential client.
