The three fixes on 80% of audited hair salon profiles

  • Your Business Description is Muting Your Salon's Voice

    Many Columbus hair salons aren't fully utilizing the 750 characters Google allows for their business description, often settling for a short, generic sentence. This valuable space is where you can articulate your unique style, highlight specialty services like balayage or precision cuts, and convey your salon's inviting atmosphere. A short description misses crucial keywords and fails to tell potential clients what makes your salon the perfect choice over competitors.

  • Ignoring Crucial Secondary Categories

    While "Hair Salon" is obvious, neglecting secondary categories like "Color Specialist," "Men's Barber," or "Bridal Hair Salon" severely limits your visibility. Google uses these to match highly specific searches. Without them, clients specifically looking for a master colorist or a place for their wedding day hair in Columbus might never see your profile, even if you offer those exact services with exceptional skill.

  • An Empty Gallery Leaves Clients Guessing

    Your Google Business Profile should be a visual portfolio showcasing your artistry. Often, hair salons have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, missing opportunities to display stunning before-and-after transformations, the clean and welcoming salon interior, your talented team in action, or close-ups of specific styles. High-quality, diverse images are vital for attracting new clients who want to see your work and feel confident about booking with you.
