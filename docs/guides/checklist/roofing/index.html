30-point Google Business Profile checklist for roofing contractors

General advice for optimizing your Google Business Profile often feels vague and overwhelming. "Make sure your profile is complete," "add relevant photos," or "get more reviews" are common refrains that, while true, lack the actionable specificity a busy roofing contractor needs. This leads to procrastination or incomplete efforts, missing tangible opportunities to attract more local clients.

Instead of generic guidance, imagine having an exact, step-by-step sequence you can work through on a slow afternoon, ticking off concrete actions that demonstrably improve your visibility. This 30-point checklist provides just that: a practical, sharp set of tasks designed specifically for roofing businesses to maximize their Google Business Profile impact, from detailed service listings to review management and beyond.

The three fixes on 80% of audited roofing contractor profiles

Business Info

  • ☐ Confirm your exact business name matches official registration documents for consistency and trust.
  • ☐ Verify your primary business address is accurate and clearly displays service area if you’re mobile.
  • ☐ Ensure your main business phone number is correct and active, ready for immediate client calls.
  • ☐ Update your website URL to the most current and mobile-friendly version of your company site.
  • ☐ Double-check your business hours, including special holiday hours, reflecting your operational availability for Roof Repair.

Categories

  • ☐ Set "Roofing Contractor" as your primary business category to rank for core services.
  • ☐ Add "Roofing Service" as a secondary category to broaden your search visibility.
  • ☐ Include "Roof Repair" as an additional category to capture specific repair-focused queries.
  • ☐ Consider "Emergency service" if you offer 24/7 support for critical roofing issues.
  • ☐ Evaluate if "Gutter cleaning service" or "Siding contractor" applies to diversify your service categories.

Photos

  • ☐ Upload 10+ high-resolution photos of recent, completed Roof Repair projects showcasing your quality.
  • ☐ Include several photos of your team actively working on a roof, demonstrating professionalism and safety.
  • ☐ Add exterior and interior photos of your business premises if clients visit your office.
  • ☐ Post at least one clear photo of your company vehicle with branding, enhancing local recognition.
  • ☐ Upload videos (10-30 seconds) of successful Roof Inspection or installation processes.

Services

  • ☐ List "Emergency Roofing" as a distinct service with a detailed description of your rapid response.
  • ☐ Create a separate service entry for "Roof Repair" outlining common issues you address (e.g., leaks, storm damage).
  • ☐ Detail "Roof Inspection" services, mentioning types like drone inspections or insurance claim assessments.
  • ☐ Add specific roofing materials you specialize in, such as "Asphalt Shingle Installation" or "Metal Roofing."
  • ☐ Include "Commercial Roofing" if you serve businesses, detailing your specific commercial capabilities.

Reviews

  • ☐ Respond to all new customer reviews within 24-48 hours, showing appreciation or addressing concerns.
  • ☐ Encourage satisfied clients to leave a Google review immediately after a successful Roof Repair project.
  • ☐ Create a short, direct link to your Google review form and share it with clients via text or email.
  • ☐ Address negative reviews professionally and constructively, offering solutions or inviting offline discussion.
  • ☐ Periodically ask long-term clients for updated reviews, especially after recent service or Roof Inspection.

Posts

  • ☐ Publish a weekly "What's New" post featuring recent Roof Repair jobs or company updates.
  • ☐ Create "Offer" posts for seasonal discounts on Roof Inspection or specific repair services.
  • ☐ Use "Event" posts to announce community involvement or upcoming promotions.
  • ☐ Write "Product" posts detailing specific roofing materials or advanced installation techniques you use.
  • ☐ Share "COVID-19 updates" (if applicable) regarding safety protocols for your Emergency Roofing services.

Why these matter specifically for roofing contractors in Checklist

After diligently working through all 30 points on this checklist, the work isn't truly over—it's just beginning. Give Google's algorithms about 14 days to process and index the significant updates you've made to your profile. During this period, resist the urge to make further major changes. Following the waiting period, delve into your Google Business Profile Insights to measure the impact of your efforts: look for increases in views, search queries, website clicks, and phone calls. This data will confirm the effectiveness of your optimized profile, guiding future adjustments. Repeat this entire checklist process every quarter to maintain peak performance and continually capture new roofing leads.
