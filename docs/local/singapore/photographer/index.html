The three fixes on 80% of audited photographer profiles

  • 1. Underutilised Business Description

    Many Singaporean photographers leave their Google Business Profile description brief, often under 400 characters, when Google allows up to 750. This means you're missing a golden opportunity to weave in crucial keywords like 'pre-wedding photography Singapore,' 'family photoshoot Singapore,' or 'corporate headshots.' A detailed description helps potential clients understand your specialities, unique style, and why they should choose you over the competition, significantly boosting your profile's search relevance.

  • 2. Neglecting Secondary Categories

    Beyond just 'Photographer,' many profiles overlook adding specific secondary categories that greatly improve search matching. For instance, if you specialise, including 'Wedding Photographer,' 'Portrait Studio,' or 'Real Estate Photographer' can significantly broaden your reach to clients searching for those precise services. In Singapore's varied market, defining your niches through these categories helps Google present your business to the right audience, reducing missed opportunities for specialised bookings.

  • 3. Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    For a visual business like photography, having fewer than 30 high-quality, owner-uploaded images is a major oversight. This isn't just about showing your best portfolio work; it's also about displaying 'behind the scenes' shots, your studio space, team photos, or even images of you interacting with clients. In Singapore, where visual appeal drives decisions, these diverse images build trust and give potential clients a comprehensive view of your brand and what to expect from your professional services.
