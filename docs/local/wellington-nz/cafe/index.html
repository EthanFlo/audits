The three fixes on 80% of audited cafe profiles

*

Your Business Description is Selling Your Cafe Short

Many cafe owners overlook the Google Business Profile description, leaving it brief when Google allows up to 750 characters. This is a prime spot to tell your cafe's unique story – from your artisan coffee blends and locally sourced ingredients to your vibrant atmosphere or award-winning brunch menu – captivating potential customers before they even visit. Don't let a generic, short description be a missed first impression.

*

Missing Out on Niche Searches with Limited Categories

We frequently find cafes using only their primary Google Business Profile category, missing crucial secondary categories that draw in specific customers. By adding relevant categories like "Coffee Shop," "Brunch Restaurant," or "Bakery," you tap into highly targeted searches beyond just "Cafe." This ensures your establishment appears for diverse customer needs, from a quick coffee run to a leisurely weekend brunch.

*

Customers Aren't Seeing Your Cafe's Best Side

Customers are visual, and too often, cafe profiles lack the essential photos that showcase what makes your spot unique. We often find missing shots of exquisite latte art, your bustling lunch service, inviting outdoor seating, signature brunch dishes, or even your smiling baristas. These aren't just pictures; they're vital selling points that draw people in and help them choose your cafe over others in Wellington.

Why these matter specifically for cafes in Wellington, NZ

For cafes in Wellington, fixing these Google Business Profile issues isn't just about good practice; it's essential for survival and growth. Our city is renowned as a "coffee capital," boasting an incredibly dense and competitive cafe scene, especially within the CBD, Cuba Street, and waterfront areas. Without a fully optimised profile, even the best coffee in town can easily get lost in the digital noise.

Wellingtonians and visitors alike often make quick decisions based on location and visual appeal, especially during morning commutes or lunch breaks. People are searching for "coffee near me" or "brunch Wellington CBD" on their phones while on the go. A profile that lacks key information, compelling photos, or the right categories simply won't grab their attention amidst a sea of options, directly impacting your walk-in traffic.

Moreover, customer search behaviour here is incredibly specific. Tourists seek unique, local experiences, while students might look for cafes with good Wi-Fi and study-friendly vibes. Locals are constantly on the hunt for their next favourite brunch spot or the perfect flat white. A robust, information-rich Google Business Profile caters to all these nuanced searches, ensuring you connect with the exact customer you want, right when they're looking.
