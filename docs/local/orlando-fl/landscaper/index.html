The three fixes on 80% of audited landscaper profiles

  • Underutilized Business Description

    Many Orlando landscapers miss a vital opportunity by keeping their Google Business Profile description too short. While Google allows up to 750 characters, I frequently find profiles with generic descriptions under 400. This space is crucial for detailing specific services like drought-tolerant landscaping, custom garden design, or irrigation repair relevant to the Florida climate. A robust description clearly communicates your unique value proposition, attracting customers searching for professional grounds care in the region.

  • Missing Secondary Categories

    Beyond the primary "Landscaper" category, many profiles neglect essential secondary categories. This oversight severely limits how many different types of local searches your business can rank for. For Orlando landscapers, I often discover missed opportunities to list services like "Lawn Care Service," "Hardscape Contractor," or "Tree Service." Utilizing these specific categories signals to Google and potential clients the full breadth of your expertise, making your profile discoverable for a wider array of specialized landscaping needs.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    A significant number of landscaper profiles in Orlando fall short on visual content, often having fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. Customers want to see your work quality, your team in action, and the stunning transformations you deliver. Frequently missing are before-and-after project shots, photos of your crew at work on different sites, and distinct examples of your tropical or native plant designs. High-quality, diverse images build trust and convey professionalism, driving more engagement.

Orlando's sprawling suburban areas and frequent traffic mean customers often prioritize landscapers close to their home or business. An optimized GBP, with accurate service areas and rich detail, helps Google precisely match you with nearby clients. If your profile isn't fully optimized, you risk being overlooked by those specifically searching for "landscaper near me" within a manageable commute radius.

Customer search behavior for landscaping in Orlando is highly visual and service-specific. People are looking for examples of yard transformations, specialized tree care, or hardscaping that stands up to Florida weather. Missing categories or a lack of project photos means your profile fails to address these specific visual and service needs, causing potential customers to quickly move on to a profile that clearly showcases the solutions they're seeking.

Given Orlando's distinct climate, customers also seek landscapers with specific expertise in native plants, irrigation systems for sandy soils, or hurricane-resistant tree trimming. A detailed, keyword-rich GBP description, combined with service-specific photos, is crucial for signaling your regional authority. Generic profiles won't cut it when clients need to trust you with their valuable outdoor spaces in a unique environment.
