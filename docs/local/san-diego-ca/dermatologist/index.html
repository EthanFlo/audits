The three fixes on 80% of audited dermatologist profiles

*

Your Google Business Profile Description is Too Short

Many San Diego dermatologists leave vital information out of their GBP description, often only using a fraction of the 750 characters available. This is a huge missed opportunity to detail specialized services like advanced skin cancer screenings, cosmetic injectables, pediatric dermatology, or even specific laser treatments. A truncated description fails to convey your practice’s full scope, leaving potential patients to guess what you offer instead of clicking to book.

*

Missing Crucial Secondary Service Categories

Most dermatologists correctly list "Dermatologist" as their primary category, but many neglect to add essential secondary categories. For San Diego practices, this means missing out on targeted searches. I frequently find profiles without categories like "Cosmetic Dermatology", "Skin Cancer Treatment Center", or "Pediatric Dermatologist". Adding these precise categories significantly expands your visibility for specific, high-intent patient searches.

*

Scarcity of Engaging Photos on Your Profile

A picture is worth a thousand words, especially for medical practices. I often see dermatologist profiles with fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. This means missing images of your welcoming waiting room, modern treatment rooms, professional staff, or even discreet, consent-based "before & after" results for cosmetic procedures. In a visual city like San Diego, a lack of compelling images can make your practice seem less inviting or less active.
