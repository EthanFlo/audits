The three fixes on 80% of audited accountant profiles

  1. Craft a Keyword-Rich Description: Write a concise, compelling description (up to 750 characters) that includes your firm's specialties and target clientele. Mention services like "small business tax preparation," "personal financial planning," and your service area to inform prospective clients and Google's algorithm effectively.
  2. Select the Precise Primary Category: Set your primary category to "Accountant" or "Certified public accountant." This is the single most crucial ranking factor and directly tells Google what your business fundamentally offers, ensuring you appear for core accounting-related searches.
  3. Add Relevant Secondary Categories: Augment your primary category with specific secondary categories that reflect your services. Choose categories like "Tax preparation service," "Bookkeeper," and "Financial consultant" to broaden your reach to specific client needs without diluting your main offering.
  4. Detail Your Services List: Populate the services section with clear, individual listings for every service you offer. For each service (e.g., "Small Business Tax Filing," "Payroll Services," "Audit Support"), add a brief description explaining its benefits and what clients can expect.
  5. Upload High-Quality Photos: Include professional photos of your office interior, exterior, your team, and even client interactions (with permission). Fresh, compelling images make your profile more engaging, build trust, and encourage potential clients to choose your firm.
  6. Implement a Consistent Google Posts Cadence: Publish Google Posts weekly or bi-weekly. Share tax tips, upcoming deadlines, firm news, or success stories. Consistent, valuable posts keep your profile active and demonstrate your expertise to both Google and potential clients.
  7. Cultivate Consistent Review Velocity: Actively encourage satisfied clients to leave reviews, aiming for a steady stream rather than sporadic bursts. Respond professionally to all reviews, positive and negative, to show you value client feedback and are engaged with your online reputation.
  8. Seed and Manage Your Q&A Section: Proactively add common questions with their answers to the Q&A section, such as "What types of businesses do you specialize in?" or "Do you offer virtual consultations?" This addresses client queries immediately and optimizes for long-tail keywords.

Why these matter specifically for accountants in How To

  • **Mistake:** Using a PO Box or virtual office address for your Google Business Profile. **Fix:** Google requires a physical address where clients can actually visit your accountant's office.
  • **Mistake:** Neglecting to fill out all available fields, especially the business hours and contact information. **Fix:** Ensure every section of your Google Business Profile is complete and accurate to maximize visibility and client trust.
  • **Mistake:** Stuffing your business name with extra keywords like "Best Tax Prep Accountant CPA Services." **Fix:** Use only your actual legal business name to avoid penalties and maintain trust with Google.
  • **Mistake:** Ignoring negative reviews or only responding to positive ones. **Fix:** Respond professionally and promptly to all reviews, both positive and negative, to demonstrate excellent customer service and transparency.
