The three fixes on 80% of audited gym profiles

  • Underutilised Business Description

    Many Gold Coast gyms overlook the full potential of their Google Business Profile description. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet I frequently see profiles with less than 400. This is a missed opportunity to detail your unique fitness philosophy, highlight popular classes like HIIT or strength training, and mention your specific equipment or amenities. A concise, keyword-rich description helps potential members understand your offerings and signals relevance to Google for local searches.

  • Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Your primary category might be "Gym," but Gold Coast residents search for specific fitness needs. Frequently, profiles miss out on vital secondary categories like "CrossFit Gym," "Yoga Studio," or "Personal Trainer." Adding these specific classifications significantly broadens your reach, ensuring you appear in more targeted searches. If someone types "yoga studio near me," and you offer yoga but only list "Gym," you're effectively invisible to that potential member.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    A picture truly tells a thousand words, especially for gyms. I often find Gold Coast gym profiles with minimal owner-uploaded photos, usually fewer than 30. This means missing out on showcasing your welcoming reception, state-of-the-art equipment, energetic group classes in action, clean changing rooms, and the friendly faces of your trainers. High-quality, diverse imagery builds trust and helps prospective members visualise themselves training at your facility, boosting engagement.
