The three fixes on 80% of audited real estate agent profiles

  • Insufficient Business Description Length

    Many Dallas real estate agents miss a critical opportunity by leaving their Google Business Profile description too short. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet profiles often use under 400. This space is vital for detailing your specialties – like luxury homes in Preston Hollow, first-time buyer assistance in Bishop Arts, or investment properties – and your unique value proposition, directly impacting how well Google understands and ranks your services locally.

  • Overlooking Crucial Secondary Categories

    A common oversight for Dallas real estate agents is the omission of relevant secondary categories. While 'Real Estate Agent' is standard, neglecting additions like 'Buyer's Agent,' 'Seller's Agent,' or 'Property Manager' limits your visibility. These signal to Google exactly what services you offer, allowing you to appear in more diverse and specific local searches, connecting you with clients actively seeking those precise specializations across Dallas neighborhoods.

  • Sparse Photo Galleries

    Many Dallas real estate agents have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos on their profiles, missing a huge chance to showcase their expertise and personality. I often find a lack of professional headshots, team photos, images of past successful closings (with consent!), neighborhood highlights, or photos of the agent in action. A robust visual presence builds trust and helps differentiate you in a crowded market, encouraging potential clients to click and engage.
