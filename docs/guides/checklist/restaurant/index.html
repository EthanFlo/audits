30-point Google Business Profile checklist for restaurants

Forget vague "optimize your profile" advice. What restaurants truly need is a clear, actionable roadmap to Google Business Profile (GBP) success. This 30-point checklist provides exactly that: a specific sequence of tasks you can tackle on a slow Tuesday afternoon, turning downtime into tangible online visibility gains.

This isn't about generic best practices; it's about precise actions designed to attract more diners to your tables. Each item is a bite-sized, concrete step, ensuring that by the time you check off the last box, your restaurant's GBP will be a powerful, dynamic marketing tool, outperforming competitors and drawing in local customers searching for their next meal.

The three fixes on 80% of audited restaurant profiles

Business Info

  • ☐ Verify your restaurant's exact business name, address, and phone number (NAP) are consistent with your website and all online directories.
  • ☐ Accurately set your primary and any secondary business hours, including special holiday hours like Thanksgiving or Christmas.
  • ☐ Write a compelling, keyword-rich "from the business" description that highlights your unique selling points, e.g., "authentic Italian dishes since 1995."
  • ☐ Ensure your website link is correct and leads directly to your main restaurant site, not a general booking platform.
  • ☐ Add all relevant attributes like "Outdoor Seating," "Good for Groups," "Wifi," or "Kid-friendly" to provide helpful details to customers.

Categories

  • ☐ Select the most specific primary category for your restaurant, e.g., "Italian Restaurant," "Sushi Restaurant," or "Steakhouse."
  • ☐ Add up to nine additional relevant categories that accurately describe all aspects of your business, such as "Pizza Restaurant" or "Seafood Restaurant."
  • ☐ Review competitor profiles to identify any effective categories they are using that you might have overlooked for your establishment.
  • ☐ Ensure your chosen categories align with the types of food and dining experiences you primarily offer to avoid misleading customers.
  • ☐ Periodically re-evaluate your categories to reflect any menu changes or new services, like becoming a "Delivery Restaurant."

Photos

  • ☐ Upload at least 10 high-resolution photos of your best-selling dishes, ensuring they are well-lit and professionally presented.
  • ☐ Include captivating interior photos showcasing your restaurant's ambiance, decor, and unique dining spaces.
  • ☐ Add exterior photos of your restaurant from different angles, making it easy for customers to identify your location.
  • ☐ Upload a team photo of your friendly staff, showing the faces behind the great service at your Steakhouse or Sushi bar.
  • ☐ Post a high-quality video walkthrough (up to 30 seconds) of your restaurant, highlighting key features and the overall dining experience.

Services

  • ☐ Clearly list all dining options offered, such as "Dine-in," "Takeout," "Delivery," "Curbside pickup," and "Drive-through."
  • ☐ Specify any unique services like "Reservations," "Catering," "Private Dining Room," or "Online Ordering" with direct links where applicable.
  • ☐ Add specific menu items or popular dishes as services, e.g., "Hand-rolled Sushi platters" or "Aged Ribeye Steaks."
  • ☐ Update your menu section with your latest offerings, prices, and mouth-watering descriptions for all appetizers, entrees, and desserts.
  • ☐ Link directly to your online reservation system (e.g., OpenTable) and your online ordering platform for seamless customer experience.

Reviews

  • ☐ Respond to 100% of all customer reviews, both positive and negative, within 24-48 hours, showing genuine appreciation or empathy.
  • ☐ Thank positive reviewers specifically for their kind words, mentioning unique aspects like the "authentic Italian pasta."
  • ☐ Address negative reviews professionally, apologizing where necessary and offering a resolution or invitation to discuss offline.
  • ☐ Encourage new reviews by placing a "review us on Google" sign near your cash register or including it on receipts.
  • ☐ Use relevant keywords in your review responses, subtly reinforcing your restaurant's identity, e.g., "glad you enjoyed our Steakhouse burgers!"

Posts

  • ☐ Create a "What's New" post highlighting a daily special, new menu item, or seasonal dish (e.g., "Fresh Tuna Sashimi this week!").
  • ☐ Publish an "Event" post for upcoming live music, tasting menus, or holiday celebrations happening at your restaurant.
  • ☐ Share an "Offer" post promoting discounts, happy hour specials, or a loyalty program for your regular diners.
  • ☐ Use a "Call to Action" button on your posts, directing customers to "Order Online," "Reserve a Table," or "Learn More" about your menu.
  • ☐ Post at least once a week to keep your profile active and visible, showcasing new content and engaging with potential customers.

Why these matter specifically for restaurants in Checklist

After meticulously checking off all 30 points, the work isn't over – it's just beginning. Give Google's algorithms about 14 days to fully process and reflect these updates across search results and Maps. During this period, resist the urge to make further significant changes. Once the two weeks are up, dive into your Google Business Profile Insights. Measure the direct impact: how many more views, website clicks, calls, and direction requests your restaurant is receiving. This data will reveal what's working best and inform your next round of optimizations, turning your GBP into a continuously improving magnet for hungry patrons.
