Issue #1 — The missing business description (72% of audits)

Google's algorithm treats the business description as a primary ranking signal. A well-written 750-character description with the primary service keyword is worth roughly 3-5 local-pack rank positions for the target query.

Example from the data: A North Vancouver plumbing company with no description was ranking #8 for "plumber north vancouver" (3,100 monthly searches). A comparable competitor with a keyword-rich description was ranking #2.

Revenue impact: At 2.1% CTR difference × 3,100 searches × 28% conversion × $185 avg job = roughly $3,400/month in missed work. For one issue. That takes 20 minutes to fix.

Issue #2 — Photo count vs. top competitor (varies by vertical)

Top-ranking local-pack businesses in our sample had an average of 64 photos. The businesses we audited averaged 8.

Photos matter for two reasons: Google's algorithm weighs profile completeness, and visitors make judgments fast. The ratio of your photo count to the top competitor's photo count is a surprisingly good proxy for how Google perceives your profile quality.
