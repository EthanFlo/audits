The three fixes on 80% of audited gym profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short and Generic

    Google allows up to 750 characters, but many San Jose gyms leave their description under 400, often using vague terms. This is a crucial missed opportunity to highlight your unique offerings – from state-of-the-art cardio zones and specialized strength training equipment to unique class formats like HIIT, spinning, or martial arts. A compelling description convinces potential members why your gym is the perfect fit for their fitness goals, setting you apart from local competitors.

  • Neglecting Crucial Secondary Categories

    Many gyms only select "Gym" as their primary category, ignoring valuable secondary options. Google allows up to nine additional categories to precisely define your services. For a San Jose gym, these could include "CrossFit Gym," "Yoga Studio," or "Personal Trainer." Without these, you’re invisible to users specifically searching for those niche services, letting competitors capture that highly targeted traffic even if you offer superior programs.

  • An Uninspiring Photo Gallery with Fewer Than 30 Images

    Visuals are paramount for gyms, yet many San Jose profiles have under 30 owner-uploaded photos. This leaves potential members guessing about your facility. You're likely missing high-quality images of your modern equipment, spacious workout areas, engaging group classes in action, clean locker rooms, and your friendly, professional trainers. A rich, diverse photo gallery builds trust and excitement, encouraging more physical visits and sign-ups.
