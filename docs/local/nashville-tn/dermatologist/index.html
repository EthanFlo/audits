The three fixes on 80% of audited dermatologist profiles

  • 1. Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many Nashville dermatologists fail to use the full 750 characters Google allows for their business description. I often find profiles with generic 200-character summaries. This is a missed opportunity to highlight your expertise in specific areas like advanced melanoma screenings, pediatric eczema treatment, or specialized cosmetic procedures, making it harder for potential patients to understand your unique value proposition.

  • 2. Missing Critical Secondary Categories

    Most practices correctly list "Dermatologist" as their primary category, but stop there. An audit frequently reveals the absence of crucial secondary categories. For Nashville, TN, adding categories like "Cosmetic Dermatologist," "Skin Cancer Treatment Center," and "Pediatric Dermatologist" is vital. This oversight limits your visibility for specific, high-intent patient searches and prevents Google from accurately understanding your full range of services.

  • 3. Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Profiles often lack the visual depth needed to stand out. I frequently see fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, missing opportunities to showcase your Nashville clinic. Patients want to see your welcoming waiting area, sterile treatment rooms, your friendly staff, and perhaps even generalized, professional 'before and after' imagery (where appropriate and compliant), building trust and familiarity before their first visit.
