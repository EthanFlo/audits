The three fixes on 80% of audited real estate agent profiles

  • Underutilized Business Description

    Many Oklahoma City real estate agents overlook the power of their Google Business Profile description, often leaving it brief or generic. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet most profiles I audit use less than 400. This is a missed opportunity to showcase your specialties, your deep knowledge of OKC neighborhoods like Nichols Hills or the Paseo Arts District, and your unique approach to buying or selling homes, directly appealing to local searchers.

  • Missing Critical Secondary Categories

    Relying solely on "Real Estate Agent" as your primary category severely limits your discoverability. In OKC’s competitive market, potential clients search specifically for agents who meet their needs. Audits often reveal missing secondary categories like "Buyer's Agent," "Seller's Agent," and "Property Manager." Adding these precise categories ensures you appear in highly targeted local searches, connecting you with clients actively seeking your specialized services in Oklahoma City.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    A visually rich Google Business Profile builds trust and engagement, yet many OKC real estate agents have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. Profiles typically lack photos of recent successful closings, your professional headshot, team photos, client testimonials with consent, local landmarks you serve, or "for sale" signs outside properties. These visual cues are crucial for humanizing your brand and attracting clients looking for a trustworthy local expert in Oklahoma City.
