30-point Google Business Profile checklist for hvac contractors

Here's your 30-point Google Business Profile checklist for HVAC contractors:

There’s a common pitfall in digital marketing advice: it’s often too vague. Telling an HVAC contractor to simply "optimize your Google Business Profile" is like telling them to "fix an AC unit" without providing tools or a diagnostic process. What’s needed isn't general guidance, but a specific, actionable sequence of steps that removes guesswork and ensures maximum impact. This checklist provides exactly that: concrete tasks to systematically enhance your online presence.

This isn't just a list of good ideas; it's a strategic blueprint designed to be worked through methodically. Imagine a slow afternoon where your trucks are off duty – this is your high-impact project. By ticking off each item, you’re not just improving your profile; you’re building a stronger foundation for local visibility, trust, and lead generation, positioning your HVAC business as the top choice for consumers in your service area.

The three fixes on 80% of audited hvac contractor profiles

Business Info

  • ☐ Verify business name, address, and phone (NAP) are consistent everywhere online.
  • ☐ Update your primary phone number to a trackable call-forwarding line.
  • ☐ Ensure your website URL links directly to your main HVAC services page.
  • ☐ Set accurate business hours, including any special holiday or seasonal adjustments.
  • ☐ Add a detailed business description highlighting unique selling points and service areas.

Categories

  • ☐ Set "HVAC Contractor" as your primary Google Business Profile category.
  • ☐ Add "Air Conditioning Contractor" as a secondary category if applicable.
  • ☐ Include "Furnace Repair Service" as another relevant category option.
  • ☐ Select "Duct Cleaning Service" if it's a core offering of your business.
  • ☐ Review all available categories for any other specific HVAC specializations you offer.

Photos

  • ☐ Upload at least 3 high-quality exterior photos of your service vehicles or office.
  • ☐ Add 5 clear interior photos showing your team working on HVAC systems.
  • ☐ Include 2 "before and after" photos of an AC repair or furnace installation project.
  • ☐ Upload a professional headshot of the owner or key service technicians.
  • ☐ Ensure all photos are geotagged if possible, showcasing your primary service area.

Services

  • ☐ List "AC Repair" with a clear description of your diagnostic and repair process.
  • ☐ Detail "Furnace Installation" specifying brands, models, and efficiency options.
  • ☐ Add "Duct Cleaning" with notes on improving air quality and system efficiency.
  • ☐ Include "Preventative Maintenance" plans, explaining benefits and inclusions.
  • ☐ Create specific service entries for any emergency HVAC services offered.

Reviews

  • ☐ Implement a system to consistently request new reviews from happy customers.
  • ☐ Respond professionally to every single review, whether positive or negative.
  • ☐ Thank customers by name in positive review responses for their detailed feedback.
  • ☐ Address negative reviews empathetically, offering private resolution steps immediately.
  • ☐ Encourage customers to mention specific services like AC Repair or Furnace Install in reviews.

Posts

  • ☐ Create a "What's New" post promoting a seasonal HVAC offer (e.g., AC tune-up special).
  • ☐ Publish an "Offer" post detailing a discount on furnace installation or duct cleaning.
  • ☐ Use an "Event" post for a local community event your HVAC business sponsors.
  • ☐ Share a "Product" post highlighting a new energy-efficient AC unit or smart thermostat.
  • ☐ Craft a "Tips & Tricks" post for seasonal HVAC maintenance homeowners can do.

Why these matter specifically for hvac contractors in Checklist

Once every item on this 30-point checklist has been meticulously addressed, your work isn't done—it's merely entering a new phase. Begin by closely monitoring your Google Business Profile Insights over the next 14 days, tracking metrics like views, calls, website clicks, and direction requests to gauge the immediate impact of your optimizations. Remember that Google's algorithm can take time to fully process and reflect changes, so patience is key. After this initial observation period, revisit your profile, analyze what’s working best, and repeat the process of refinement and addition, treating your GBP as a living, evolving asset that requires ongoing attention to maintain its peak performance.
