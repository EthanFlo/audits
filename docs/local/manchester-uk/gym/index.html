The three fixes on 80% of audited gym profiles

  • Underutilised Business Description

    Many gym profiles in Manchester don't take full advantage of the 750 characters Google allows for the business description, often leaving it under 400. This is a missed opportunity to showcase your unique selling points – perhaps you specialise in functional fitness, offer unique classes like aerial yoga, or have state-of-the-art equipment. A short, generic description fails to capture attention, communicate your value, or include vital keywords that tell Google what you're truly about, impacting your search relevance.

  • Missing Secondary Categories

    Your gym isn't just a "Gym." Google Business Profile allows for a primary category and multiple secondary categories, yet many Manchester gyms only use one. This severely limits your discoverability. For instance, if you offer specialised training but haven't added "Personal Trainer," "CrossFit Gym," or "Yoga Studio" as secondary categories, you'll be invisible to customers searching specifically for those services, even if you’re the perfect fit.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Profiles often have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, failing to visually engage potential members. This typically means missing crucial visuals like dynamic shots of classes in action (e.g., a spin class, weightlifting area), detailed photos of your equipment, clean changing rooms, reception area, or even your trainers engaging with members. High-quality, diverse images build trust and help prospects visualise themselves in your space, driving conversions.

Why these matter specifically for gyms in Manchester, UK

For gyms in a vibrant, competitive city like Manchester, these Google Business Profile oversights aren't just minor errors; they're significant barriers. The city is dense with fitness options, from boutique studios in Ancoats to large chain gyms around Piccadilly and student-focused facilities near the universities. When someone searches for "gym near me" or "CrossFit Northern Quarter," Google's algorithm prioritises the most complete, relevant, and engaging profiles. In such a high-density market, even small improvements can make the difference between appearing on page one or being relegated to page two of local search results.

Commute patterns are also critical in Manchester. Many potential gym members search based on their home, work, or daily travel routes. If your profile doesn't clearly articulate *what* you offer and *where* you're located through optimised categories and descriptions, you'll struggle to appear in hyper-local searches relevant to these specific journeys. Prospects in Salford Quays won't find your city centre boxing gym if your profile isn't meticulously detailed for relevant keywords and services.

Customer search behaviour for gyms is highly visual and amenity-driven. People want to see the vibe, the equipment, and the cleanliness before visiting. A lack of diverse, high-quality photos means Manchester prospects are less likely to click through to your website or make an enquiry, instead opting for a competitor with a visually rich profile that offers a clearer sense of what to expect. Without those crucial visuals, you're invisible to a key segment of local searchers.
