The three fixes on 80% of audited physical therapist profiles

  • Business Description is Underutilized (Under 400 Characters)

    Many San Diego physical therapy profiles fail to leverage the full 750 characters Google allows for the business description. Instead, I often find a brief, generic blurb that hardly explains your specialization beyond "physical therapy." This misses a critical opportunity to highlight your unique treatment philosophies, state-of-the-art equipment, or specific conditions you excel at treating, leaving potential patients to guess what makes your clinic stand out from the dozens of others in San Diego.

  • Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Beyond the primary "Physical Therapist" category, many San Diego clinics neglect to add specific secondary categories. This oversight severely limits their discoverability for niche searches. I frequently see profiles missing categories like "Sports Medicine Clinic," "Orthopedic Rehabilitation Center," or "Post-Surgical Rehabilitation." Without these, you are invisible to patients specifically searching for the specialized care you offer, like athletes recovering from an injury or individuals needing rehab after surgery.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Google Business Profiles for San Diego physical therapists often suffer from a severe lack of compelling, high-quality images. I commonly find profiles with less than 30 photos, mostly stock images or just a few basic exterior shots. Missing are essential visual elements like clear photos of your treatment rooms, specialized equipment, your friendly front desk and waiting area, staff headshots, or therapists actively engaging with patients (staged for privacy) to showcase your patient-centered environment.
