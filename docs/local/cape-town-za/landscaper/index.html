The three fixes on 80% of audited landscaper profiles

  • Your Google Business Profile Description is Too Short

    Many Cape Town landscapers use a generic, brief description that barely scratches the surface of their expertise. Google allows up to 750 characters, but I often find profiles using less than 400. This is a missed opportunity to highlight unique services like indigenous fynbos garden design, water-wise landscaping solutions, or specialized hardscaping for the local climate, differentiating you from competitors and attracting specific clients looking for your niche.

  • You're Missing Crucial Secondary Service Categories

    While 'Landscaper' is your primary category, many profiles neglect to add secondary categories. This significantly limits your visibility for specific searches. I frequently see landscapers missing key categories like 'Lawn Care', 'Hardscaping', and 'Tree Service'. These allow Google to better understand the full scope of your offerings, making your business discoverable for a wider range of services you actually provide across the Peninsula.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Visual Content

    Your Google Business Profile should be a visual portfolio, yet many landscapers have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. This often means missing crucial 'before-and-after' project shots, examples of local garden designs, team photos in action, or high-quality images of specific plant installations suitable for Cape Town's climate. Without these, potential clients can't fully visualize your craftsmanship or the quality of your completed projects.
