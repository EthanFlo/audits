The three fixes on 80% of audited physical therapist profiles

  • Underutilised Business Description

    Many physical therapy profiles in Melbourne fail to leverage the full 750-character potential of their GBP business description. Often, I find generic, short descriptions under 400 characters that miss opportunities to detail specific specialisations like sports injury recovery, women's health physiotherapy, or chronic pain management. This oversight means you're not communicating your unique value proposition to potential patients effectively, hindering your ability to attract those searching for specialised care.

  • Absence of Crucial Secondary Categories

    Google Business Profile allows for multiple categories beyond 'Physical Therapist.' Yet, many Melbourne practices only list the primary one. I consistently find missing vital secondary categories such as 'Sports Injury Clinic,' 'Orthopedic Physical Therapy,' or 'Rehabilitation Center.' Without these specific additions, your practice misses out on appearing in highly relevant, targeted searches from patients looking for specialised services, limiting your overall visibility and reach.

  • Insufficient Visual Content

    Visuals are crucial for building trust, especially in healthcare. Audits frequently reveal fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos on physical therapist profiles. Clinics often lack images showcasing their modern equipment, comfortable waiting areas, treatment rooms in use (with models/staff), or even external shots of the building for easy identification. This scarcity leaves potential patients with a sterile impression and makes it harder for them to visualise visiting your welcoming Melbourne practice.
