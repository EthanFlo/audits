The three fixes on 80% of audited real estate agent profiles

  • Your Google Business Profile Description is Selling You Short

    Many Orlando real estate agents miss a critical opportunity by keeping their business description under 400 characters. Google allows up to 750, meaning you're likely omitting valuable keywords like specific Orlando neighborhoods (e.g., Lake Nona, Winter Park), property types, or unique specializations. This limitation severely hinders your discoverability when potential clients search for precise criteria.

  • You're Not Using Key Secondary Categories to Attract Specific Clients

    Most agents only select 'Real Estate Agent' as their primary category, but Google Business Profile allows for several secondary categories. Failing to include specific ones like 'Buyer's Agent,' 'Seller's Agent,' or 'Property Manager' means you're not fully signaling your expertise to Google. This reduces your chances of appearing for specialized searches by Orlando clients with unique needs.

  • Your Photo Gallery is Bare, Missing Crucial Visual Trust-Builders

    Your Google Business Profile photo gallery is often a blank slate. Many real estate agents have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, missing vital opportunities to build trust. I frequently find profiles lacking professional headshots, engaging client testimonials (with permission), images showcasing local Orlando neighborhoods, or interior/exterior shots of recent successful listings. Visual proof builds confidence with potential clients.
