The three fixes on 80% of audited gym profiles

  • Your Google Business Profile Description is Too Short

    Many Jacksonville gyms only use a fraction of the available 750 characters in their GBP description, often leaving out crucial details. This prevents you from showcasing your unique offerings, like specialized fitness programs, state-of-the-art equipment, or the welcoming community atmosphere that sets your gym apart. A concise description misses opportunities to highlight what makes members choose you over the competition, failing to fully engage potential clients searching for their perfect fitness home.

  • You're Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Many gyms only list "Gym" as their primary category, which severely limits visibility. Google uses secondary categories to match users with specific services. For instance, if you offer high-intensity training, "CrossFit Gym" is crucial. For personalized instruction, "Personal Trainer" is vital. And if you have dedicated wellness spaces, "Yoga Studio" expands your reach. Without these, you're invisible to prospects searching for your specific offerings.

  • Your Profile Lacks Sufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    In the fitness world, visuals are everything. Many gym profiles in Jacksonville suffer from a scarcity of high-quality, owner-uploaded images. This means potential members can't visualize your space, the equipment, or the vibrant energy of your classes. We often find profiles missing shots of active group fitness sessions, a welcoming front desk, sparkling clean locker rooms, or even a clear exterior photo showing street presence. This visual void deters engagement and trust.
