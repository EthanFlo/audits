30-point Google Business Profile checklist for photographers

This 30-point Google Business Profile checklist for photographers isn't just another guide; it's a precise blueprint designed for immediate action. General advice often falls short because it lacks the actionable steps necessary to make a real impact. This list cuts through the noise, offering concrete, sequential tasks tailored specifically for photography businesses, ensuring every aspect of your online presence is meticulously optimized, not just vaguely improved.

Imagine a quiet afternoon with no shoots, no editing deadlines, just dedicated time for your business growth. This checklist provides the exact sequence of steps a wedding photographer, portrait studio, or real estate photography service should work through. It's your opportunity to methodically polish your Google Business Profile, turning it into a powerful client-attraction magnet that consistently captures the attention of prospective clients actively searching for your unique photographic services.

The three fixes on 80% of audited photographer profiles

Business Info

  • ☐ Verify your exact business name (e.g., "Smith Photography") matches legal documents and website branding precisely.
  • ☐ Ensure your primary phone number is current, directly rings to you, and includes the correct country code.
  • ☐ Double-check your physical address for your studio or service area, ensuring pin accuracy on the map for local searches.
  • ☐ Confirm your operating hours are fully updated, reflecting seasonal changes, holiday closures, or appointment-only availability.
  • ☐ Add or update your business description, highlighting specialties like wedding, portrait, or commercial photography in concise language.

Categories

  • ☐ Set "Photographer" or "Photography Studio" as your primary category to accurately represent your core service.
  • ☐ Add relevant secondary categories like "Wedding Photographer," "Portrait Studio," or "Commercial Photographer" as applicable.
  • ☐ Review all selected categories annually, removing outdated ones and adding new specialties as your business evolves.
  • ☐ Research competitor categories to ensure you're not missing any high-volume or niche-specific relevant options.
  • ☐ Avoid overly broad or irrelevant categories; stick strictly to services you actively provide and want to rank for.

Photos

  • ☐ Upload a high-resolution, professional profile photo, ideally a clear headshot or your business logo.
  • ☐ Ensure your cover photo showcases your best work (e.g., a stunning wedding shot or a compelling portrait).
  • ☐ Create specific photo albums for different services: "Weddings," "Portraits," "Real Estate Photography," etc., with 10+ images each.
  • ☐ Geotag newly uploaded photos if they were taken at specific client locations, aiding local discovery.
  • ☐ Consistently upload fresh, high-quality images from recent client projects weekly or bi-weekly to show current work.

Services

  • ☐ List all individual photography services with detailed descriptions (e.g., "Engagement Photography," "Maternity Sessions").
  • ☐ Include pricing details or "request a quote" options for each service to manage client expectations efficiently.
  • ☐ Add custom services like "Photo Booth Rental" or "Drone Photography" if offered, beyond standard categories.
  • ☐ Describe the value proposition of each service, focusing on client benefits and unique aspects of your photography.
  • ☐ Regularly update service descriptions and offerings to reflect new packages, equipment, or artistic directions.

Reviews

  • ☐ Set up a direct link for clients to leave reviews, sharing it via email signature, invoices, or social media.
  • ☐ Promptly respond to every review, positive or negative, within 24-48 hours with personalized, professional replies.
  • ☐ Encourage clients to include specific details about their experience (e.g., "amazing wedding photos," "patient portrait photographer").
  • ☐ Strategically ask for reviews from satisfied clients immediately after delivering their final photo galleries.
  • ☐ Address negative feedback constructively, offering solutions or clarifications publicly and privately to show commitment.

Posts

  • ☐ Create "What's New" posts announcing new portfolio additions, special offers, or recent client testimonials.
  • ☐ Publish "Event" posts for mini-sessions, workshop dates, or open house events at your photography studio.
  • ☐ Utilize "Offer" posts to promote seasonal discounts (e.g., "Holiday Portrait Package 20% Off") with clear expiry dates.
  • ☐ Include high-quality, relevant photos or short videos with every post to increase engagement and visual appeal.
  • ☐ Maintain a consistent posting schedule, aiming for at least one to two posts per week to keep your profile active.
