The three fixes on 80% of audited restaurant profiles

  • Business Description is Too Short

    Many profiles I see for Melbourne eateries have descriptions that are far too brief. They often just state the cuisine type and a single location. Google allows up to 750 characters, but I frequently find them under 400. This is a massive missed opportunity to showcase your unique menu items, your chef's philosophy, or the amazing dining experience you offer, truly setting you apart.

  • Missing Key Secondary Categories

    A common oversight for Melbourne restaurants is not utilising secondary categories. While you might have "Restaurant" as your primary, I often see profiles missing crucial specific classifications. For example, if you run a pizza place, you should also have "Pizza restaurant." Other valuable additions could be "Italian restaurant" or "Dessert restaurant," depending on your niche, to capture specific searches.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    In an audit, I often discover Melbourne restaurant profiles with fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, sometimes even less than ten. Customers eat with their eyes first! Many profiles are missing essential images like high-quality shots of your signature dishes, the inviting interior and exterior, happy customers enjoying their meals, your kitchen team in action, or even your daily specials board.

Why these matter specifically for restaurants in Melbourne, AU

  • Melbourne's culinary scene is globally renowned, but that also means intense competition. Every street, especially in foodie hubs like Fitzroy, Richmond, or the CBD, is saturated with incredible dining options. A perfectly optimised Google Business Profile isn't just a nice-to-have; it's a critical differentiator, ensuring your establishment stands out amidst hundreds of similar businesses fighting for attention.

  • Melbourne diners are often searching on the go, whether planning an evening out or looking for a quick lunch during their commute. They frequently use "near me" searches or specify suburbs. If your GBP lacks detail, miscategorises your offerings, or doesn't show up for hyper-local queries, you're effectively invisible to this dynamic and often spontaneous customer base, missing out on crucial walk-in and last-minute bookings.

  • For restaurants, visuals are paramount. Melbourne's food culture is highly visual, driven by social media and review platforms. Customers expect to see high-quality photos of the ambience and, crucially, the food before they even consider booking. A profile with few or poor images immediately signals a lack of engagement and can deter potential diners who are making quick decisions based on visual appeal and trust.
