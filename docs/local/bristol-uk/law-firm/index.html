The three fixes on 80% of audited law firm profiles

  • Your Google Business Profile Description is Too Short

    Many Bristol law firms leave significant portions of their Google Business Profile description blank. With a generous 750 characters available, under-utilising this space, often clocking in under 400, means you're missing a prime opportunity to detail your firm's specialisations, values, and unique selling points for potential clients researching legal services in Bristol.

  • Missing Key Secondary Service Categories

    Most law firms correctly use 'Law Firm' as their primary category. However, a common oversight is failing to add crucial secondary categories that specify their practice areas. For Bristol law firms, this often includes neglecting categories like 'Personal Injury Lawyer', 'Family Law Attorney', or 'Estate Planning Lawyer', which significantly limits their visibility for specific, high-intent searches.

  • A Dearth of Engaging, Owner-Uploaded Photos

    A frequent audit finding is fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, often just a logo and a single exterior shot. Potential clients in Bristol want to see your office environment, your team, meeting rooms, and local landmarks near your practice. Missing these visually compelling elements makes your profile less trustworthy and less engaging compared to competitors with a rich visual presence.
