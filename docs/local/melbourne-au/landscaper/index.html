The three fixes on 80% of audited landscaper profiles

  • Undersized Business Description

    Many Melbourne landscapers aren't using the full potential of their Google Business Profile description. Google allows up to 750 characters, but I frequently see profiles with less than 400. This is a missed opportunity to showcase your unique specialities – perhaps native garden design, complex hardscaping projects, or water-wise solutions – and include crucial keywords local customers are searching for, helping you stand out from the crowd.

  • Missing Key Secondary Categories

    While "Landscaper" is your primary category, many profiles fail to add specific secondary categories that capture niche services. This severely limits your visibility for detailed searches. For optimal exposure, landscapers should include relevant categories like "Lawn Care Service," "Hardscape Contractor," and "Tree Service." These additions tell Google exactly what services you offer, connecting you with more precise local customer queries.

  • Scarcity of Owner-Uploaded Photos

    A significant number of landscaper profiles in Melbourne have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. Customers want to see your actual work, your team, and your equipment. This often means missing before-and-after shots, detailed close-ups of garden features, team photos on-site, or images of your branded vehicles. More high-quality, relevant photos build trust and significantly boost engagement and local search rankings.

Why these matter specifically for landscapers in Melbourne, AU

In a densely populated city like Melbourne, local competition among landscapers is intense. Every suburb, from Brighton to Brunswick, has multiple businesses vying for attention. Google's algorithm prioritises relevancy and proximity, meaning an incomplete or under-optimised Google Business Profile puts you at a severe disadvantage, pushing potential clients to competitors who’ve invested in their local online presence.

Melbourne’s sprawling nature means commute patterns are a major factor for both you and your clients. Customers often search for "landscaper near me" or "landscaping [specific Melbourne suburb]" because they prefer a local business for convenience and familiarity. A well-optimised profile with precise service areas helps Google connect you directly with these geographically targeted searches, minimising wasted leads from areas you don't service efficiently.

Customer search behaviour for landscaping services here is highly visual and service-specific. Melburnians want to see examples of native gardens, modern hardscaping for compact blocks, or expertise in drought-resistant plants suited to our climate. A robust Google Business Profile, rich with high-quality photos and detailed service descriptions, directly addresses this visual demand and specific regional interests, building confidence before they even click to your website.
