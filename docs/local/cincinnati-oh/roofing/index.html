The three fixes on 80% of audited roofing contractor profiles

  • Your Business Description Sells You Short

    Many Cincinnati roofing contractors use a short, generic description, missing out on valuable real estate. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet most profiles barely hit 200. This is a missed opportunity to showcase your expertise in storm damage repair, commercial roofing, or specific neighborhoods you serve, making it harder for potential clients to understand your full value proposition at a glance.

  • Missing Key Service Categories

    Your primary category might be "Roofing Contractor," but are you utilizing all the relevant secondary categories? Many profiles fail to add crucial service-specific tags, which limits visibility for exact searches. Key categories like "Roof Repair," "Emergency Roofing Service," and "Roof Inspection Service" are often overlooked, preventing you from appearing for highly specific and urgent customer inquiries.

  • An Uninspiring Photo Gallery

    A bare-bones photo gallery is a huge red flag for potential customers. Most Cincinnati roofing contractors have fewer than 10 owner-uploaded photos, when a minimum of 30 high-quality images is recommended. This means you're likely missing vital visuals like before-and-after project shots, team photos on job sites, pictures of your service vehicles, and completed projects showcasing your craftsmanship.
