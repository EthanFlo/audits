The three fixes on 80% of audited hair salon profiles

  • Underutilized Business Description

    Many OKC hair salons underutilize the 750-character limit for their Google Business Profile description. I often find descriptions that are too short, vague, or simply list services without highlighting the salon's unique personality or specialties. This crucial text should be packed with keywords like "balayage," "men's cuts," "bridal updos," and your salon's distinct atmosphere, making it easier for Google to match you with local searchers seeking specific services and a particular vibe.

  • Missing Essential Secondary Categories

    Your primary category might be "Hair Salon," but many profiles miss essential secondary categories. I often find key descriptors like "Hair Colorist," "Barber Shop," or "Hair Extensions Service" are entirely absent. Adding these specific categories allows your OKC salon to appear in more targeted searches. This connects you directly with clients seeking specialized expertise, rather than just a general haircut, ensuring you capture a wider range of potential bookings.

  • Scarcity of Engaging Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Potential clients judge a hair salon by its look and feel before they even step inside. Many profiles I audit in Oklahoma City have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, often missing crucial visual content. This includes high-quality photos of client transformations, inviting interior shots, stylists at work, and product displays. A robust, frequently updated photo gallery builds trust and showcases your OKC salon's aesthetic and skill, which is vital for attracting new clients online.
