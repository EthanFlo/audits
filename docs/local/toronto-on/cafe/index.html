The three fixes on 80% of audited cafe profiles

  • Incomplete Business Description

    Many Toronto cafes are missing out on a crucial opportunity by using a business description under 400 characters, despite Google allowing up to 750. This means you're likely not showcasing your unique selling points like your ethical bean sourcing, award-winning latte art, signature almond croissants, cozy work-friendly atmosphere, or commitment to local artists. A rich description helps potential customers understand your cafe's full appeal before they even step inside, making their decision easier.

  • Neglecting Secondary Categories

    Too often, cafe Google Business Profiles only list "Cafe" as their primary category, ignoring vital secondary options. In Toronto, most cafes offer more than just coffee. You could be missing out on customers searching for "Brunch Restaurant," "Coffee Shop," or "Bakery." Without these specific categories, your profile won't surface for these highly relevant searches, sending potential patrons to competitors who have fully optimized their listing.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Google Business Profiles with fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos struggle to capture attention. Customers want to visualize their experience, especially when choosing a cafe in a vibrant city like Toronto. Missing are crucial shots of your perfectly brewed espressos, inviting interior seating, bustling outdoor patio, fresh daily pastries, or even a smiling barista crafting a drink. High-quality, diverse photos are essential for illustrating your cafe's ambiance and offerings.
