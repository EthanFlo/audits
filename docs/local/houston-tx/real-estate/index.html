The three fixes on 80% of audited real estate agent profiles

  • Underutilized Business Description

    Many Houston real estate agents underutilize their Google Business Profile description, often keeping it under 400 characters when Google allows up to 750. This means missing valuable space to highlight specialties like first-time buyer assistance, luxury home sales in River Oaks, or specific neighborhood expertise in The Heights, limiting discoverability for precise client searches.

  • Missing Secondary Categories

    A common oversight for real estate agents in Houston is only selecting "Real Estate Agency" as their primary category. Profiles frequently miss crucial secondary categories such as "Buyer's Agent," "Seller's Agent," or "Property Manager." Without these, Google struggles to accurately match your services with specific buyer- or seller-focused searches, reducing your local search visibility.

  • Insufficient Photo Gallery

    Many real estate agent profiles have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, failing to leverage visual content to build trust and engagement. We often find a lack of professional headshots, team photos, images showcasing agent activity (e.g., at open houses or closings), client testimonials (with permission), and photos highlighting iconic Houston landmarks or specific neighborhood vibes.
