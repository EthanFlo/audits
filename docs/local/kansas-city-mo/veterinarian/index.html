The three fixes on 80% of audited veterinarian profiles

  • Underutilized Business Description

    Many Kansas City vet profiles barely scratch the surface with their business description. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet I frequently see clinics using less than 400. This is a missed opportunity to showcase your unique services, compassionate approach to pet care, or specialized equipment right in your profile, leaving potential clients to guess what makes your practice stand out among other KC vets.

  • Missing Key Secondary Categories

    Beyond the primary "Veterinarian" category, many KC veterinary clinics neglect to add crucial secondary categories. This oversight severely limits how many different searches your practice can appear in. Proper categorization expands your reach to pet owners searching for specific services like "Emergency Vet," "Animal Hospital," or "Pet Surgery," ensuring you don't miss out on vital patient leads.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Most vet profiles in Kansas City have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. High-quality images of your clinic's exterior, interior waiting areas, exam rooms, and especially your friendly staff interacting with pets build trust and warmth. Missing are photos showcasing diagnostic equipment, surgical suites, or even a welcoming "meet the team" gallery, all crucial for a pet owner choosing a new vet in the metro area.
