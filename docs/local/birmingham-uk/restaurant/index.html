The three fixes on 80% of audited restaurant profiles

  • Underexploited Business Description

    Many Birmingham restaurants use less than half of Google's generous 750-character limit for their business description. This means missing a prime opportunity to highlight unique selling points like your signature dishes, award-winning chef, specific ambiance, or whether you cater for special dietary needs. A concise, keyword-rich description acts as a vital virtual welcome, enticing potential diners scrolling through local results.

  • Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Your primary category might be "Restaurant," but missing secondary categories significantly limits your reach. I often find profiles omitting classifications like "Indian Restaurant," "Fine Dining Restaurant," or "Vegan Restaurant," despite offering these specific cuisines or experiences. Properly defining these helps Google connect you with highly targeted searches, ensuring you appear when a diner is specifically craving your specialty.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    In a visual city like Birmingham, potential diners rely heavily on imagery. Many restaurants fall short of the ideal 30+ high-quality, owner-uploaded photos. This often means missing shots of your beautifully plated dishes, inviting interior and exterior, a bustling bar area, or your friendly staff in action. Robust, recent photography builds trust and creates an irresistible visual story that converts browsers into paying customers.
