The three fixes on 80% of audited physical therapist profiles

Incomplete Business Description

Many Atlanta physical therapists limit their Google Business Profile description to just a few sentences, often under 400 characters, missing out on valuable keyword opportunities. Google allows up to 750 characters, giving you ample space to detail your specialties, patient-centric philosophy, and unique treatment approaches, whether it’s dry needling, manual therapy, or specific sports injury rehab. A thorough description helps Google understand exactly who you serve and allows prospective patients to connect with your specific services more effectively, standing out in a crowded market.

Missing Secondary Categories

Your primary category, "Physical Therapist," is essential, but many Atlanta clinics neglect to add crucial secondary categories. These additional classifications tell Google and potential patients more about your specialized services. Common missing categories include "Sports Medicine Clinic," "Rehabilitation Center," and "Pediatric Physical Therapy." Identifying and adding these specific categories significantly broadens your reach, ensuring you appear in searches for specific conditions or patient demographics beyond just general physical therapy needs across the city.

Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

Many profiles fall short of the recommended 30+ owner-uploaded images. For Atlanta physical therapy clinics, this means missing key opportunities to showcase your inviting waiting areas, state-of-the-art equipment, and therapists actively engaging with patients during treatments. Photos of your compassionate team, clean treatment rooms, and diverse patient demographics (with consent) build immediate trust. This visual content is critical for attracting new patients searching for local care, helping them envision their experience before they even call.
