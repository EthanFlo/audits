The three fixes on 80% of audited real estate agent profiles

  • Incomplete Business Description

    Many Winnipeg real estate agents unknowingly limit their profile's reach by only using a fraction of the available 750 characters in their business description. This often means crucial details about their local expertise, specific neighbourhoods they serve (e.g., River Heights, St. Boniface), or unique selling propositions (like first-time home buyer specialists or luxury market focus) are left out. A too-short description misses vital keywords potential clients use, hindering visibility in targeted local searches.

  • Neglecting Secondary Categories

    A common oversight for real estate professionals in Winnipeg is not fully utilizing secondary categories. While "Real Estate Agency" is standard, many agents miss opportunities to specify their niche. Adding categories like "Buyer's Agent," "Seller's Agent," and "Property Manager" precisely tells Google and prospective clients exactly what services you offer. This ensures your profile appears for highly specific searches, connecting you with clients who know exactly what type of agent they need.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Real estate is a visual business, yet many agents have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos on their profile. This often means missing critical visuals that build trust and showcase expertise. What's often absent are photos of the agent in action (showing homes, at open houses), team photos, images of key Winnipeg landmarks they serve, and client testimonials with consent. A rich photo gallery communicates professionalism and helps clients connect with you before they even call.
