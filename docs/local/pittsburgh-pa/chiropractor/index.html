The three fixes on 80% of audited chiropractor profiles

  • Underutilized Business Description

    Many Pittsburgh chiropractors only scratch the surface of their Google Business Profile description, often using less than 400 characters when Google allows up to 750. This means missing a crucial opportunity to detail your specialized techniques, patient focus (e.g., pediatric, sports injuries, chronic pain management), and unique approach that sets you apart from other local clinics. A sparse description fails to capture attention or provide search engines enough context about your practice.

  • Missing Key Secondary Categories

    Your primary category is "Chiropractor," but neglecting secondary categories severely limits your discoverability. I frequently find practices in Pittsburgh, PA, missing vital additional categories such as "Sports Chiropractor," "Auto Accident Chiropractor," or "Family Chiropractor." These categories allow patients searching for specific needs to find your clinic, significantly broadening your reach beyond generic chiropractic searches within the Pittsburgh area.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Often, chiropractic profiles feature fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, a significant oversight. What’s typically missing are high-quality images of your treatment rooms, welcoming reception area, diverse staff (not just the main doctor), equipment in use, or even patient testimonials (with permission). These visuals build trust and show potential patients what to expect, vital for attracting new clients who want to feel comfortable before their first visit.
