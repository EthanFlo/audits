The three fixes on 80% of audited photographer profiles

  • Underutilized Business Description

    Many Leeds photographers are leaving hundreds of valuable characters unused in their Google Business Profile description. Instead of a powerful narrative that highlights your unique style, niche, and client experience – perhaps focusing on capturing the vibrant energy of city weddings or tranquil family portraits in Roundhay Park – your profile often features a sparse, keyword-light summary. This missed opportunity means Google and potential clients don't fully grasp your specialized offerings, hindering your visibility for crucial local searches.

  • Missing Critical Secondary Categories

    Your primary category, like 'Photographer,' is a good start, but relying solely on it severely limits your reach. I frequently find Leeds photographers missing essential secondary categories that specifically tell Google exactly what services you offer. Adding relevant options such as 'Wedding Photographer,' 'Portrait Studio,' and 'Real Estate Photographer' ensures you appear in highly targeted searches. Without these, you're invisible to clients searching for niche photography services, handing opportunities directly to your competitors.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Visuals are paramount for photographers, yet many Google Business Profiles showcase fewer than 30 owner-uploaded images. Beyond just client portfolio shots, I often see profiles lacking crucial photos that build trust and engagement. This includes images of your studio space if you have one, your team, candid shots of you in action, examples of your print products, and even client testimonials or behind-the-scenes glimpses. A rich, diverse photo gallery convinces potential Leeds clients of your professionalism and quality.
