The three fixes on 80% of audited law firm profiles

  • Your Business Description Sells You Short

    Many Oklahoma City law firms use only a brief sentence or two for their Google Business Profile description. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet most firms use under 400. This is a missed opportunity to articulate your practice areas, client focus, unique selling propositions, and commitment to the OKC community. A thin description fails to fully inform potential clients about what makes your firm the right choice, leaving them to guess rather than convert.

  • Critical Secondary Categories Are Missing

    Your primary Google Business Profile category gets you in the door, but secondary categories tell prospective clients exactly what you do. I often find OKC law firms missing crucial specific categories like "Personal Injury Lawyer," "Family Law Attorney," or "Estate Planning Lawyer." Without these, you limit your visibility for highly specific searches, meaning clients looking for exactly what you offer might never see your listing among their top options on Google Maps.

  • Your Profile Lacks Engaging Visuals

    A Google Business Profile with fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos fails to build trust or showcase professionalism effectively. I frequently find OKC law firms missing critical images such as team headshots, interior office photos (reception area, conference rooms), clear exterior building shots, or even pictures of community involvement. Prospects want to see who they’ll be working with and where they’ll meet, yet many profiles remain visually barren, missing this vital connection point with potential clients.
