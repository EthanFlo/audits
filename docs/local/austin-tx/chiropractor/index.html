The three fixes on 80% of audited chiropractor profiles

  • Underutilized Business Description

    Many Austin chiropractors have a Google Business Profile description that's far too short, often under 400 characters, when Google allows up to 750. This means you're missing a crucial opportunity to showcase your unique approach, specialized treatments like spinal decompression or prenatal care, and what makes your practice stand out. A sparse description fails to capture the attention of potential patients actively seeking specific chiropractic solutions in our city.

  • Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Beyond "Chiropractor," many profiles in Austin neglect to add vital secondary categories. This limits how many specific searches you can appear for. I frequently see practices missing key categories such as "Sports Chiropractor," "Auto Accident Chiropractor," or "Family Chiropractor." Adding these tells Google exactly what services you offer, connecting you with more targeted patient searches looking for specialized care beyond general adjustments.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Most Austin chiropractic profiles have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, a significant oversight. This typically means missing inviting photos of your waiting room, treatment rooms, happy patients interacting with staff, or clear exterior shots from different angles. High-quality, diverse images build trust and show prospective patients what to expect, making your practice more appealing than competitors with outdated or generic visuals.
