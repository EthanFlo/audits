The three fixes on 80% of audited restaurant profiles

  • Your Google Business Profile Description is Underselling You

    Many Edinburgh restaurants use fewer than 400 characters for their Google Business Profile description, missing a huge opportunity. Google allows up to 750 characters to highlight your unique atmosphere, signature dishes, chef's story, or commitment to local Scottish produce. This shortchanges potential diners, failing to convey what makes your establishment truly special and enticing them to choose you over a competitor.

  • You're Missing Out on Niche Customer Searches

    Relying solely on "Restaurant" as your primary category drastically limits your reach. I often find businesses haven't optimised their secondary categories. For an Edinburgh restaurant, adding specific categories like "Scottish Restaurant," "Seafood Restaurant," or "Vegan Restaurant" can capture highly targeted searches, ensuring you appear when locals or tourists look for exactly what you offer, not just a generic eatery.

  • Your Restaurant's Visual Appeal Isn't Shining

    Visuals are key for restaurants, yet many profiles have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. Customers want to see what to expect. I often find profiles lacking inviting interior shots, high-quality images of signature dishes, happy diners, the bar area, or your unique exterior lighting at dusk. These missing visuals mean potential customers are less likely to choose your restaurant when browsing options on Google Maps.
