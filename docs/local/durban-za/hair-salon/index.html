The three fixes on 80% of audited hair salon profiles

  • Your Business Description Sells You Short

    Many hair salons in Durban use less than half the available 750 characters in their Google Business Profile description. This is a huge missed opportunity to showcase your specialties, such as intricate braiding, advanced colour techniques, relaxing treatments, or bespoke bridal styling. A comprehensive description allows Google to better understand your offerings and match you with clients searching for those specific services, making your salon more discoverable.

  • Ignoring Crucial Secondary Categories

    Beyond the primary "Hair Salon" category, Google offers several secondary categories that significantly enhance your visibility for niche searches. Often, profiles are missing these vital additions. For maximum impact in Durban's diverse market, I often find salons need to add categories like "Hair Extension Technician," "Hair Colourist," and "Barber Shop" to accurately reflect their full service range and attract targeted clientele.

  • A Photo Gallery That Doesn't Inspire

    Having fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos is a common oversight that leaves potential clients without a clear visual of your salon's vibe and expertise. High-quality images of your beautifully styled clients, a welcoming salon interior, your talented team in action, and close-ups of specific treatments (like vibrant balayage or intricate weaves) are essential. These visuals build trust and showcase the quality of your work, directly influencing booking decisions.
