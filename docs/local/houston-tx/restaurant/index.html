The three fixes on 80% of audited restaurant profiles

  • Your Business Description is Incomplete

    Many Houston restaurants use less than 400 of the 750 available characters in their Google Business Profile description. This is a massive missed opportunity. You should be spotlighting your unique culinary style, signature dishes like award-winning BBQ, or the vibrant ambiance of your Midtown location. A sparse description fails to capture attention, leaving potential diners unaware of what truly makes your establishment special and worth visiting over the competition.

  • Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    A surprising number of Houston restaurants only list one primary category. Google allows you to add up to nine additional secondary categories, which significantly expands your reach. If you're a "Restaurant," you might also be a "Tex-Mex Restaurant," "Seafood Restaurant," or "Brunch Restaurant." Omitting these limits the specific searches you can appear in, making it harder for diners to find your unique cuisine when they're actively looking for it.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    In a visual city like Houston, inadequate photos are a huge deterrent. Many profiles have fewer than 30 high-quality, owner-uploaded images, relying instead on customer snapshots. You're missing critical shots of your meticulously plated entrees, the inviting interior and patio spaces, your friendly staff in action, and the exterior for easy navigation. A lack of professional, diverse imagery fails to showcase your restaurant's full appeal to browsing customers.
