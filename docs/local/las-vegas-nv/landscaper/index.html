The three fixes on 80% of audited landscaper profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many Las Vegas landscapers leave their Google Business Profile description almost empty, missing a huge opportunity. Google allows up to 750 characters, but I often see profiles using less than 400. This prime real estate should detail your unique services – from xeriscaping and desert-friendly designs to custom paver patios and outdoor lighting – and highlight why you’re the best choice for a local Las Vegas yard. A detailed description helps both search engines and potential clients understand your expertise.

  • You’re Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    While "Landscaper" is your primary category, many profiles stop there, leaving valuable ranking potential on the table. Google allows up to ten categories. For Las Vegas landscapers, key secondary categories like "Lawn Care Service," "Hardscaping Contractor," and "Tree Service" are often overlooked. Adding these helps your business appear for more specific, high-intent searches. If a client is specifically looking for a new patio or palm tree trimming, you want to be visible for those precise needs.

  • Your Profile Lacks Sufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Visual appeal is critical for landscapers, yet many profiles have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. Customers want to see your work! I often find missing images of recent drought-tolerant installations, before-and-after transformations, stunning water features, or even your professional crew in action. High-quality photos showcasing various projects across different Las Vegas neighborhoods build trust and demonstrate your capabilities far better than stock images. More photos mean more engagement and confidence from potential clients.
