The three fixes on 80% of audited photographer profiles

  • Your Google Business Profile Description is Too Short

    Many Glasgow photographers don't realise their Google Business Profile description can be up to 750 characters. I often find profiles using less than 400, leaving vital space unused. This prevents you from showcasing your unique style, target clientele, and specialist services like newborn, commercial, or event photography, making it harder for potential clients to choose you over rivals actively searching online.

  • You're Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Your primary category might be "Photographer," but secondary categories are key for specific searches. I frequently see profiles without "Wedding Photographer," "Portrait Studio," or "Real Estate Photography" – all vital for Glasgow-specific searches. Adding these tells Google precisely what services you offer, significantly improving visibility for those niche, high-value client queries that lead to direct bookings.

  • Your Profile Lacks Sufficient High-Quality Photos

    A strong Google Business Profile for a photographer should ideally feature 30+ owner-uploaded photos. I often see profiles with fewer, missing key types. Potential clients want to see your studio space, a diverse portfolio of recent work (weddings, portraits, commercial projects), candid behind-the-scenes shots, and even your team. These visuals build trust and showcase your expertise before they even click to your website.
