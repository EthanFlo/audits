The three fixes on 80% of audited accountant profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many Orlando accountants limit their Google Business Profile description to just a few sentences, often under 400 characters. Google allows up to 750! This is a missed opportunity to showcase your specialties, talk about serving Orlando's unique small business community, or detail specific tax planning strategies. We need to expand this with rich, keyword-optimized content that truly informs potential clients about your firm's value.

  • Missing Key Secondary Categories

    Simply listing "Accountant" as your primary category isn't enough to capture all the local searches. I frequently find firms neglecting vital secondary categories. For accountants in Orlando, you should also be using "Tax Preparation," "Bookkeeping," and "Small Business CPA." Adding these precise categories significantly broadens your reach and connects you with clients actively searching for those specific services.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    A surprising number of accountant profiles in Orlando have very few, or generic, images. Google prioritizes profiles with robust visual content, ideally 30 or more high-quality, owner-uploaded photos. This often means missing professional shots of your office interior, your dedicated team, pictures of client meeting areas, or the building's exterior. These visuals build trust and professionalism before a client even calls.
