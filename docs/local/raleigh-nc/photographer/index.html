The three fixes on 80% of audited photographer profiles

  • 1. An Underutilized Business Description

    Many Raleigh photographers overlook the power of the Google Business Profile description, often leaving it short or generic. Google allows up to 750 characters, but I frequently see profiles with descriptions under 400 characters, failing to fully showcase their unique photographic style, niche (like newborn, corporate, or senior portraits), and what makes their service exceptional to potential clients searching locally. This is a missed opportunity to connect.

  • 2. Neglecting Secondary Categories

    Your primary category might be 'Photographer,' but Google allows you to add several secondary categories to capture more specific searches. Too many Raleigh photography businesses miss out on this crucial step, limiting their visibility. I often find profiles that could greatly benefit from including categories like 'Wedding Photographer,' 'Portrait Studio,' or 'Real Estate Photography Service' to precisely match specialized client needs.

  • 3. Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Clients are highly visual, especially when choosing a photographer. I frequently see profiles with fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, which is a significant drawback. This often means missing critical visual elements like 'behind-the-scenes' shots, studio interiors (if applicable), client testimonials with photos, images showcasing various specialties, and professional headshots, which build trust and illustrate your brand's personality.
