The three fixes on 80% of audited photographer profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many Cape Town photographers miss a huge opportunity by keeping their Google Business Profile description under 400 characters, leaving valuable space unused. Google allows up to 750 characters. This often means missing crucial keywords like "Cape Town wedding photography," "lifestyle portraits," or "commercial photography Western Cape," and failing to articulate your unique style or niche that truly resonates with local clients searching for specific services.

  • Missing Secondary Categories

    A common audit finding is photographers only using a single primary category. This significantly limits your reach. To capture diverse searches in Cape Town, you should leverage secondary categories like "Wedding Photographer," "Portrait Studio," and "Commercial Photographer." Without these, clients specifically seeking those services are less likely to discover your business, even if you offer them.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Google Business Profiles with extensive, high-quality photos perform better. Photographers often neglect uploading a diverse range beyond just portfolio shots. We typically find missing key visuals like behind-the-scenes glimpses, photos of your studio or specific shoot locations in Cape Town, pictures of your team, and client testimonials with accompanying images. This visual deficit can make your profile less engaging and trustworthy to potential clients.
