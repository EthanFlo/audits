The three fixes on 80% of audited restaurant profiles

  • Your Google Business Profile Description is Underutilised

    Many Adelaide restaurants only scratch the surface with their Google Business Profile description. Google allows up to 750 characters, but most profiles I see are well under 400. This is a missed opportunity to highlight your unique menu items, the ambiance, your head chef's vision, or locally sourced ingredients that make your restaurant special and entice potential diners.

  • Missing Critical Secondary Categories

    Your primary category is vital, but secondary categories tell Google exactly what other dining experiences you offer, significantly improving your visibility for specific searches. I frequently find restaurants missing these. Adding precise categories like "Italian Restaurant," "Modern Australian Restaurant," or "Seafood Restaurant" ensures you appear in niche searches beyond just "Restaurant."

  • Scarcity of High-Quality, Diverse Photos

    A picture truly is worth a thousand words for a restaurant. Many profiles have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, which is insufficient. Diners need to see more than just a few menu shots. You're likely missing mouth-watering, perfectly plated dishes, your restaurant's inviting interior and exterior, a bustling kitchen, or even happy customers enjoying their meals.
