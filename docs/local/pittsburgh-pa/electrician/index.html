The three fixes on 80% of audited electrician profiles

  • Underutilized Business Description (Under 400 Characters)

    Many electricians in Pittsburgh miss a huge opportunity by only using a short, generic description. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet most profiles barely scratch 200. This prime real estate should detail your specializations like residential upgrades, commercial wiring, or generator installations. A robust description packed with relevant keywords helps Google understand your services better, significantly improving your chances of ranking for specific electrician searches beyond just "electrician near me."

  • Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Your primary category is "Electrician," but Google Business Profile allows several secondary categories. I often find Pittsburgh electricians missing out on vital ones that could capture niche searches. For example, adding "Emergency Electrician," "EV Charging Station Installation Service," or "Electric Panel Installation Service" can help you rank for those specific, high-intent searches. Neglecting these signals to Google that your services are broader, not specialized, hurting your visibility when customers have precise needs.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Google prioritizes active, well-maintained profiles, and photos are a huge trust signal for prospective clients. Many electrician profiles have fewer than 10 photos, usually just a logo. Your profile should ideally have at least 30 owner-uploaded images, including team photos, company vehicles, safety equipment, before-and-after examples of your work, and even your Pittsburgh-based service area. Missing these visuals makes your business appear less established and trustworthy compared to visually rich competitor profiles.
