The three fixes on 80% of audited physical therapist profiles

  • Business Description Under 400 Characters

    Many Jacksonville physical therapists only scratch the surface with their Google Business Profile description, often using less than half of the available 750 characters. This is a missed opportunity to highlight your unique specializations like orthopedic rehab, concussion therapy, or specific techniques. A detailed description allows you to weave in keywords potential patients are searching for, telling Google exactly what services you excel at and why your clinic stands out in Duval County.

  • Missing Key Secondary Categories

    While "Physical Therapist" is your primary category, neglecting secondary categories significantly limits your visibility. I often find practices missing crucial additions like "Sports Medicine Clinic," "Orthopedic Physical Therapist," or "Pain Management Clinic." These specific categories help you appear for niche searches from athletes recovering from injuries or residents seeking relief for chronic conditions, ensuring you connect with the right patients who need your specialized care.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Your Google Business Profile should be a visual showcase, yet many clinics have fewer than a dozen photos. I frequently see profiles lacking images of the modern clinic interior, the welcoming reception area, or your expert therapists in action (even if staged). High-quality photos of your equipment, treatment rooms, and friendly staff build trust and give potential patients a clear sense of your practice before they even call, making them more likely to choose you.

Leveraging every character in your description, adding all relevant categories, and showcasing your facility with ample photos allows Google to understand your specific specialties. This precision targeting ensures you appear for those high-intent, long-tail searches that lead directly to new appointments, helping your Jacksonville clinic attract the exact patient demographic it’s best equipped to serve.
