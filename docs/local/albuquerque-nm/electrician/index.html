The three fixes on 80% of audited electrician profiles

  • Your Google Business Profile Description is Too Short

    Many Albuquerque electricians use a generic, short description that barely scratches the surface of what they offer. Google allows up to 750 characters, but I often find profiles with descriptions under 400 characters. This is a missed opportunity to highlight your specialized services like generator installation, smart home wiring, or commercial electrical work, directly telling potential customers in the North Valley or Rio Rancho why you're the best choice.

  • Neglecting Crucial Secondary Business Categories

    A common oversight is failing to utilize all relevant secondary categories. Beyond 'Electrician,' many profiles miss out on vital tags that connect them with specific customer needs. I frequently see profiles in Albuquerque without categories like 'Emergency Electrician,' 'EV Charger Installation Service,' or 'Electric Panel Upgrade Service,' preventing them from showing up for high-value, specific searches across the city.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos and Visual Proof

    Many electrician profiles fall short on the visual front, with fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. Customers want to see your work, your team, and your professionalism. This often means missing crucial images of completed projects – before-and-after shots of panel upgrades, safety measures on commercial jobs, close-ups of quality wiring, or even your service vehicles and team members serving different Albuquerque neighborhoods.
