The three fixes on 80% of audited plumber profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many Miami plumbers underutilize the Google Business Profile description, often leaving it under 400 characters when Google allows up to 750. This is a missed opportunity to showcase your specialties like water heater repair, emergency leak detection, or drain cleaning expertise. A brief description fails to include vital keywords local customers are searching for, making your business less discoverable and less appealing compared to competitors who fully detail their services and customer-focused approach.

  • Crucial Secondary Categories Are Missing

    Beyond "Plumber," many profiles neglect to add specific secondary categories, severely limiting their visibility for niche searches. If you aren't listed as an "Emergency Plumber," "Drain Cleaning Service," or "Water Heater Installation Service," Google won't show your business to customers seeking those exact needs. This oversight means direct leads are diverted elsewhere, even if you excel in these areas, because your profile doesn't explicitly tell Google what else you do.

  • Insufficient or Generic Photos

    Local searchers highly value visual proof of professionalism, yet many plumber profiles have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, often featuring just a logo. Missing are images of your team in uniform, branded vans on a job site, technicians performing common repairs, or clear shots of your Miami service area. These specific visual cues build trust and demonstrate reliability, which is critical when customers need urgent plumbing help and are choosing between local options.
