The three fixes on 80% of audited landscaper profiles

  • Your Description is Mowing Down Opportunities

    Many Miami landscapers use only a few short sentences in their Google Business Profile description. Google allows up to 750 characters, but I often see profiles barely hitting 200-300. This is a missed chance to showcase your unique services – from hurricane-resistant planting to drought-tolerant designs – and tell Miami clients why you're the best choice for their tropical outdoor spaces. Let's make every character count.

  • Ignoring Key Secondary Service Categories

    Beyond "Landscaper," many profiles overlook crucial secondary categories that significantly boost visibility. I frequently find businesses missing categories like "Lawn Care Service," "Hardscape Contractor," or "Tree Service." In Miami, clients search for these specific needs. Without them, you're invisible for specialized services, directly costing you leads for everything from paver patios to palm tree trimming.

  • A Sparse Photo Gallery That Underwhelms

    Your Google Business Profile should be a vibrant portfolio, yet I often see fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. Customers want to visualize their dream yard. Are you showcasing your stunning before-and-after transformations, unique water features, sustainable garden designs, or your team in action? Missing these dynamic visuals means lost opportunities to impress Miami clients and differentiate your work from the competition.
