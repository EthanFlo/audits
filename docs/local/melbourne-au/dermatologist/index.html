The three fixes on 80% of audited dermatologist profiles

  • Underutilised Business Description

    Many Melbourne dermatologists fail to leverage the full 750-character potential of their Google Business Profile description, often leaving critical information out. I frequently find profiles with descriptions under 400 characters, missing opportunities to highlight specific treatments like advanced melanoma screenings, aesthetic rejuvenation, or chronic skin condition management. A comprehensive description not only informs potential patients but also signals relevancy to Google, improving your chances of ranking for specialized searches across Melbourne suburbs.

  • Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Your primary category might be "Dermatologist," but Google allows up to nine additional categories to specify your services. I often discover profiles missing vital secondary categories like "Cosmetic Dermatology," "Skin Cancer Clinic," or "Pediatric Dermatology." Without these, you're invisible to patients explicitly searching for these specialised services. Optimising these categories is critical for capturing niche patient segments within Melbourne and ensures your practice appears for the most relevant searches.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    A surprising number of dermatology profiles in Melbourne lack a robust collection of owner-uploaded photos, often having fewer than 30. Patients want to see your modern clinic interior, the welcoming reception area, state-of-the-art treatment rooms, and your friendly team. Missing are visuals that build trust and show professionalism, such as detailed photos of specialized equipment or staff interaction with patients. High-quality, diverse imagery significantly boosts patient confidence and engagement, converting more views into visits.
