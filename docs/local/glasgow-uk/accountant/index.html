The three fixes on 80% of audited accountant profiles

Too Short: Your Business Description

Many Glasgow accountants miss a golden opportunity by leaving their Google Business Profile description brief and unoptimised. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet most firms use under 400. This space is crucial to detail your specialisms – perhaps you excel in contractor accounts, small business tax, or payroll for local SMEs – and highlight your unique value proposition to potential clients actively searching for those exact services. Maximise this text to tell your full story.

Overlooked: Secondary Service Categories

Your primary category might be "Accountant," but the real power comes from leveraging secondary categories. We often find Glasgow firms failing to utilise additional, highly relevant classifications such as "Tax preparation service," "Bookkeeping service," or "Payroll service." These specific categories are vital for matching your profile with precise user searches, ensuring you appear for clients looking for more than just a generic accounting firm, helping you capture niche market segments.

Scarcity: Lack of Owner-Uploaded Photos

A visually barren Google Business Profile is a missed connection. Most Glasgow accountants have fewer than 10 owner-uploaded photos, far from the ideal 30+. Clients want to see your professional office environment, your friendly team at work (perhaps with blurred faces for privacy), a clear photo of your building exterior for easy navigation, and perhaps even local Glasgow landmarks near your office to reinforce your local presence. High-quality images build trust and make your firm more approachable.
