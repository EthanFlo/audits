Best Google Business categories for a hair salon

Optimizing your Google Business Profile (GBP) categories is one of the most impactful, yet frequently overlooked, strategies for local SEO. Your primary category defines your core business, but it's the nine secondary categories that dramatically expand your visibility, effectively creating ten distinct "ranking lanes" on Google Maps and Search. Many hair salons only select their primary category, leaving nine valuable opportunities untapped and ceding crucial search territory to competitors.

By strategically populating all available category slots, a hair salon can capture a much wider array of specific service-based searches. This ensures that when potential clients search for anything from "balayage specialist" to "men's haircut" or "bridal hair", your salon has a direct chance to appear. Leveraging the full spectrum of relevant categories is critical for dominating local search results and attracting a broader clientele beyond just generic "hair salon near me" queries.

The three fixes on 80% of audited hair salon profiles

Hair Salon

The most precise and impactful primary category for a general hair salon is "Hair Salon." This term directly communicates your core offering to Google's algorithm and users, aligning perfectly with the most common and direct search queries. While "Beauty Salon" is an option, it is too broad and dilutes your specificity for hair services, potentially making you compete with businesses primarily focused on nails, skincare, or waxing. "Hairdresser" is also available but often implies a sole proprietor rather than a full-service salon, making "Hair Salon" the optimal choice for comprehensive local search visibility.

Why these matter specifically for hair salons in Categories

  1. **Hairdresser**: Unlocks searches for individual stylists and those seeking personal hair care professionals, complementing the broader "Hair Salon" primary.
  2. **Hair coloring service**: Catches high-intent searches like "balayage near me," "hair dye," or "highlights," essential for salons specializing in color.
  3. **Hair extension technician**: Targets clients specifically looking for services related to hair extensions, from installation to maintenance.
  4. **Barber shop**: Crucial for salons that cater to men's haircuts, beard trims, and traditional barbering services, capturing a distinct male clientele.
  5. **Cosmetologist**: Positions your business for broader beauty professional searches, indicating a range of skilled services beyond just basic cuts.
  6. **Makeup artist**: If your salon offers professional makeup applications, particularly for events or photoshoots, this category connects you with relevant beauty bookings.
  7. **Hair braiding service**: Attracts clients specifically seeking intricate braiding, twists, or dreadlocks, a niche but often popular service.
  8. **Children's hair salon**: Targets families and parents searching for kid-friendly haircuts and environments, expanding your demographic reach.
  9. **Wedding service**: Essential for capturing searches related to bridal hair styling and wedding day preparations, directly addressing the "Bridal" hint.
