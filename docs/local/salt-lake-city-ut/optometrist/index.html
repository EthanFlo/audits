The three fixes on 80% of audited optometrist profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many Salt Lake City optometrists use a brief, generic description like "Trusted eye care in SLC." Google allows up to 750 characters, yet most profiles barely scratch 400. This is a missed opportunity to detail your specialized services, state-of-the-art equipment, and patient-focused approach. A comprehensive description improves relevance for specific searches and tells potential patients exactly why they should choose your practice for their eye health needs.

  • Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    While "Optometrist" is your primary category, neglecting secondary categories significantly limits your visibility. I frequently find practices missing key specific services that local patients are searching for. Optimizing your profile with additional categories like "Eye Exam," "Contact Lens Fitting," and "Pediatric Eye Care" ensures your practice appears for a wider array of high-intent searches, capturing patients looking for these exact services.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Google Business Profiles with robust photo galleries significantly outperform those without. I often see Salt Lake City optometrists with just a few exterior shots. Patients want to see your friendly staff, the cleanliness of your exam rooms, your optical dispensary's frame selection, and perhaps even some behind-the-scenes glimpses of your advanced diagnostic tools. More photos build trust and encourage engagement from local searchers.
