How to optimize a moving company's Google Business Profile in 2026

Are your phones silent while competitors with fewer reviews seem to get all the calls? Frustrated by being stuck on page two of Google Maps, despite offering superior service and a better reputation? Many moving companies struggle because their Google Business Profile is an afterthought, not a lead-generating powerhouse. This isn't just about presence; it's about prominence.

I'm here to fix that. This 2026 guide will show you exactly how to optimize your moving company's Google Business Profile. By following these steps, you will outrank local competitors, attract more high-quality leads, and keep your moving crews consistently booked. Let's make your Google Business Profile your best salesperson.

The three fixes on 80% of audited moving company profiles

  1. Optimize Your Business Description: Craft a keyword-rich description (up to 750 characters) that details your services. Include terms like "local moves," "long distance moving," "packing services," "storage solutions," and mention your primary service areas to attract relevant searchers looking for your moving company.
  2. Select the Perfect Primary Category: The absolute most critical step. Your primary category must be "Moving Company." Google uses this to understand your core business, so do not dilute it with less relevant choices. This ensures you appear for essential "movers near me" searches.
  3. Add Relevant Secondary Categories: Expand your reach with niche categories that accurately reflect additional services. Include categories like "Long Distance Movers," "Office Relocation Service," "Piano Mover," or "Packing Service" to capture specific customer segments looking for specialized moving solutions.
  4. Populate Your Services List: Exhaustively list every service your moving company offers. Go beyond "Moving" and create separate, keyword-rich entries for "Residential Moving," "Commercial Relocation," "Packing & Unpacking," "Furniture Disassembly & Assembly," and "Storage Solutions."
  5. Upload High-Quality, Geotagged Photos: Showcase your professional crews, clean, branded trucks, and successful moves. Include photos of your office, storage facilities, and team. Ensure photos are geotagged with your service locations to boost local search relevance for your moving company.
  6. Maintain a Consistent Google Posts Cadence: Publish a new Google Post weekly. Highlight promotions, new equipment, success stories, or moving tips. Include strong calls to action like "Get a Free Quote" and link directly to service pages to drive immediate engagement.
  7. Drive Consistent Review Velocity: Actively solicit new 5-star reviews from every satisfied customer immediately after their move. Text or email review links. New, positive reviews are vital for ranking higher and building trust. Respond to all reviews, both positive and negative, promptly and professionally.
  8. Seed Your Q&A Section: Proactively answer common questions potential customers might ask. Create a list of 5-10 frequently asked questions (e.g., "Do you offer moving insurance?", "What areas do you serve?", "Can you move pianos?") and provide concise, helpful answers.

Why these matter specifically for moving companys in How To

  • Mistake: Ignoring negative reviews or responding unprofessionally. Fix: Respond promptly and empathetically to all reviews, especially negative ones, offering solutions and showing customer care.
  • Mistake: Using a P.O. Box or an unstaffed virtual office as your business address. Fix: Google requires a legitimate, physical business location where your moving company operates to ensure accurate local search results.
  • Mistake: Not keeping your business hours, holiday hours, or service areas consistently updated. Fix: Regularly verify and update all operational details to prevent customer frustration and ensure your moving company appears in relevant searches.
  • Mistake: Failing to fully leverage the "Services" section of your profile. Fix: Detail every specific service your moving company offers, from full-service packing to specialty item transport, for maximum keyword visibility.
