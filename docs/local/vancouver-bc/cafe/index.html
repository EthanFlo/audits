The three fixes on 80% of audited cafe profiles

  • Underutilized Business Description

    Many cafe owners in Vancouver miss the chance to fully describe their unique atmosphere, signature espresso blends, or artisanal pastries. Your current description might be a brief sentence, but Google allows up to 750 characters. This prime real estate is often overlooked, leaving potential customers unaware of what makes your Vancouver cafe truly special and why they should choose you over the competition.

  • Incomplete Category Selection

    Most cafes correctly select "Cafe" as their primary category, but stop there. Google allows multiple secondary categories which significantly expand your reach. I often find Vancouver cafes missing crucial additions like "Coffee Shop," "Brunch Restaurant," or "Bakery," preventing them from appearing in searches for those specific services. This limits visibility to customers seeking more than just a general cafe experience.

  • Insufficient Visual Content

    Customers eat with their eyes, especially when choosing a cafe. Many profiles have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, often missing key visuals. I typically see a lack of inviting interior shots, close-ups of latte art, delectable food items like avocado toast or croissants, exterior photos showcasing curb appeal, or even staff showcasing friendly service. Fresh, high-quality images drive engagement and trust.
