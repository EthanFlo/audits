How to Merge Duplicate Google Business Listings

How to Merge Duplicate Google Business Listings

To merge duplicate Google Business Listings, report the duplicate to Google via your Business Profile manager or directly on Google Maps, ensuring you manage the primary, most complete profile.

Understanding Duplicates and Their Impact

Duplicate Google Business Profiles occur for several reasons, often unintentionally. Common causes include business relocations, changes in ownership, multiple individuals creating profiles for the same location, Google’s own indexing errors, or user suggestions that create redundant entries. While seemingly minor, duplicate listings can significantly harm a business’s online presence and local search performance.

The negative impact of duplicates includes:

  • Confused Customers: Potential customers may encounter conflicting information, such as different phone numbers, hours, or addresses, leading to frustration and lost business.
  • Split Reviews: Customer reviews and ratings can be scattered across multiple profiles, diluting your overall rating and making it harder for searchers to gauge your business’s reputation.
  • Weakened SEO: Google’s algorithms prefer clear, consistent information. Duplicates can confuse search engines about which profile is authoritative, potentially lowering your local search ranking.
  • Wasted Effort: Maintaining multiple profiles means dividing your efforts in updating information, responding to reviews, and adding photos.

Identifying Your Authoritative Business Profile

Before you can merge or remove a duplicate, it's crucial to identify which of your listings is the primary, most accurate, and most valuable profile. This is the profile you want to keep and to which you’d like all relevant data, like reviews, to be consolidated.

Consider the following factors when determining your authoritative profile:

  1. Ownership and Management: Is it a profile you actively manage and have full access to through your Google Business Profile dashboard?
  2. Completeness and Accuracy: Does it contain the most up-to-date and comprehensive information (name, address, phone number, website, hours, services/products)?
  3. Review Count and Quality: Does it have the highest number of genuine customer reviews and a strong average rating? Google prioritizes profiles with more social proof.
  4. Photos and Updates: Has it been regularly updated with current photos and posts?
  5. Consistency: Does its information match your website and other major online directories?

Once you’ve identified your primary profile, any other identical or very similar listings for the same physical location should be considered duplicates.

Step-by-Step: Merging or Removing Duplicate Listings

Merging duplicate listings isn't a direct action you perform, but rather a request you submit to Google. Google’s team then reviews the request and performs the merge, ideally transferring reviews and other valuable data to your primary listing.

Option 1: Reporting a Duplicate You Manage (or Have Access To)

If you have access to both the primary and duplicate profiles within your Google Business Profile account, or suspect one of your managed profiles has a duplicate:

  1. Log in to your Google Business Profile manager. Go to business.google.com.
  2. Select your primary business profile. Ensure this is the listing you want to keep.
  3. Search for the duplicate within Google Maps. Even if you manage both, sometimes the public view on Maps reveals the issue more clearly. Locate the duplicate listing by searching for your business name and address.
  4. Contact Google Business Profile support.
    • From your Google Business Profile dashboard, look for the "Support" option (often represented by a question mark icon or found in a sidebar menu).
    • Explain the situation clearly, providing the full name and address of your primary listing and the full name and address of the duplicate listing(s). Include the Google Maps URLs for both profiles.
    • Specifically request that the duplicate profile(s) be merged into your primary, authoritative profile.
    • Google's support team will investigate and, if they confirm it's a duplicate, will proceed with the merge. This process can take several days to a few weeks.

Option 2: Reporting a Duplicate You Don't Manage (Public Reporting)

If you discover a duplicate listing for your business that you do not manage, you can report it directly on Google Maps:

  1. Search for the duplicate listing on Google Maps. Type your business name and address into Google Maps to find the erroneous listing.
  2. Click on the duplicate listing. This will open its information panel.
  3. Click "Suggest an edit." This option is typically found near the bottom of the information panel.
  4. Select "Close or remove."
  5. Choose "Duplicate place" as the reason.
  6. Indicate the correct listing. If prompted, you may be asked to link to or identify the correct, primary listing for your business. Provide as much detail as possible to help Google understand which listing is the authoritative one.
  7. Submit your suggestion. Google will review your suggestion. While this method is generally effective, it might take longer for Google to process, as they need to verify the information.

Concrete Example: Imagine "Reliable Pipes Plumbing," a reputable plumbing service, has its main office at 123 Main Street. They have a well-managed Google Business Profile with over 200 positive reviews. However, due to a previous employee setting up an old profile years ago, there's a duplicate listing for "Reliable Plumbing Co." at the same 123 Main Street address, but with an outdated phone number and only 15 reviews. The owner, recognizing the "Reliable Pipes Plumbing" profile as primary, would use Option 1. They would contact Google Business Profile support, providing the URLs for both listings, and request that "Reliable Plumbing Co." be merged into "Reliable Pipes Plumbing." Google would then consolidate the listings, ideally transferring the 15 reviews to the primary profile.

Preventing Future Duplicates and Maintaining Profile Health

Preventing duplicates is more efficient than merging them after they appear. Implement these practices to maintain a clean and effective Google Business Profile:

  1. Consistent NAP: Ensure your Name, Address, and Phone number are identical across your website, Google Business Profile, and all other online directories (citations).
  2. Designate a Single Manager: Assign one responsible individual or team to manage your Google Business Profile to avoid multiple, conflicting profile creations.
  3. Regular Audits: Periodically search for your business on Google Maps and Search to identify any new or old duplicate listings that may have appeared.
  4. Claim All Locations: For businesses with multiple physical locations, ensure each location has its own distinct, verified Google Business Profile.
  5. Verify Information: Always ensure all information, especially address details like suite numbers, is precise and consistent.
