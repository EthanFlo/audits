The three fixes on 80% of audited landscaper profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many Durban landscapers miss a crucial opportunity by using a bare-bones business description, often well under Google's 750-character limit. Instead of a quick sentence, your description should vividly detail your services—from garden design and irrigation installation to pool area landscaping and indigenous plant selection. A robust, keyword-rich description helps potential clients in Durban understand your full capabilities and helps Google match your business to relevant local searches beyond just "landscaper."

  • Underutilised Secondary Categories

    Simply listing yourself as a "Landscaper" isn't enough to capture all relevant searches in Durban. Many profiles miss vital secondary categories that signal your specific expertise to Google and potential clients. We frequently find profiles that should be leveraging categories like "Lawn Care Service," "Hardscape Designer," and "Tree Service." Adding these specific classifications significantly broadens your reach, ensuring you appear for more precise service searches in local areas like Umhlanga or Kloof.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    A visually compelling Google Business Profile is critical for landscapers, yet many Durban businesses have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. This often means missing images of stunning before-and-after transformations, diverse project examples like rockeries or water features, close-ups of planting schemes, or even team photos showing your crew in action. High-quality, diverse photos not only attract more clicks but also build trust and give potential clients a real sense of your craftsmanship.
