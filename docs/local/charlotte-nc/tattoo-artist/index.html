The three fixes on 80% of audited tattoo artist profiles

  • Your Google Business Profile Description is Too Short

    Many Charlotte tattoo artists I work with are only using a fraction of the available space in their Google Business Profile description. Google allows up to 750 characters, but most profiles are stuck under 400. This is a missed opportunity to showcase your distinct artistic style – whether it's black and grey realism, vibrant neo-traditional, or delicate fine line work – your studio's unique atmosphere, hygiene standards, and critical keywords that Charlotte clients are searching for. Let's make every character count.

  • You're Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Your primary category might be "Tattoo Shop," but that's just the beginning. Google allows you to add several secondary categories that vastly improve your visibility for niche searches. For tattoo artists in Charlotte, I often find missing categories like "Fine Line Tattoo Artist," "Traditional Tattoo Shop," or "Cover-Up Specialist." These specific tags help Google understand your specializations, connecting you with Charlotte clients searching for exact styles, rather than just a generic tattoo studio.

  • Your Photo Gallery Lacks Depth and Personality

    Most tattoo studios in Charlotte have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos on their profile, which is far too low. Beyond just a few examples of your finished art, I often find missing critical visuals. Clients want to see high-quality images of your clean studio interior, individual artist portraits, your sterilization setup, consultation areas, and a diverse portfolio showcasing various styles and angles. A visually rich profile builds trust and attracts more high-value clients.
