The three fixes on 80% of audited cafe profiles

  • Incomplete Business Description

    Many San Jose cafes are only using a fraction of the allowed 750 characters in their Google Business Profile description. You're likely missing out on prime real estate to highlight your unique ambiance, signature lattes, ethically sourced beans, or artisan pastries. A brief description means Google has less text to understand your specific offerings, making it harder for potential customers searching for precisely what you provide to find you.

  • Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Often, cafes in San Jose are only listed under 'Cafe' as their primary category. This overlooks the power of secondary categories which tell Google much more about your specialized services. I frequently find profiles neglecting essential additions like 'Coffee Shop', 'Brunch Restaurant', or 'Bakery'. Without these, you're invisible to customers specifically searching for those particular experiences, even if you offer them.

  • Too Few Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Your Google Business Profile should showcase your cafe's full experience, but many San Jose establishments have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. This often means missing shots of your inviting interior, popular menu items like avocado toast or specialty cold brews, your friendly baristas in action, or even the bustling exterior. Robust photo galleries boost engagement and show customers exactly what to expect before they even step inside, directly impacting their decision to visit.
