The three fixes on 80% of audited locksmith profiles

  • Underutilized Business Description

    Many Columbus locksmiths overlook their Google Business Profile's description, leaving it brief or generic, often well under the 750-character limit. This crucial section is an opportunity to detail your unique expertise – perhaps you specialize in antique lock repair, high-security system installations, or specific car key programming. A short description misses vital keywords and fails to convey your full value, making your service less appealing to a client in a lockout emergency who needs specific assurances right away.

  • Missing Key Secondary Categories

    Beyond the primary 'Locksmith' category, many Columbus profiles neglect powerful secondary categories that broaden visibility. Often, I find locksmiths missing critical classifications like 'Emergency Locksmith', 'Auto Locksmith', or 'Safe Opening Service'. These specific additions help Google understand the full scope of your services, ensuring you appear in searches for urgent car lockouts, forgotten safe combinations, or late-night home emergencies, rather than just general locksmith inquiries.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    A surprising number of Columbus locksmith profiles feature fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, a significant missed opportunity. Customers want to see proof of work and professionalism. Missing are images of your team in uniform, your service van, specific tools for safe cracking or key cutting, diverse lock installations, and before/after shots of repairs. Without these, your profile appears less trustworthy and engaging, reducing the likelihood of a potential client choosing your service over a competitor with a richer visual story.
