Best Google Business categories for a law firm

Mastering your Google Business Profile (GBP) categories is paramount for any law firm aiming to dominate local search results. Think of each category, primary and secondary, as a distinct ranking lane, allowing your firm to appear for a wider array of relevant client searches. Many law firms mistakenly set only a single primary category, severely limiting their visibility.

This common oversight means firms are leaving up to nine additional secondary category "lanes" completely vacant. By strategically utilizing all available category slots, you effectively tell Google the full spectrum of legal services you provide, exponentially increasing your chances of ranking for specialized search terms that potential clients are actively using to find legal representation.

The three fixes on 80% of audited law firm profiles

Law firm

The optimal primary category for most general practice or multi-specialty law firms is "Law firm." While "Attorney" is a valid alternative, "Law firm" more accurately represents the business entity itself and broadly encompasses a team of legal professionals, rather than a single practitioner. This primary choice allows your firm to rank for generic searches like "law firms near me" while providing a strong foundation for more specific secondary categories to build upon, ensuring wide-ranging visibility.

Why these matter specifically for law firms in Categories

  1. Personal injury attorney: This unlocks searches for clients seeking representation after accidents, such as "car accident lawyer" or "slip and fall attorney."
  2. Family law attorney: Crucial for appearing in results related to divorce, child custody, alimony, and other domestic legal matters.
  3. Estate planning attorney: Catches searches from individuals needing assistance with wills, trusts, probate, and powers of attorney.
  4. Criminal defense attorney: Essential for attracting clients searching for legal help with charges like DUI, drug offenses, or assault.
  5. Bankruptcy attorney: Enables ranking for searches from individuals or businesses facing financial distress and considering Chapter 7 or Chapter 13.
  6. Real estate attorney: Targets clients requiring legal support for property transactions, landlord-tenant disputes, or property line issues.
  7. Immigration attorney: Positions your firm for searches related to visas, green cards, citizenship, or deportation defense.
  8. Corporate lawyer: Attracts business clients seeking legal advice on formation, contracts, mergers, or regulatory compliance.
  9. Civil litigation attorney: Broadly covers a wide range of non-criminal disputes, from contract breaches to personal disputes not covered by personal injury.
