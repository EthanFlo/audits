The three fixes on 80% of audited locksmith profiles

  1. Craft an Engaging Description: Write a compelling, keyword-rich description for your locksmith business. Include terms like "emergency locksmith," "auto lockout service," "safe opening," "residential locksmith," and specific service areas. Highlight what makes you unique, such as 24/7 availability, mobile service, or specialized security solutions.
  2. Select the Right Primary Category: Your primary category is crucial for visibility. Choose "Locksmith" as your main category. This tells Google exactly what service you provide and helps you appear in relevant local searches. Do not get creative or broad here; stick to the most direct option.
  3. Add Relevant Secondary Categories: Maximize your reach by adding specific secondary categories. Utilize options like "Emergency Locksmith," "Auto Locksmith," and "Safe Opening Service." These categories ensure you appear for more niche, high-intent searches from potential customers.
  4. Detail Your Services List: Populate the services section with an exhaustive list of every offering. Include "key duplication," "lock repair," "rekeying," "smart lock installation," "commercial security solutions," and "eviction services." Provide brief, keyword-optimized descriptions for each service to enhance searchability.
  5. Upload High-Quality Photos and Videos: Showcase your work and professionalism with a diverse set of photos. Include exterior shots of your shop and service vehicles, team photos in uniform, images of tools and equipment, and examples of completed jobs like new lock installations or repaired safes. Videos demonstrating services also boost engagement.
  6. Maintain a Consistent Google Posts Cadence: Publish new Google Posts weekly. Share updates on special offers, such as discounted key cutting or new smart lock systems. Post security tips, recent successful lockout stories, or introduce new team members. Always include a clear call to action, like "Call Now" or "Get Quote."
  7. Prioritize Review Velocity and Quality: Actively encourage customers to leave reviews immediately after service completion. Provide a direct link or QR code. Respond promptly and professionally to every review, both positive and negative. Genuine, frequent reviews significantly boost your profile's authority and ranking.
  8. Seed Your Q&A Section: Proactively answer common locksmith questions in your Q&A section. Ask and answer questions like "How much does a house lockout cost?" or "Do you install high-security locks?" This addresses customer concerns upfront, utilizes more keywords, and helps your profile appear for a wider range of queries.

Why these matter specifically for locksmiths in How To

  • **Neglecting to claim and verify your profile:** Many locksmiths miss out on optimization simply by not owning their listing. Claim and verify your Google Business Profile immediately.
  • **Using a P.O. Box instead of a physical service area:** Google penalizes businesses that don't display a legitimate service address or physical location. Always use your actual business address or accurately define your service radius.
  • **Ignoring customer reviews, especially negative ones:** Failing to respond makes your business appear unengaged and uncaring. Respond professionally to all reviews, turning potential negatives into opportunities to show excellent customer service.
  • **Inconsistent business information (NAP) across the web:** Google looks for uniformity in your Name, Address, and Phone number. Ensure your NAP is identical on your website, social media, and all online directories.
