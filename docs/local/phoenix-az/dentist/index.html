The three fixes on 80% of audited dentist profiles

  • Business Description Under 400 Characters

    Many Phoenix dental practices are using a Google Business Profile description that barely scratches the surface, often staying well below the 750-character limit. This means you're missing a vital opportunity to articulate your practice's unique selling points, advanced technologies, or patient-centric philosophy. A truncated description fails to convey the full spectrum of care you provide, from preventative to specialized treatments, leaving potential patients to guess what makes your practice stand out.

  • Missing Secondary Categories

    Your primary category might be "Dentist," but if you're not utilizing secondary categories, you're invisible to patients searching for specific services. I often find profiles omitting crucial classifications like "Cosmetic Dentist," "Pediatric Dentist," or "Emergency Dental Service." Neglecting these specific categories means you're not appearing in targeted searches, directly reducing the likelihood of connecting with patients seeking those exact services in Phoenix.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Many dental practices have sparse photo galleries, often with fewer than 30 owner-uploaded images. This means critical visual elements are missing from your Google Business Profile. Prospective patients in Phoenix want to see high-quality photos of your welcoming reception area, the state-of-the-art treatment rooms, your friendly team members, and even before-and-after smile transformations, which all build trust and familiarity before they even step foot in your office.
