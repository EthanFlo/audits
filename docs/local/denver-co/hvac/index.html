The three fixes on 80% of audited hvac contractor profiles

  • Underutilized Business Description (Under 400 Characters)

    Google allows up to 750 characters for your business description, yet many HVAC contractors in Denver leave theirs incredibly short. A concise description misses crucial opportunities to highlight your specialized services, certifications, and unique selling points like 24/7 emergency service or specific brands you install. This brief overview fails to inform potential customers about your comprehensive offerings, causing them to move on to a competitor who provides more detail upfront.

  • Missing Secondary Service Categories

    Many Denver HVAC profiles only list "HVAC Contractor" as their primary category, overlooking vital secondary options. Neglecting categories like "AC Repair," "Furnace Installation Service," and "Duct Cleaning Service" severely limits your visibility for specific, high-intent searches. Customers rarely search just for "HVAC"; they look for exact solutions. Without these precise categories, your business won't appear when locals search for the specific services they urgently need, funneling those calls elsewhere.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    A Google Business Profile with sparse imagery (under 30 owner-uploaded photos) looks unestablished and unappealing. Your Denver HVAC business needs to showcase more than just a logo. Missing are images of your skilled technicians in action, completed installations, your clean service vehicles, and professional team photos. These visuals build trust, demonstrate your capabilities, and help customers envision you solving their heating or cooling problems, which generic stock photos or a few basic shots simply cannot achieve.
