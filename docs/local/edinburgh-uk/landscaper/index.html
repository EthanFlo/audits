The three fixes on 80% of audited landscaper profiles

  • Incomplete Business Description

    Many Edinburgh landscapers leave their Google Business Profile description brief, missing out on crucial keyword opportunities. Google allows 750 characters, yet most use less than half. This limits your ability to showcase specialist services like bespoke garden design, Edinburgh stone wall repairs, or sustainable planting schemes, causing you to lose visibility to clients searching for detailed solutions.

  • Missing Key Service Categories

    Limiting your profile to just "Landscaper" means you're not appearing for specific searches. I frequently find landscapers missing vital secondary categories like "Lawn Care Service," "Hardscape Contractor," or "Tree Service." This prevents potential clients in Morningside or Stockbridge from finding you when they need a new patio, regular lawn maintenance, or professional tree pruning.

  • Lack of Engaging Project Photos

    Your Google Business Profile should be a visual portfolio. Many landscapers have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, often missing crucial 'before and after' shots, team-at-work photos, or detailed images of completed projects like decking installations or bespoke water features. Customers searching in areas like Portobello want to visualise your work before making contact.
