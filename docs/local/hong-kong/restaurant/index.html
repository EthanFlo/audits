The three fixes on 80% of audited restaurant profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many Hong Kong restaurants underutilize the Google Business Profile description. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet I often find profiles with only a few sentences, barely scratching 200 characters. This is a missed opportunity to highlight your unique cuisine, ambiance, signature dishes, or your chef's philosophy, especially important for stand-out restaurants in a crowded market.

  • You’re Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    A common oversight is only having a primary category like 'Restaurant.' However, Google allows multiple secondary categories which significantly boost discoverability for specific searches. For example, if you serve dim sum, you should also be listed as 'Cantonese Restaurant,' 'Dim Sum Restaurant,' or even 'Chinese Restaurant.' Without these, you're invisible to customers searching for precise dining experiences in Hong Kong.

  • Too Few Owner-Uploaded Photos

    In a visual city like Hong Kong, high-quality photos are essential. Many profiles have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded images, relying instead on customer contributions. I often find critical photos missing: inviting interior shots, mouth-watering close-ups of your signature dishes, your team at work, or even specific menu items that showcase your culinary artistry. Customers eat with their eyes first, especially when choosing a restaurant.

Why these matter specifically for restaurants in Hong Kong

Hong Kong's dining scene is incredibly dense. Every district, from Central to Tsim Sha Tsui, boasts hundreds of restaurants vying for attention. When your Google Business Profile lacks detail, specific categories, or enticing photos, you instantly blend into the background. A perfectly optimised profile isn't just an advantage here; it's a necessity to stand out amongst fierce local competition and ensure your unique offering gets noticed by discerning diners.

Diners in Hong Kong often have specific intentions and limited time. They’re not just looking for 'a restaurant'; they're searching for 'pizza near Causeway Bay,' 'vegan dim sum Wan Chai,' or 'rooftop bar Central.' If your profile's categories are vague or your description doesn't highlight your specialities, you won't appear in these crucial, highly-converting local searches. Optimised profiles guide these precise searches directly to your door.

Furthermore, Hong Kong's fast-paced culture means quick decisions. Customers often make dining choices while on the MTR or walking through a neighbourhood. High-quality, owner-uploaded photos act as instant visual menus and ambiance previews. Without compelling images of your signature dishes, inviting interiors, or unique dining experiences, potential patrons quickly swipe past, unable to visually connect with what you offer, leading to missed bookings.
