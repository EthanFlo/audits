The three fixes on 80% of audited restaurant profiles

  • Shortened Business Description

    Many Dallas restaurant profiles utilize a business description under 400 characters, significantly under Google’s 750-character limit. This means missing out on vital opportunities to highlight your unique culinary story, signature dishes, ambiance, or specific dietary accommodations. A truncated description fails to entice potential diners looking for that perfect experience, leaving them to guess what makes your establishment special.

  • Missing Secondary Categories

    Your Google Business Profile often lacks crucial secondary categories beyond just "Restaurant." For example, profiles should often include specific descriptors like "Italian Restaurant," "Sushi Restaurant," or "Steakhouse" if applicable. Neglecting these precise categories means your restaurant isn't appearing in highly targeted searches from customers specifically craving a certain type of cuisine, reducing your visibility to qualified leads.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Many restaurant profiles feature fewer than 30 high-quality, owner-uploaded photos. Diners want to see inviting interior shots, mouth-watering close-ups of signature dishes, photos of your exterior for easy navigation, and even happy customers enjoying the atmosphere. A profile lacking diverse, recent visuals fails to convey your restaurant's true appeal, making it harder to attract new guests who often make dining decisions visually.
