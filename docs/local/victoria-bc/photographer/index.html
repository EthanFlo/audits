The three fixes on 80% of audited photographer profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many photographers in Victoria, BC miss a huge opportunity by only using a fraction of the 750 characters Google allows for their business description. This space is vital for showcasing your unique style, niche (e.g., fine art portraits, adventurous elopements, commercial branding), and brand story. A detailed description helps search engines understand your specific offerings, making you more discoverable for nuanced local searches and helping potential clients immediately connect with your vision.

  • Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Failing to add secondary categories severely limits who sees your profile. Beyond "Photographer," your Google Business Profile should precisely reflect your specializations. For Victoria, BC photographers, this often means adding categories like "Wedding Photographer," "Portrait Studio," and "Real Estate Photography." These specific tags tell Google exactly what services you offer, ensuring you appear in highly relevant searches from clients seeking those particular types of photography.

  • Insufficient Portfolio Photos

    A photographer's business is inherently visual, yet many profiles feature fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. Beyond just "best shots," you should include diverse examples of client work, studio space (if applicable), behind-the-scenes moments, team headshots, and event candids. This rich visual content builds trust, demonstrates professionalism, and allows potential Victoria, BC clients to visualize working with you and understand the full scope of your photography services.
