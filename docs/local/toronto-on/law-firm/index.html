The three fixes on 80% of audited law firm profiles

  • Underutilized Business Description

    Many Toronto law firms use a generic or overly brief business description on their Google Business Profile, often falling well under the generous 750-character limit. This crucial section is frequently treated as an afterthought, missing the opportunity to highlight your firm’s specific practice areas, unique selling propositions, and commitment to clients in the Toronto area. A descriptive, keyword-rich profile can significantly boost your visibility for relevant searches.

  • Neglecting Crucial Secondary Categories

    While most law firms correctly select "Law Firm" or "Lawyer" as their primary category, a common oversight is failing to add relevant secondary categories. For a Toronto law firm, this means missing out on targeted searches for specific legal needs. We often find profiles without essential additions like "Personal Injury Lawyer," "Family Law Attorney," "Estate Planning Lawyer," or "Real Estate Attorney," which directly connect you with clients seeking those precise services.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Many law firm profiles in Toronto feature fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, a significant missed opportunity for engagement and trust-building. Potential clients want to see more than just a logo. Firms often lack high-quality images of their office exterior and interior, the legal team in professional settings, and actual consultation spaces. These visuals build credibility and help clients feel more comfortable before even making contact.

In a city as large and diverse as Toronto, ON, the sheer volume of law firms creates an incredibly dense competitive landscape. Customer search behavior for legal services is unique; individuals are often in distress or facing significant life events, leading to highly specific, urgent searches. They rarely browse page after page. They look for immediate, trustworthy results from firms that clearly articulate their expertise in specific legal areas. An incomplete or unoptimized profile signals a lack of professionalism, pushing these clients toward more visible, seemingly credible options.
