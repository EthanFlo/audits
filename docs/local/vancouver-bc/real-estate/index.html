The three fixes on 80% of audited real estate agent profiles

  • Underutilized Business Description

    Many Vancouver real estate agents miss a critical opportunity by leaving their Google Business Profile description short, often under 400 characters. Google allows up to 750 characters, a prime space to detail your hyper-local expertise, specific neighbourhood focus areas like Kitsilano or Yaletown, and unique selling propositions. A concise description fails to inform potential clients about what truly sets you apart from the dozens of other agents they might consider.

  • Missing Essential Secondary Categories

    Beyond "Real Estate Agent," many profiles lack crucial secondary categories that boost visibility for specific services. I frequently find agents neglecting to add "Buyer's Agent," "Seller's Agent," and "Property Manager." Without these, you are not appearing in searches for the precise services your clients are actively seeking, significantly narrowing your reach and allowing competitors with fully categorized profiles to dominate relevant local queries.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    A real estate agent's Google Business Profile should showcase at least 30 high-quality, owner-uploaded photos. Profiles often fall short, missing images of the agent themselves, client testimonials (with permission), local landmarks they serve, successful transaction photos (exterior home shots), and community involvement. These visual assets build trust and rapport, giving potential clients a tangible sense of your professionalism and connection to the Vancouver market.
