The three fixes on 80% of audited locksmith profiles

  • Your Google Business Profile Description is Too Short

    Many Kansas City locksmiths miss a crucial opportunity by using only a few sentences in their GBP description. With a 750-character limit, you could be detailing your 24/7 emergency services, specialty in smart lock installations, or specific commercial door solutions across areas like Overland Park or downtown KC. A sparse description leaves potential customers guessing about your full capabilities.

  • You're Missing Key Secondary Categories

    Google allows you to select up to nine categories beyond just "Locksmith." Many profiles in the Kansas City area only use the primary, severely limiting their visibility for specific services. I often find profiles lacking essential categories like "Emergency Locksmith," "Auto Locksmith," or "Safe Opening Service," which are critical for capturing targeted local searches.

  • Your Profile Lacks Sufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    A Google Business Profile with under 30 owner-uploaded photos tells me you're not fully showcasing your professional image. Customers searching for a locksmith in Kansas City want to see your branded service vehicles, technicians in uniform, specific tools, and examples of different locks or security systems you work with. Missing these visual proofs of expertise can deter potential clients.
