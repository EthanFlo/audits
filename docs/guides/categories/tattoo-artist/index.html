Best Google Business categories for a tattoo artist

For a tattoo artist, optimizing your Google Business Profile (GBP) is crucial, yet many overlook a powerful ranking tool: secondary categories. While most studios correctly identify their primary category, they stop there, leaving up to nine additional category slots completely empty. Each category you select acts as a unique ranking lane, allowing your business to appear in search results for a wider array of relevant queries beyond just your core service.

By strategically filling these secondary categories, you transform your single ranking lane into a multi-lane highway. This expanded presence ensures your studio is discoverable by potential clients searching for specific styles, related services, or even the artistic aspects of your business, dramatically increasing your visibility and potential customer base, whether they seek a fine line, traditional, or cover-up specialist.

The three fixes on 80% of audited tattoo artist profiles

### Tattoo shop The optimal primary Google Business category for a tattoo artist is "Tattoo shop." This term is universally understood by potential clients searching for tattoo services and directly aligns with Google's most relevant business classification. While "Tattoo studio" might seem synonymous, "Tattoo shop" often carries a slightly broader commercial connotation that Google's algorithm seems to favor for general searches. Selecting this specific term ensures your business immediately signals its core offering to both Google and its users, setting the foundational context for all subsequent category selections.

Why these matter specifically for tattoo artists in Categories

  1. Body piercing service: This unlocks searches for related body modification services like "piercings near me" or "ear piercing," appealing to a similar clientele often found in tattoo studios.
  2. Permanent make-up clinic: Captures searches for cosmetic tattooing procedures such as "microblading," "permanent eyeliner," and "lip blushing," broadening your reach to the beauty segment.
  3. Art studio: Positions the business as a creative space, drawing searches for "custom art commissions," "local artist studio," or "unique art," emphasizing the artistic skill involved in all tattoo styles.
  4. Art gallery: If the studio regularly exhibits and sells artist work or prints, this category attracts searches like "local art exhibits" or "buy original art," highlighting the creative output beyond tattoos.
  5. Graphic designer: For artists offering design services beyond skin, this unlocks searches for "custom logo design," "illustration services," or "branding consultation," showcasing diverse artistic capabilities relevant to custom tattoo requests.
  6. Custom t-shirt store: If the artist sells their own branded apparel or custom-designed merchandise, this captures searches for "custom printed shirts" or "artist merchandise," adding a retail dimension.
  7. Clothing store: This implies selling ready-to-wear clothing, including branded merchandise or artist-designed apparel, broadening appeal for local fashion searches among those seeking unique styles.
  8. Gift shop: For studios selling unique, art-related gifts, prints, or branded merchandise, this attracts searches for "unique gifts" or "local souvenirs," tapping into casual shoppers.
  9. Event venue: If the studio occasionally hosts art shows, pop-ups, or workshops, this category can attract searches for "local events" or "art workshops," positioning the space as a community hub.
