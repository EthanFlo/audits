30-point Google Business Profile checklist for hair salons

When it comes to enhancing a hair salon's online presence, general advice often falls short. Telling a salon owner to "optimize their GBP" is vague and provides little actionable direction. What's truly needed is a precise, step-by-step roadmap – a specific sequence of tasks that can be completed without guesswork. This focused approach ensures every critical element is addressed, transforming abstract suggestions into concrete improvements that directly impact local search visibility and client acquisition.

This 30-point Google Business Profile checklist is designed as the exact sequence a hair salon owner or manager can work through on a slow afternoon. Rather than being overwhelmed by the broad concept of "online marketing," you can systematically tick off each item, knowing that every completed step directly contributes to a more robust, attractive, and high-ranking profile. Get ready to turn quiet hours into productive steps towards a thriving online presence.

The three fixes on 80% of audited hair salon profiles

Business Info

  • ☐ Verify your salon's name, address, and phone number (NAP) are precisely accurate and consistent across all platforms.
  • ☐ Ensure your primary website URL is correctly linked, directing potential clients to your main salon page.
  • ☐ Update your salon's operating hours, including any special holiday hours or temporary closures, for accuracy.
  • ☐ Craft a compelling business description (~750 characters) using keywords like "haircut," "color specialist," "bridal hair," and "men's barber."
  • ☐ Add or update a direct appointment booking link, streamlining the process for clients to schedule services.

Categories

  • ☐ Select "Hair Salon" as your primary Google Business Profile category to accurately represent your core business.
  • ☐ Add "Hairdresser" as a secondary category to capture broader searches for styling and cutting services.
  • ☐ Include "Barber Shop" if you offer specialized men's haircutting and grooming services to attract male clientele.
  • ☐ Add "Beauty Salon" or "Nail Salon" if your establishment offers a wider range of beauty treatments.
  • ☐ Consider adding "Wedding Hairstyle Service" if bridal styling is a significant part of your salon's offerings.

Photos

  • ☐ Upload a high-resolution, inviting exterior photo that clearly shows your salon's signage and entrance.
  • ☐ Add multiple high-quality interior photos showcasing your salon's clean, stylish, and welcoming ambiance.
  • ☐ Include professional photos of your staff in action, demonstrating expertise and friendly client interaction.
  • ☐ Showcase stunning before-and-after client photos, particularly highlighting your Color Specialist's work (with client consent).
  • ☐ Upload diverse photos of finished styles, including modern Men's Barber cuts and intricate Bridal updos.

Services

  • ☐ List all core services like "Haircuts," "Coloring," "Styling," and "Blowouts" with brief descriptions.
  • ☐ Detail specialized services such as "Balayage," "Highlights," "Ombre," and "Keratin Treatments," specifying benefits.
  • ☐ Clearly list men's specific services, including "Men's Haircuts," "Beard Trims," and "Shaves" (if applicable).
  • ☐ Create distinct entries for "Bridal Hair Styling," "Bridesmaid Hair," and "Bridal Consultation" with package details.
  • ☐ Provide pricing information for each service, or a clear "Call for Consultation" for complex or custom services.

Reviews

  • ☐ Respond promptly and professionally to every single review, whether positive or negative, showing active engagement.
  • ☐ Develop a strategy to politely encourage satisfied clients to leave reviews after their service, perhaps with a small card.
  • ☐ Address any negative feedback constructively, offering solutions or inviting dialogue offline to resolve issues.
  • ☐ Share positive reviews on your salon's social media channels and website (with permission) to build trust.
  • ☐ Monitor new reviews at least weekly to maintain a strong reputation and address any emerging concerns quickly.

Posts

  • ☐ Create a "What's New" post announcing new stylists, salon upgrades, or product lines carried.
  • ☐ Publish an "Offer" post promoting a limited-time discount on a popular service, like a "Color Specialist Package."
  • ☐ Use an "Event" post to highlight a bridal styling open house, a men's grooming workshop, or a special charity event.
  • ☐ Generate a "Product" post featuring a specific hair care product, explaining its benefits and how to use it.
  • ☐ Write an "Update" post about seasonal hair trends, safety protocols, or changes to salon operations.

Why these matter specifically for hair salons in Checklist

After meticulously working through all 30 points on this checklist, the work isn't quite done – it's just beginning. The next crucial step is to leverage Google Business Profile Insights to monitor the impact of your optimizations. Pay close attention to changes in search queries, views, website clicks, and direction requests over the next 14 days. This allows the changes to be indexed and for search engine algorithms to reflect your enhanced profile. After this initial monitoring period, analyze what worked best, identify areas that could still be improved, and then repeat the cycle of review and refinement. Consistent maintenance and adaptation are key to sustained online visibility and attracting more clients to your hair salon.
