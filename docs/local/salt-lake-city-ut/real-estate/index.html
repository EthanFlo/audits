The three fixes on 80% of audited real estate agent profiles

  • Your Google Business Profile Description is Too Short

    Many Salt Lake City real estate agents don't realize Google Business Profiles allow up to 750 characters for their business description, yet most use less than 400. This is a massive missed opportunity to showcase your specialties—like first-time home buyers in Sugar House, luxury properties in The Avenues, or investment opportunities—and integrate crucial keywords that help local clients find your specific services.

  • Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Your Google Business Profile might only list "Real Estate Agent" as its primary category. However, neglecting secondary categories severely limits your visibility for specific searches. We often find agents missing vital options like "Buyer's Agent," "Seller's Agent," and "Property Manager." Adding these precise categories ensures you appear in more targeted searches, directly connecting you with clients looking for your exact services in Salt Lake City.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Google Business Profiles with fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos often underperform. For Salt Lake City real estate agents, this means missing out on building immediate trust and connection. Crucial photos often absent include professional agent headshots, team photos, images of local Salt Lake City landmarks with a 'sold' sign, interior/exterior shots of local listings, and your welcoming office space, all of which convey professionalism and local expertise.
