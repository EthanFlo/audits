The three fixes on 80% of audited veterinarian profiles

  • Underutilised Business Description

    Many Leeds veterinary practices are missing a golden opportunity by using a description under Google's 750-character limit, often falling below 400. This crucial text should detail your practice's unique offerings, specialisations like feline-friendly clinics, exotic pet care, or advanced diagnostics, and your team's compassionate approach. A detailed description helps potential clients understand your services immediately and feel more connected to your practice before they even call.

  • Incomplete Service Category Listings

    One common oversight is failing to add all relevant secondary categories to your Google Business Profile. While "Veterinarian" is the primary, many practices miss vital additions. For veterinarians in Leeds, I often find missing categories like "Emergency Vet," "Animal Hospital," and "Pet Surgery," which are critical for capturing specific, high-intent searches. Expanding these categories ensures you appear for a wider range of services you actually offer.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Google Business Profiles with fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos often lag behind competitors. I frequently see Leeds vets underutilising this feature. You might be missing images of your friendly reception area, examination rooms, surgical suites, or dedicated staff interacting with pets. Showing your clean facilities, modern equipment, and the warm environment of your clinic builds trust and helps pet owners visualise their visit before they step through your door.
