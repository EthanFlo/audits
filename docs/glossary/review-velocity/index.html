Review Velocity

Review velocity refers to the rate at which a local business consistently acquires new online reviews over a specific period. It is not merely about the total number of reviews a business has, but rather the frequency and regularity with which fresh feedback is posted, reflecting ongoing customer engagement and recent experiences. A high review velocity indicates that customers are actively and consistently sharing their opinions, providing a continuous stream of up-to-date information about the business. This metric is critically important for a local business's visibility in Google Business Profile (GBP) rankings. Google's algorithm favors businesses that demonstrate ongoing activity and relevance, and a steady influx of new reviews signals that a business is active, popular, and providing current value to its customers. Businesses with strong review velocity often see improved local search rankings, as Google interprets this consistent feedback as a reliable indicator of quality and trustworthiness. The stakes are high: better ranking means more visibility in map packs and local searches, leading directly to increased website visits, phone calls, and foot traffic for brick-and-mortar operations. Consider a Chicago dentist's office. Dentist A has accumulated 300 reviews over ten years, but only two new reviews arrived in the last six months. Dentist B, on the other hand, has 200 reviews in total, but consistently receives 5-10 new reviews every month. Even though Dentist A has a higher total review count, Dentist B possesses significantly better review velocity. Google's algorithm will likely prioritize Dentist B in local search results because the ongoing stream of fresh feedback indicates a more currently relevant and actively preferred practice, reassuring potential new patients about the quality of recent experiences. One common mistake businesses make is focusing solely on the sheer volume of reviews, neglecting the recency aspect. Many assume that having thousands of reviews, even if dated, is enough. However, a large volume of old reviews without recent additions can actually signal stagnation or a decline in customer engagement to search engines. Another misconception is that aggressive, one-time pushes for reviews will yield lasting results. While a burst of reviews can help, consistent effort over time is far more effective than sporadic campaigns for maintaining healthy review velocity. To foster strong review velocity, businesses should implement a continuous strategy:
  • Simplify the process: Make it incredibly easy for customers to leave reviews, using direct links or QR codes.
  • Ask at the right time: Politely request reviews when customers are most satisfied, such as immediately after a positive service experience or successful purchase.
  • Automate follow-ups: Use email or SMS follow-ups for customers who have recently interacted with your business.
  • Respond to all reviews: Actively engaging with both positive and negative feedback encourages more customers to share their experiences.
  • Train your staff: Ensure your team understands the importance of reviews and knows how to respectfully encourage customers to leave feedback.
For businesses seeking to understand their online presence more deeply, related terms to explore include "Review Recency" (the age of the most recent reviews), "Review Volume" (the total number of reviews), and "Online Reputation Management" (the broader strategy of monitoring and influencing public perception of a business online).
