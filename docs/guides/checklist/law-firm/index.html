30-point Google Business Profile checklist for law firms

In the dynamic digital marketing landscape, general advice often falls short when tangible results are the goal. For law firms, where every potential client search matters, a precise, step-by-step checklist is invaluable. It removes ambiguity, provides a clear roadmap, and ensures no critical optimization step is overlooked, turning vague recommendations into concrete actions that drive local visibility.

This 30-point Google Business Profile checklist is crafted to be your exact sequence. Dedicate a focused afternoon to methodically work through each item. By checking off these specific actions, your firm will systematically enhance its local search presence, directly connecting with clients actively seeking Personal Injury, Family Law, or Estate Planning legal services in your area.

The three fixes on 80% of audited law firm profiles

Business Info

  • ☐ Ensure your primary business name accurately reflects your firm's legal entity and matches state bar records.
  • ☐ Verify your physical address is precise and consistent across all online directories for local SEO.
  • ☐ Update your main phone number to a direct line answered by your firm, not a general answering service.
  • ☐ Set precise operating hours, including any holiday closures or specific availability for consultations.
  • ☐ Write a compelling business description highlighting specialties like Personal Injury or Estate Planning, using relevant keywords.

Categories

  • ☐ Select "Law Firm" as your primary category to ensure broad legal service classification.
  • ☐ Add secondary categories like "Personal Injury Attorney" or "Family Law Attorney" to specify expertise.
  • ☐ Include "Estate Planning Attorney" if applicable, to capture specific client searches.
  • ☐ Review all selected categories annually to ensure they still accurately reflect your current legal services.
  • ☐ Avoid overly broad or irrelevant categories that do not directly pertain to your firm's practice areas.

Photos

  • ☐ Upload a high-resolution exterior photo of your building, making it easy for clients to locate your office.
  • ☐ Add several interior photos showcasing reception areas, meeting rooms, and professional office spaces.
  • ☐ Include professional headshots of all attorneys, enhancing trust and personal connection with potential clients.
  • ☐ Post photos of your team working or engaging in community events to humanize your law firm brand.
  • ☐ Ensure all uploaded photos are well-lit, professional, and represent your firm's established brand image.

Services

  • ☐ List all primary legal services offered, such as "Personal Injury Representation" with brief descriptions.
  • ☐ Create separate service entries for specific areas like "Divorce & Child Custody" under Family Law.
  • ☐ Detail services like "Wills & Trusts" or "Probate Administration" for Estate Planning clients.
  • ☐ Include pricing models or consultation fees if applicable, or state "Contact for consultation."
  • ☐ Regularly update service descriptions to reflect new practice areas or changes in legal offerings.

Reviews

  • ☐ Implement a polite, automated system to request reviews from satisfied clients immediately after case conclusion.
  • ☐ Respond professionally and promptly to every review, both positive and negative, demonstrating client care.
  • ☐ Actively solicit reviews from former clients who had positive experiences, providing a direct link.
  • ☐ Never offer incentives for reviews, as this violates Google's policies and can lead to penalties.
  • ☐ Report any clearly fake or spam reviews to Google Business Profile support for removal.

Posts

  • ☐ Publish weekly "Update" posts about recent case successes, firm news, or community involvement.
  • ☐ Create "Offer" posts for limited-time free consultations or special legal advice seminars.
  • ☐ Utilize "Event" posts to promote upcoming webinars, legal clinics, or public speaking engagements.
  • ☐ Share "Product" posts for specific legal services or downloadable guides relevant to client needs.
  • ☐ Ensure all posts include a relevant image/video and a clear call-to-action button, like "Call Now."

Why these matter specifically for law firms in Checklist

Once all 30 items are meticulously checked off, your work isn't entirely done – but the heavy lifting is. The next crucial step is observation and iteration. Allow approximately 14 days for Google to fully index and reflect these comprehensive changes across its search ecosystem. Following this period, dive deep into your Google Business Profile Insights. Analyze key metrics such as views, searches, and actions taken (calls, website visits, direction requests) to understand the impact of your efforts. Use this data to identify areas for further refinement, and then confidently repeat the process, always striving for peak local visibility.
