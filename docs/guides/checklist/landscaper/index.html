30-point Google Business Profile checklist for landscapers

Navigating the digital landscape for your landscaping business can feel overwhelming, with general advice often leaving you more confused than clear. This isn't another vague guide; it's a precise, actionable checklist designed to transform your Google Business Profile (GBP) into a powerful lead-generation tool. Instead of wondering what to do, you'll have 30 specific, measurable tasks.

Imagine a slow Tuesday afternoon when the rain has cleared, but new work hasn't started yet. This checklist provides the exact sequence of steps you should undertake. Each point is a concrete action tailored for landscapers, from perfecting your service categories to showcasing your latest hardscaping marvels. Work through these items one by one, and watch your local visibility soar.

The three fixes on 80% of audited landscaper profiles

Business Info

  • ☐ Verify your exact business name matches your official registration documents.
  • ☐ Ensure consistent physical address details (street, suite, zip) across all platforms.
  • ☐ Confirm your primary phone number is active, local, and regularly monitored.
  • ☐ Update your website URL to the current, mobile-friendly version of your site.
  • ☐ Set accurate service hours, including any seasonal adjustments for landscaping work.

Categories

  • ☐ Select "Landscaper" as your primary business category to define your core service.
  • ☐ Add "Lawn care service" as a secondary category to capture relevant searches.
  • ☐ Include "Tree service" if your business offers tree trimming, removal, or care.
  • ☐ Consider "Hardscape contractor" for services like patios, walkways, or retaining walls.
  • ☐ Add "Irrigation system supplier" if you install or maintain sprinkler systems.

Photos

  • ☐ Upload a high-resolution version of your company logo as your profile photo.
  • ☐ Add a compelling cover photo showcasing a signature landscaping project or your team.
  • ☐ Include at least five recent, high-quality photos of completed lawn care transformations.
  • ☐ Feature five clear images of your best hardscaping projects, like patios or outdoor kitchens.
  • ☐ Post five photos of your team actively working on-site, demonstrating professionalism and safety.

Services

  • ☐ List "Lawn Mowing" with a detailed description outlining edges, trimming, and cleanup.
  • ☐ Add "Tree Trimming & Removal" specifying expertise in safety and proper equipment.
  • ☐ Define "Hardscape Installation" clearly, mentioning materials like pavers, stone, or concrete.
  • ☐ Include "Seasonal Cleanup" detailing tasks like fall leaf removal or spring garden bed preparation.
  • ☐ List "Irrigation System Repair & Installation" with notes on water efficiency and maintenance.

Reviews

  • ☐ Respond promptly and professionally to every existing customer review, positive or negative.
  • ☐ Implement a consistent strategy to politely request new reviews from satisfied clients.
  • ☐ Address any negative feedback constructively, offering a clear path to resolution offline.
  • ☐ When responding, highlight specific services like "excellent lawn care" to reinforce keywords.
  • ☐ Encourage clients to mention the specific project or service (e.g., "patio installation") in their review.

Posts

  • ☐ Create a "What's New" post announcing your seasonal lawn care packages or specials.
  • ☐ Share an "Offer" post, such as a discount for new clients on their first tree service.
  • ☐ Post an "Event" about any local garden show participation or community beautification projects.
  • ☐ Create a "Product" post featuring a new line of sustainable landscaping materials you offer.
  • ☐ Share a "Service" post detailing your expertise in eco-friendly or drought-tolerant landscaping solutions.

Why these matter specifically for landscapers in Checklist

Once all 30 points on this checklist are meticulously addressed, your Google Business Profile will be significantly optimized. Don't stop there; bookmark your GBP Insights and monitor your performance metrics for the next 14 days. Observe changes in views, website clicks, and phone calls. After two weeks, revisit your profile, check for any new reviews or information updates, and consider repeating this checklist, refining where necessary, to maintain peak local visibility.
