Google Business Profile Seasonal Hours Not Updating: The Real Fix

By Ethan Florendo · 2026-04-20

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is often the first interaction a potential customer has with your business. It's how they find you, how they call you, and crucially, how they know when you're open. When your seasonal or special hours don't update correctly, it's not just an inconvenience, it's a direct hit to your bottom line, causing lost sales, frustrated customers, and a ding to your local search rankings.

You’ve tried updating your hours in your Google Business Profile dashboard. You’ve re-entered them, you’ve double-checked, and yet, Google stubbornly shows the old hours, or worse, incorrect hours, to your customers. It's maddening, and you're not alone. Many small business owners face this exact problem, feeling helpless against Google's mysterious algorithms.

As someone who helps local businesses navigate the complexities of Google, I've seen this issue countless times. There's a specific set of steps and understandings that can usually resolve it. This isn't about some secret trick; it's about understanding how Google processes information and what actions truly trigger an update. Let's dig in.

Understanding the Problem: Why Your Hours Are Stuck

Before we jump into the fix, let's understand why your hours might be getting stuck. It's not always a glitch; sometimes, it's a misunderstanding of how Google categorizes and prioritizes different types of hour information.

The Hierarchy of Hours on Google Business Profile

Google Business Profile generally recognizes a few different types of hours, and their interplay can be confusing:

  • Standard Hours: These are your regular, day-to-day operating hours, like "Monday - Friday, 9 AM - 5 PM." These are your default.
  • Special Hours: These are for specific dates when your hours deviate from your standard hours, typically for holidays (Christmas, New Year's Day, Thanksgiving) or special events. Google specifically prompts you to set these for common holidays.
  • More Hours (Optional): This section allows you to specify hours for particular services, such as "Senior Hours," "Delivery Hours," "Brunch Hours," or "Pickup Hours." These are supplementary and usually don't interfere with your primary open/closed status.

The problem often arises when you update Special Hours, but Google either fails to revert to your Standard Hours afterwards, or it gets confused by overlapping entries or past errors. Google's system is designed to prioritize the most specific, timely information, but if that information isn't perfectly clear or conflicts with other data, it can default to older, seemingly more stable information, which is often your old Standard Hours or even community-contributed data.

Common Pitfalls Leading to Stalled Updates

Several scenarios often lead to your hours not updating correctly:

  1. Forgetting to Add "Special Hours" for Specific Dates: Many business owners simply change their "Standard Hours" for a holiday period and then change them back. This is a common mistake. You should ideally leave your Standard Hours untouched and use "Special Hours" for one-off changes.
  2. Overlapping or Conflicting Special Hours: If you've entered Special Hours for a range of dates, and then enter another Special Hours entry that overlaps, Google might get confused about which set of hours to display.
  3. Relying on Google to "Know" Holidays: While Google often prompts you for major holidays, it won't automatically apply "closed" status. You need to explicitly state your hours for each holiday.
  4. Google's Internal Processing Delays: Sometimes, even after you hit save, it can take a few minutes, hours, or in rare cases, even a day for the changes to propagate across Google's various services (Search, Maps, Knowledge Panel).
  5. External Data Overrides: Google cross-references your information with other sources on the web, including your website, social media profiles, and other business directories (like Yelp, TripAdvisor, industry-specific listings). If there's conflicting information, especially from what Google considers an authoritative source, it might override your GBP settings.
  6. Community Edits: Anyone can "Suggest an edit" on your Google Business Profile. While these suggestions require verification, a sufficiently popular suggestion (e.g., from multiple local guides or users) can sometimes push through incorrect information.

Understanding these points is the first step. Now, let's get to the actionable solution.

The Immediate Fix: How to Force an Update

When your Google Business Profile hours are stuck, the most effective solution often involves a bit more than just re-entering the special hours. You need to reset the system's understanding of your base hours, and then layered on your special hours. This isn't just about editing, it's about explicitly re-confirming your primary data.

Step-by-Step Guide to Forcing an Update

Follow these steps precisely from your Google Business Profile dashboard:

  1. Log In to Your Google Business Profile: Go to business.google.com and select the business location you want to edit.
  2. Navigate to the "Info" Section: In the left-hand menu, click on "Info." This section contains all your core business details, including hours.
  3. Locate the "Hours" Section: You'll see your standard operating hours listed.
  4. CRITICAL STEP: Re-enter Your Standard Hours: This is the key. Even if your standard hours appear correct, you need to explicitly re-save them.
    • Click on the pencil icon (edit) next to your hours.
    • For each day of the week, uncheck and then re-check the box next to your operating days.
    • Even if the times are correct, click into the time fields for each day and re-select or re-type the exact opening and closing times. Make sure there are no typos or accidental spaces.
    • If you are closed on a particular day, ensure the box for that day remains unchecked.
    • Important: Do this for *all* operating days. This action forces Google to re-evaluate and re-ingest your primary operating schedule.
  5. Save Your Standard Hours: Click "Apply" or "Save" (the button name can vary slightly) at the bottom of the hours editor. Wait for the changes to process. You might see a "Pending review" message briefly.
  6. Add or Re-confirm Special Hours (if applicable): If you have specific upcoming seasonal or holiday hours that deviate from your newly confirmed standard hours, now is the time to add them.
    • Immediately after saving your standard hours, go back to the "Hours" section within "Info."
    • Look for the "Add special hours" option (it might be under "More hours" or directly below your standard hours).
    • Click on the date for the special hours you need to set. Google often pre-populates common holidays. If not, select the specific date from the calendar.
    • Enter the exact opening and closing times for that special day. If you are closed, select "Closed."
    • Crucial: Ensure these special hours are only for the specific dates you intend. Do not apply them for extended periods that should be covered by your standard hours.
  7. Save Your Special Hours: Click "Apply" or "Save" for these special hours.
  8. Check Your Public Profile: Open a private or incognito browser window and search for your business by name and city. Check your Knowledge Panel (the box on the right of search results) and your listing on Google Maps. It might take anywhere from a few minutes to a few hours for the changes to fully reflect. If after an hour, it's still incorrect, try repeating steps 4-5.

This method works because re-entering and saving your standard hours, even if they appear correct, triggers a deeper re-indexing process within Google's system, essentially telling it, "This is my definitive base schedule." Any subsequent special hours you add then layer on top of this newly confirmed foundation.

Concrete Example 1: Sarah's Salon Holiday Mix-up

Sarah owns "Sarah's Salon" in Anytown, CA. Her standard hours are Tuesday-Saturday, 9 AM - 6 PM, closed Sunday and Monday. For the Christmas and New Year's season, she had several changes:

  • December 24th (Christmas Eve): Open 9 AM - 2 PM
  • December 25th (Christmas Day): Closed
  • December 31st (New Year's Eve): Open 9 AM - 4 PM
  • January 1st (New Year's Day): Closed

Sarah went into her GBP and successfully added special hours for Dec 24th, 25th, and 31st. However, for January 1st, she initially just clicked "Closed." When January 2nd (a regular business day, Tuesday) rolled around, her Google Business Profile still showed "Closed." Customers were confused, and she lost several walk-ins. When she checked her GBP, her standard hours for Tuesday were still listed as 9 AM - 6 PM, but Google on the public profile just showed "Closed."

The Fix: Sarah followed the steps above. She went to her GBP "Info" section, clicked on "Hours." Even though Tuesday-Saturday, 9 AM - 6 PM, was displayed, she clicked the edit pencil. She then *unchecked* and *re-checked* the boxes for Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, and Saturday. For each day, she *re-selected* 9 AM and 6 PM for the opening and closing times. After doing this for all standard days and clicking "Apply," she then went to "Add special hours," found January 1st, and explicitly confirmed "Closed." Within 20 minutes, her profile for January 2nd onwards correctly displayed her standard 9 AM - 6 PM hours.

Deep Dive into Google's Verification Process

Understanding Google's verification process is crucial for long-term management of your GBP. Google doesn't just take your word for it; it cross-references and validates information from various sources to ensure accuracy for its users.

How Google Determines "Truth"

Google's goal is to provide the most accurate and helpful information possible. To do this, it employs a sophisticated system of checks and balances:

  • Authoritative Sources: Your own Google Business Profile is a primary source, but Google also highly values information directly from your official website, especially if you use structured data (Schema Markup) to define your business hours.
  • User Contributions: Google Maps users, especially Local Guides (users who contribute reviews, photos, and edits), can "Suggest an edit" to any business profile. If multiple reputable users suggest the same change, Google might consider it, even if it conflicts with your GBP settings.
  • Third-Party Data: Google gathers information from other business directories, social media profiles (like Facebook's business page), review sites (like Yelp or TripAdvisor), and data aggregators. Inconsistent information across these platforms can confuse Google and prevent updates from sticking.
  • Real-World Signals: Google may even use signals like Wi-Fi data, GPS location data, and popular times traffic data to infer operating hours, especially if there's conflicting information.

The Role of Business Information Consistency (NAP)

Consistency in your Name, Address, and Phone number (NAP) across the web is a cornerstone of local SEO. The same principle applies to your hours. If your website says you're open until 6 PM, but your Facebook page says 7 PM, and your Yelp listing says 5 PM, Google sees this as a red flag. When information is inconsistent, Google becomes less confident in any single source, including your own GBP edits, which can lead to delays or outright rejections of your updates.

Monitoring and Managing Community Edits

The "Suggest an edit" feature is a double-edged sword. It can help correct outdated information, but it can also introduce errors. You need to be vigilant:

  • Regularly Check Your Public Profile: Make it a habit to search for your business on Google Search and Maps regularly (e.g., once a week). Look for your hours, phone number, address, and website link.
  • Check for Pending Edits: Log into your Google Business Profile dashboard. Sometimes, Google will alert you to user-suggested edits that are awaiting your review.
  • Report Incorrect Information: If you find incorrect information on your public profile that you cannot fix through your dashboard (perhaps it's a persistent, old entry from a data aggregator), you can use the "Suggest an edit" option yourself to correct it, or use the "Send feedback" link usually found at the bottom of the Google search results page or Google Maps interface. Clearly state the incorrect information and provide proof (e.g., a link to your official website with correct hours).

Being proactive in monitoring and correcting your business information across the web reinforces to Google that your GBP is the most reliable source for your hours.

Proactive Strategies to Prevent Future Issues

The best fix is prevention. By adopting a disciplined approach to managing your Google Business Profile, you can significantly reduce the chances of your seasonal or special hours getting stuck again.

Best Practices for Managing Your GBP Hours

  1. Regular GBP Audits: Schedule a recurring task (e.g., once a month or quarterly) to review your entire Google Business Profile. Check all sections, not just hours. This ensures everything is up-to-date and accurate.
  2. Plan Special Hours in Advance: Google allows you to enter special hours for dates far into the future. As soon as you know your holiday schedule for the upcoming season, input those special hours. This sets the expectation with Google well in advance.
  3. Use "Special Hours" Exclusively for Deviations: Do not change your "Standard Hours" for temporary closures or adjusted schedules. Your standard hours should reflect your typical, unchanging weekly schedule. All deviations should be handled via the "Special Hours" function.
  4. Confirm "Closed" for Holidays: For any holiday you're closed, explicitly set "Closed" in the special hours. Don't assume Google will know. If you don't enter anything, it might default to your standard hours, leading to customer confusion.
  5. Keep Your Website Up-to-Date: Your website should always be the single most authoritative source for your business information, including hours. If your website has incorrect hours, Google might pull that data and override your GBP. Consider using Schema Markup for LocalBusiness on your website to explicitly tell Google your hours in a machine-readable format. This significantly boosts Google's confidence in your data.
  6. Educate Your Team: If multiple people have access to your GBP, ensure everyone understands the correct procedure for updating hours to prevent conflicting entries.

Concrete Example 2: The Daily Grind Coffee Shop's Summer Hours

"The Daily Grind" coffee shop in a popular tourist town wanted to extend its hours during the busy summer months, from Memorial Day weekend to Labor Day weekend. Their standard hours were 6 AM - 5 PM, Monday-Friday, and 7 AM - 4 PM on Saturday-Sunday. For the summer, they wanted to stay open an extra hour each day: 6 AM - 6 PM, Monday-Friday, and 7 AM - 5 PM on Saturday-Sunday.

Previously, the owner, Mark, would just go into his "Standard Hours" and change them in late May, and then change them back in September. This often led to issues where Google would sometimes revert to old hours prematurely or display an inconsistent mix of old and new hours, causing frustration for his customers and his baristas.

The Proactive Fix: Based on learnings, Mark changed his strategy. In early May, he logged into his Google Business Profile. He made sure his "Standard Hours" were correctly set to his year-round, non-summer hours (6 AM - 5 PM M-F, 7 AM - 4 PM S-S).

Then, he used the "Add special hours" feature. Instead of selecting individual holidays, he chose a date range: from May 27th (Memorial Day weekend start) to September 2nd (day after Labor Day). Within this range, for Monday through Friday, he entered 6 AM - 6 PM. For Saturday and Sunday, he entered 7 AM - 5 PM. He made sure to save these specific summer hours.

The beauty of this approach is that his "Standard Hours" remained untouched in the GBP settings. The "Special Hours" simply overlaid the summer schedule for the defined period. Once September 3rd arrived, Google automatically reverted to his original "Standard Hours" without any further action needed from Mark. This eliminated confusion, kept his profile accurate, and ensured a smooth transition for his customers.

Beyond the Profile: The Ecosystem of Local SEO

While your Google Business Profile is undeniably the most critical component for local search visibility, it doesn't exist in a vacuum. Google takes into account your entire online presence when determining your authority and accuracy. Thinking holistically about your online information amplifies the impact of your GBP efforts.

Your Website: The Ultimate Authority

As mentioned, your website is your digital storefront. Ensure your hours are prominently displayed on your contact page, footer, and ideally, on every page. Going a step further and implementing Schema Markup for Local Business tells Google in its own language exactly what your business is, where it is, and when it's open. This structured data is a powerful signal of authority and accuracy.

Social Media and Directory Listings

Think of every online platform where your business is listed as a potential source of truth for Google. This includes:

  • Facebook Business Page: Make sure your hours are identical to your GBP.
  • Yelp, TripAdvisor, Zocdoc (for dentists), Booksy (for salons), etc.: Any industry-specific or general review/directory site where you have a listing should mirror your GBP hours.
  • Apple Maps, Bing Places, Yellow Pages, etc.: These platforms also feed information to search engines.

The more consistent your NAP (Name, Address, Phone) and hours are across all these platforms, the more confident Google becomes in the data you provide on your GBP, which in turn, makes your updates stick faster and more reliably.

The Cumulative Effect on Local Rankings

When Google sees consistent, accurate, and regularly updated information across multiple reputable sources, it builds trust. This trust translates into better local search rankings. Google wants to provide its users with the most reliable information to avoid sending them to a closed business. By being meticulous with your hours, you're not just preventing customer frustration; you're actively boosting your local SEO, making it easier for new customers to find and choose your business.

Managing your Google Business Profile can feel like a chore, but it's an investment that pays off directly in customer satisfaction and local visibility. By understanding Google's verification process, employing the immediate fix when needed, and adopting proactive management strategies, you can take full control of your business's online presence and ensure your hours are always accurate.
