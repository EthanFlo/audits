The three fixes on 80% of audited restaurant profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Brief

    Many Dublin restaurants are underselling themselves by using just a few sentences in their Google Business Profile description. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet most local eateries use less than 400. This is a missed chance to highlight your unique menu items, signature dishes, ambiance, or whether you cater to specific diets like vegan or gluten-free, directly appealing to searchers before they even click through to your website.

  • Neglecting Crucial Secondary Categories

    While "Restaurant" is essential, many Dublin establishments stop there, missing out on vital specificity. You could be an Italian Restaurant, a Sushi Restaurant, or a Steakhouse. Adding these secondary categories drastically improves your visibility for niche searches. If you offer Brunch, or specialise in Seafood, using those precise categories helps Google match you with the right hungry customers seeking exactly what you offer, rather than a generic dining experience.

  • Lack of Engaging, High-Quality Photos

    A common issue is a Google Business Profile with fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, often leaving potential diners with little visual information. Restaurants in Dublin need to showcase more than just their storefront. Think vibrant shots of your best-selling dishes, the warm, inviting interior, your attentive staff, bustling dinner service, and even detailed close-ups of your dessert menu. These visuals are critical for enticing customers and setting you apart.

Why these matter specifically for restaurants in Dublin, IE

Dublin's vibrant culinary scene is incredibly competitive, especially in areas like Grafton Street, Temple Bar, or the Docklands. With hundreds of excellent restaurants vying for attention, being merely "present" on Google isn't enough. Both visiting tourists, often relying solely on Google Maps for immediate dining options, and locals looking for a new spot, scan results quickly. A lack of comprehensive information or compelling visuals means you'll simply be overlooked for a better-optimized competitor, regardless of your food quality.

Commute patterns across Dublin also mean customers are often searching for dinner on the go – whether finishing work in the city centre or heading out from the suburbs. They're not browsing endlessly; they want quick, relevant results. Missing secondary categories, for example, means your authentic Thai eatery won't appear when someone specifically searches for 'Thai food Dublin,' even if you're just around the corner. Precise optimization helps capture these time-sensitive, intent-driven searches.

Dublin diners, whether locals or visitors, have high expectations and are visual creatures when choosing a restaurant. They want to see what they're getting. An under-optimized profile with few photos or a vague description fails to answer key questions like 'What's the atmosphere like?' or 'Are there vegetarian options?' This directly impacts their decision-making, as they'll gravitate towards profiles that offer a richer, more reassuring preview of their potential dining experience.
