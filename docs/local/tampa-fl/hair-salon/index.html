The three fixes on 80% of audited hair salon profiles

  • Your Google Business Profile Description is Too Short

    Many Tampa hair salons only use a few lines to describe their unique services, leaving hundreds of valuable characters unused. Google allows up to 750 characters for your business description, which is prime real estate to highlight your salon's specialties, atmosphere, and what makes you stand out. A sparse description fails to capture the attention of potential clients scrolling through search results, meaning they might overlook your salon for a competitor with a more engaging and comprehensive overview of their offerings.

  • You're Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Most salons only select "Hair Salon" as their primary category, but Google Business Profile allows for several secondary categories. I often find Tampa hair salons missing out on vital tags like "Colorist," "Hair Extensions Service," or "Men's Hairdresser." These specific categories are essential for attracting clients searching for niche services. Without them, you're invisible to potential customers using highly targeted search terms, severely limiting your reach and the specific types of bookings you receive.

  • Not Enough Owner-Uploaded Photos Showing Your Work

    A Google Business Profile with fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos often signals missed opportunities. For hair salons, this means clients aren't seeing enough examples of stunning balayage, precise haircuts, vibrant colors, or your inviting salon interior. You need a rich visual portfolio. These photos aren't just decorative; they're proof of your skill and a powerful way to entice new clients. Relying on customer-uploaded photos alone means you're not fully controlling your visual story.
