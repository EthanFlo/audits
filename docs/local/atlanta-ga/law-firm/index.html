The three fixes on 80% of audited law firm profiles

  • Underutilized Business Description

    Many law firm Google Business Profiles I review in Atlanta are failing to leverage the full 750-character potential of their business description, often settling for a brief, generic statement. This is a critical missed opportunity to inject keywords related to your specialties, service areas within Atlanta, and your firm's unique value proposition. A short description tells Google and potential clients very little about why your firm is the right choice for their legal needs, hindering your ability to rank for specific, high-intent searches.

  • Missing Critical Secondary Categories

    A common oversight is neglecting to add relevant secondary categories beyond the primary "Attorney" or "Law Firm" designation. Google allows multiple categories, and for Atlanta law firms, this means a chance to be found for specific practice areas. I frequently find profiles missing vital categories like "Personal Injury Lawyer," "Family Law Attorney," or "Estate Planning Attorney," which directly impacts whether your firm appears in targeted searches. Without these, you're missing out on highly qualified local leads.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    A surprising number of law firm profiles have fewer than 30 high-quality, owner-uploaded photos, if any at all. While client reviews often include photos, these are less controlled. Missing are essential images of your Atlanta office exterior and interior, team headshots, specific practice area graphics, and even localized imagery that grounds your firm in the community. Visuals build trust and demonstrate professionalism, and a lack of them makes a profile seem incomplete or less credible to potential clients.
