How to Claim a Google Business Profile

How to Claim a Google Business Profile

To claim a Google Business Profile, navigate to business.google.com, sign in with your Google Account, search for your business, and follow the on-screen prompts to manage or create your listing, initiating the crucial verification process.

Why Claim Your Google Business Profile?

Claiming and verifying your Google Business Profile is a foundational step for any business seeking online visibility, particularly in local search results. This free tool allows you to control how your business appears on Google Search and Maps, directly impacting your ability to connect with potential customers. A claimed profile ensures accuracy of information, such as your business hours, address, phone number, and website, preventing misinformation that could deter customers. Beyond basic details, it enables you to engage with customers through reviews and Q&A features, post updates, showcase products or services, and gain valuable insights into how customers are finding and interacting with your business online. It is a powerful local SEO asset, driving foot traffic and online inquiries.

Step-by-Step: Claiming an Existing Profile

If your business already appears on Google Maps or Search, but you haven't claimed ownership, follow these steps to take control:

  1. Sign In to Google: Go to business.google.com and sign in with the Google Account you wish to associate with your business. This should ideally be an account used for other business-related activities.
  2. Search for Your Business: In the search bar, type your business name and address. Google will show you potential matches.
  3. Select Your Business: If your business appears, click on it. If it says "Own this business?" or "Manage now," click that option.
  4. Initiate Claim: Google will then guide you through the process of claiming the profile. This often involves confirming that you are authorized to manage the business.
  5. Address Existing Claims (if applicable): If the profile has already been claimed by someone else, Google will inform you. You'll then have the option to request ownership from the current owner. This typically involves Google sending a request to the email address associated with the existing claimant, allowing them 3-7 days to respond. If they don't respond, you may be able to verify ownership through alternative means.

Once you initiate the claim, the next critical step is verification.

Creating and Claiming a Brand New Profile

If your business is new or has never appeared on Google before, you will need to create a new profile from scratch. The claiming process is integrated into the creation:

  1. Access Google Business Profile Manager: Go to business.google.com and sign in with your Google Account.
  2. Add Your Business: Click on the "Add your business to Google" option or the "Manage now" button.
  3. Enter Business Name: Type your exact business name. If it's truly new, Google won't find a match, prompting you to "Create a business with this name."
  4. Select Business Category: Choose the primary category that best describes your business. Be as specific as possible.
  5. Add Location (or Service Area):
    • If you have a physical storefront customers can visit, select "Yes" and enter your full business address.
    • If you're a service-area business (e.g., a plumber or landscaper) that operates from a home office and serves customers at their locations, select "No" when asked for a customer-facing location. You will then specify your service areas (cities, counties, zip codes).
  6. Provide Contact Information: Enter your business phone number and website URL (if you have one).
  7. Finish and Verify: Review your entered information. Once complete, you will be prompted to choose a verification method.

Verifying Your Business: The Critical Final Step

Verification is Google's way of confirming that you are the legitimate owner or authorized manager of the business. Without verification, your profile will not be live or fully editable on Google Search and Maps.

Common verification methods include:

  1. Mail Postcard (Most Common): Google sends a postcard containing a unique 5-digit verification code to your business address. This can take 5-14 business days to arrive. Once you receive it, log back into your Google Business Profile dashboard and enter the code. Example: For "The Daily Crumb Bakery," a physical storefront located at 123 Main Street, the most common verification method would be receiving a postcard by mail. This postcard will contain a unique 5-digit code that must be entered on the Google Business Profile dashboard within 30 days of receipt to finalize verification.
  2. Phone or Text: Some businesses may have the option to receive a verification code via an automated phone call or text message to a listed business number. This is often the quickest method if available.
  3. Email: If an email verification option is presented, Google will send a verification code to an email address associated with your business domain.
  4. Video Recording: This newer method requires you to record a short video proving your presence at the business location, showcasing business assets, or demonstrating management access.
  5. Instant Verification: If your business website is already verified with Google Search Console, you might be instantly verified if you use the same Google Account for both services.

After selecting a method, carefully follow the instructions. Keep an eye on your dashboard for updates on your verification status. Once verified, your profile becomes active, allowing you to fully manage and optimize it.

Optimizing Your Profile After Claiming

Claiming and verifying are just the beginning. To maximize your profile's impact, regularly:

  • Complete All Sections: Fill out every available field, including hours of operation, services offered, products, accessibility features, and attributes.
  • Add High-Quality Photos: Upload interior, exterior, product, and team photos to showcase your business.
  • Write a Compelling Description: Craft a concise, keyword-rich overview of your business.
  • Respond to Reviews: Engage with customers by responding to all reviews, both positive and negative.
  • Post Updates Regularly: Use the Posts feature for announcements, offers, events, or product updates.
  • Monitor Performance: Utilize the Insights section to understand how customers find and interact with your profile.
