AggregateRating Schema

AggregateRating Schema is a type of structured data markup that websites use to display an overall rating for a product, service, or business, derived from multiple individual reviews.

This markup informs search engines about the average rating, the total number of reviews, and often the best and worst possible rating values, helping them understand the collective sentiment regarding an entity. When implemented correctly, this information typically appears as star ratings (known as "rich snippets") directly within the search results, making a listing more visually appealing.

While AggregateRating Schema directly influences your website's organic search appearance, its impact extends to local SEO and Google Business Profile (GBP) ranking by enhancing your online visibility and credibility. Rich snippets with star ratings significantly boost click-through rates (CTR) from the search results page to your website. A higher CTR signals to Google that your listing is highly relevant and valuable, which can indirectly contribute to improved overall local search visibility and authority. Without these prominent visual cues, your search result may blend in with numerous competitors, potentially losing out on valuable clicks and customer engagement.

Consider a Chicago dentist, "Windy City Dental," aiming to attract more local patients. On their main services page, which outlines various dental treatments, they implement AggregateRating Schema. This markup informs Google that Windy City Dental has, for instance, an average rating of 4.9 out of 5 stars based on 220 patient reviews. When a potential patient searches for "dentist Chicago," Windy City Dental's organic listing might appear with five bright gold stars next to it. This visual distinction helps their website stand out significantly from other search results that lack this powerful enhancement, drawing immediate attention and trust.

A common mistake is applying AggregateRating Schema to a page that does not actually display visible customer reviews for the entity being rated. Google strictly requires that the ratings data be present and easily accessible on the page where the schema is applied. Another error involves implementing the schema incorrectly, such as using the wrong item type (for example, marking a service as a product) or failing to provide all required properties like reviewCount and ratingValue. Some businesses mistakenly assume that simply adding this schema will automatically improve their Google Business Profile rating, when in fact, the schema primarily affects how their website appears in organic search, not the direct star rating within the GBP itself.

  • Ensure Visibility: Always make sure the ratings and reviews you mark up are clearly visible on the page to users. If a review isn't visible, do not mark it up.
  • Be Specific: Apply AggregateRating Schema to specific service pages or product pages, not just the homepage, unless the homepage truly summarizes all reviews for the entire business.
  • Collect Reviews: Actively encourage customers to leave reviews on your website (or a platform your website aggregates from) to provide valid, up-to-date data for your schema.
  • Monitor for Errors: Regularly use Google's Rich Results Test tool to check for any implementation errors or warnings, ensuring your schema is correctly interpreted.
  • Update Regularly: As new reviews come in, update the ratingValue and reviewCount properties in your schema to reflect the most current information, maintaining accuracy.
