The three fixes on 80% of audited photographer profiles

*

Your Google Business Profile Description is Too Short

Many Seattle photographers write a brief, unoptimized description for their Google Business Profile, often well under Google's 750-character limit. This is a missed opportunity to include crucial keywords like "PNW wedding photography," "senior portraits Seattle," or "commercial photography Tacoma" and elaborate on your unique style, experience, and the specific services you offer. A detailed description helps Google understand exactly what you do, boosting your relevance for local searches.

*

Missing Key Secondary Categories

Beyond "Photographer," many profiles lack specific secondary categories that truly define their niche. To effectively reach clients searching for very specific services in Seattle, you need them. I often find profiles missing "Wedding Photographer," "Portrait Studio," and "Real Estate Photography Service." Adding these helps Google categorize your business precisely, ensuring you appear for highly targeted searches in the competitive Seattle market.

*

Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

A bare-bones photo gallery on your GBP means missed connections. Most profiles I see have too few owner-uploaded images, relying on sparse client uploads. You should showcase a diverse portfolio: professional headshots, behind-the-scenes studio shots, vibrant examples of specific client work (weddings, family, events), and even photos of your studio or office. High-quality, diverse visuals are critical for standing out and building trust with potential clients in Seattle.
