The three fixes on 80% of audited locksmith profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many Brisbane locksmiths use a brief, often generic description under 400 characters, missing a huge opportunity. Google allows up to 750 characters to tell your story, detail services like emergency lockout, key cutting, and security system installation, and highlight your local expertise. A concise description fails to capture search intent for specific services, making it harder for potential clients to understand your full offerings and choose your business over a competitor.

  • Missing Key Secondary Categories

    Beyond "Locksmith," your Google Business Profile likely isn't leveraging crucial secondary categories. Many Brisbane locksmiths miss out on narrowing down their service specialties. I often find profiles lacking categories like "Emergency Locksmith," "Auto Locksmith," and "Safe Opening Service." Adding these tells Google exactly what niche services you offer, significantly improving your visibility for specific, high-intent searches from customers in urgent need.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Most locksmith profiles in Brisbane fall short on owner-uploaded photos. Instead of just a logo, your profile should showcase your team on jobs, your service vehicles, actual examples of locks repaired or installed, and even satisfied customer interactions. Without a diverse gallery of at least 30 high-quality images, your profile appears less trustworthy and engaging, failing to visually reassure potential clients about your professionalism and range of services before they even call.
