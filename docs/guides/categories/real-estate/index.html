Best Google Business categories for a real estate agent

When optimizing your Google Business Profile (GBP) as a real estate agent, understanding the strategic importance of categories is paramount. Your chosen categories don't just describe your business; they dictate the specific "ranking lanes" in which Google considers you for local search queries. Think of them as the storefront signs telling Google exactly what services you offer and to whom.

Most real estate professionals make the critical mistake of selecting only one primary category, leaving the potential for up to nine additional secondary categories completely untapped. This oversight means you're only competing in one lane, while your savvier competitors are leveraging all ten available opportunities to appear in a wider array of relevant searches, significantly increasing their visibility and lead generation potential.

The three fixes on 80% of audited real estate agent profiles

Real estate agent

Choosing "Real estate agent" as your primary category is crucial because it is the most direct and specific match for your profession within Google's taxonomy. While "Real estate agency" is another option, it can dilute your individual agent profile by suggesting a larger firm rather than your personal brand and services. This precise categorization ensures your profile ranks for direct searches like "real estate agent near me" or "find a real estate agent," immediately connecting you with individuals actively seeking a personal representative for buying or selling property.

Why these matter specifically for real estate agents in Categories

  1. Buyer's agent: Unlocks searches from individuals specifically looking for assistance in purchasing a home or property.
  2. Seller's agent: Captures queries from homeowners actively seeking representation to sell their property.
  3. Property management company: Attracts landlords and investors looking for professional services to manage their rental properties.
  4. Residential real estate agency: Broadens your reach for general residential property inquiries, encompassing both buying and selling homes.
  5. Commercial real estate agency: Positions you for searches related to the purchase, sale, or lease of commercial properties like offices or retail spaces.
  6. Real estate consultant: Targets clients seeking expert advice, market analysis, or strategic planning beyond standard transaction services.
  7. Apartment rental service: Helps you appear for searches by individuals looking to find rental apartments or other residential leases.
  8. House hunting service: Appeals to clients who need comprehensive, guided assistance throughout the process of finding their ideal home.
  9. Real estate appraiser: While often a separate profession, some agents offer Broker Price Opinions (BPOs), justifying inclusion to attract valuation-focused queries.
