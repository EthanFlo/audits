The three fixes on 80% of audited landscaper profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many landscapers in Adelaide truncate their business description on Google Business Profile, missing a crucial opportunity to stand out. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet most profiles I audit use less than 400. This space is vital for detailing your unique services, specifying your Adelaide service areas, and highlighting your expertise in native plantings, irrigation, or custom garden designs, giving potential clients a comprehensive understanding of what you offer.

  • Missing Key Secondary Categories

    Your Google Business Profile likely lists you solely as a "Landscaper," which is a good start but often insufficient. To truly capture diverse local searches in Adelaide, you need to utilise secondary categories. I often find profiles missing essential additions like "Lawn Care Service," "Hardscaping Contractor," or "Tree Service." Adding these helps Google understand the full breadth of your offerings and matches you with specific customer queries.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Are you showcasing your incredible work with fewer than 30 high-quality photos on your profile? Many Adelaide landscapers neglect this visual aspect. Missing photos typically include before-and-after transformations, close-ups of specific plant selections, shots of your team in action, or images of finished projects under different lighting. Customers want to visualise their dream garden, and a sparse photo gallery fails to build that vital trust and inspiration.

    Adelaide's commute patterns mean residents often prefer hiring services from within their immediate region or a very accessible neighbouring suburb.     Customer search behaviour for landscapers is highly visual and trust-based. Detailed descriptions, specific categories, and ample high-quality photos help customers instantly assess if your style and services align with their vision, before they even make a call.
