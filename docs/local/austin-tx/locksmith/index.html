The three fixes on 80% of audited locksmith profiles

  • Underutilized Business Description

    Many Austin locksmiths stop their Google Business Profile description at less than 400 characters, missing out on valuable space. Google allows up to 750 characters, and leaving it brief means you're not fully communicating your specializations like 24/7 emergency service, specific lock types handled, or your commitment to rapid response times. This is a crucial missed opportunity to include relevant keywords and tell your story beyond the basics, limiting your profile's discoverability in specific searches.

  • Missing Critical Secondary Categories

    A common finding is that locksmith profiles only use the primary "Locksmith" category. However, Google allows multiple secondary categories which significantly boost visibility for niche searches. Many Austin locksmiths fail to add specific, high-intent categories such as "Emergency Locksmith," "Auto Locksmith," and "Safe Opening." Without these, your business is invisible to customers specifically searching for these urgent and specialized services, directing them to competitors who have properly categorized their offerings.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Your Google Business Profile should showcase a robust gallery, yet many Austin locksmiths have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. Missing are images of your branded service vehicles, your team in uniform, specific high-tech tools, examples of different lock types you service, your storefront or workshop, and before/after photos of successful repairs. These visual elements build trust and professionalism, and a sparse photo gallery suggests a less established or less active business to potential customers in urgent need.
