The three fixes on 80% of audited photographer profiles

  • Business Description Under 400 Characters

    Many photographers underutilise their GBP description, often only listing services in a few short sentences. Google allows up to 750 characters, a crucial space to tell your brand story, highlight your unique photographic style, and connect emotionally with potential clients in Wellington. A brief, uninspired description fails to capture attention, losing out on a chance to differentiate yourself from the countless other studios and freelancers.

  • Missing Secondary Categories

    Your primary category might be "Photographer," but without specific secondary categories, you miss out on niche searches. I often find profiles lacking precise labels like "Wedding Photographer," "Portrait Studio," or "Real Estate Photographer." This oversight means your business won't show up when a Wellington client searches for those exact services, giving an immediate advantage to competitors who have properly categorized their offerings.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    A strong visual presence is vital for photographers, yet many profiles have sparse owner-uploaded content. Beyond your portfolio, clients want to see behind-the-scenes shots, your studio space, your team, and even unique Wellington locations where you shoot. A lack of diverse, high-quality images suggests an inactive profile and fails to build trust or showcase the breadth of your work and professionalism.
