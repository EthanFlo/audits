The three fixes on 80% of audited hvac contractor profiles

  • Your Google Business Profile Description is Too Short

    Many Dallas HVAC contractors use a brief, generic description that barely scratches the surface. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet most profiles I review barely hit 200-300. This is a lost opportunity to highlight your Dallas service areas, specialties like smart thermostat installation, emergency repair availability, and unique selling points. A robust description signals authority and relevance to both customers and Google's algorithm.

  • You're Not Maximizing Your Service Categories

    Most HVAC businesses nail the primary "HVAC Contractor" category, but overlook crucial secondary ones. Google uses these to understand the full scope of your services, matching you to more diverse search queries. For Dallas HVAC professionals, I often find missing vital categories like "AC Repair Service," "Furnace Repair Service," or "Duct Cleaning Service." Adding these accurately expands your reach significantly within the Dallas metroplex.

  • Your Photo Gallery is Underperforming

    A picture is worth a thousand leads, especially in a visual search environment. Many Dallas HVAC profiles have fewer than 10 owner-uploaded photos, when ideally, you should aim for 30+. You're likely missing images of your team in uniform, technicians working on AC units or furnaces, before/after shots of duct cleaning, clearly branded service vans, or even photos showcasing your office and happy customers (with permission).
