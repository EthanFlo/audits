The three fixes on 80% of audited hair salon profiles

  • Missed Opportunity in Your Business Description

    Many Winnipeg hair salons use only a brief description, often well under Google's 750-character limit. This leaves crucial details unsaid. You’re missing chances to highlight your salon's unique atmosphere, specific techniques you excel in, or the brands you proudly use. A short description fails to capture what truly sets your salon apart from the competition, diminishing its appeal in local searches.

  • Incomplete Service Categorization

    Frequently, salons only list "Hair Salon" as their primary category. While accurate, this overlooks vital secondary categories that attract specific clientele. I often find profiles missing "Hair Colorist," "Barber Shop," or "Bridal Hair Salon," for example. Without these precise categories, you're not appearing for highly targeted searches from clients seeking those exact services in Winnipeg, limiting your specialized market reach.

  • Sparse Visual Portfolio

    A common issue is a Google Business Profile featuring fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. Potential clients want to visualize your expertise and the salon environment. Profiles often lack compelling 'after' shots of stunning transformations, inviting interior views, candid team photos, or even clear images of your premium product lines. This visual scarcity prevents clients from fully connecting with your brand online.

Winnipeg has a vibrant but dense hair salon market. New salons are always popping up in areas like Osborne Village, Corydon, and The Exchange District.
