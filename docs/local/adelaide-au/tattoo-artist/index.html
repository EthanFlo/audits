The three fixes on 80% of audited tattoo artist profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many Adelaide tattoo studios use a brief, often generic description that barely scratches the surface. Google allows up to 750 characters, but I frequently find profiles using less than 400. This is a missed opportunity to showcase your studio's unique style, the specific techniques you excel in, your studio vibe, and what truly sets your artistry apart in the competitive local scene. Expanding this text helps Google understand your business better, leading to more relevant searches finding you.

  • Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Beyond "Tattoo Shop," many profiles neglect to add secondary categories. These are vital for Adelaide clients searching for specific styles. I often find studios missing categories like "Fine Line Tattoo Artist," "Traditional Tattoo Artist," or "Cover-Up Specialist." Without these, Google struggles to match your specific expertise to a client's detailed search, meaning you miss out on highly qualified leads looking for exactly what you offer, right here in Adelaide.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    I consistently observe tattoo artist profiles with sparse image galleries, often fewer than 30 photos. This overlooks the visual nature of your industry. Potential clients want to see high-quality, diverse examples of your finished work, close-ups of intricate details, the clean and welcoming interior of your studio, and even team photos that convey professionalism. A limited gallery fails to build trust or showcase your full artistic range to someone browsing for their next tattoo in Adelaide.
