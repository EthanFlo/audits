The three fixes on 80% of audited physical therapist profiles

  • Your Thin Business Description Misses Key Patient Needs

    Many Kansas City physical therapists leave valuable space unused in their Google Business Profile description. With Google allowing up to 750 characters, a description under 400 often means you're not showcasing your full range of services – like specialized dry needling, concussion therapy, or specific sports injury rehab – to potential patients actively searching for those exact solutions in the metro area.

  • Neglecting Crucial Secondary Service Categories

    Beyond 'Physical Therapist,' Google offers powerful secondary categories that pinpoint your specializations. I frequently find Kansas City PT profiles missing critical additions like 'Sports Therapy,' 'Post-Surgery Rehabilitation,' or 'Pediatric Physical Therapy.' Adding these tells Google exactly who you serve, significantly improving your visibility for specific, high-intent searches from patients needing precise care.

  • Too Few Visuals to Build Patient Trust and Interest

    A common oversight for Kansas City physical therapists is an under-optimized photo gallery. Many profiles have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, missing opportunities to showcase your welcoming clinic interior, individual treatment rooms, state-of-the-art equipment, and smiling staff interacting with patients. These visual cues are vital for prospective patients to get a feel for your practice before they even call or visit, especially in a competitive market.
