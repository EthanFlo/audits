The three fixes on 80% of audited landscaper profiles

  • Your Google Business Profile Description is Too Short

    Many Toronto landscapers use a sparse description, missing out on valuable keywords and space. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet most profiles are under 400. This means you’re not fully explaining your unique value proposition, specific services like interlocking or garden design, or the neighborhoods you serve within Toronto. A robust description helps Google understand your business better, improving visibility for relevant local searches and showcasing your expertise to potential clients.

  • You're Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Often, landscapers only list "Landscaper" as their primary category. This limits your reach. An audit frequently reveals missing secondary categories that would capture more specific searches. For Toronto landscapers, adding categories like "Lawn Care Service," "Hardscaping Contractor," and "Tree Service" significantly broadens your visibility. These specific categories tell Google exactly what services you offer, connecting you with customers searching for those exact needs, rather than just generic landscaping.

  • Your Profile Lacks Sufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    A Google Business Profile audit often shows landscapers with fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. While customers upload some, professional, varied images are key. We often find profiles missing before-and-after shots of garden makeovers, detailed close-ups of interlock patios, team photos in action, or even specific seasonal work like spring cleanups or fall planting. High-quality, diverse photos build trust and showcase your work, crucial for attracting visual-minded clients across Toronto looking for their next outdoor project.
