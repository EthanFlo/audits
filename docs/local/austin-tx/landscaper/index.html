The three fixes on 80% of audited landscaper profiles

  • Limited Business Description

    Many Austin landscapers underutilize the crucial business description field, often leaving it brief and generic, sometimes under 400 characters. Google allows up to 750 characters to highlight your unique selling points. This is where you should detail your specialties—from sustainable xeriscaping to stunning patio installations—and showcase why you’re the best choice for Austin homeowners, providing rich keywords that help Google understand your comprehensive services.

  • Missing Essential Secondary Categories

    A common oversight is failing to add relevant secondary categories beyond just "Landscaper." To truly capture diverse local searches, your profile needs to reflect the full scope of your Austin services. We frequently find landscapers missing critical categories like "Lawn Care Service," "Hardscape Contractor," and "Tree Service." Adding these helps Google accurately categorize your business, ensuring you appear for specific, high-intent searches that potential clients are making.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Local landscaping clients want to visualize your work, yet many profiles feature fewer than 30 high-quality photos uploaded by the business. We often find a lack of "before and after" project images, photos of your team in action, diverse plant installations, or completed hardscaping. Google prioritizes fresh, varied visuals. An audit identifies these photo gaps, ensuring your profile effectively showcases your best work and builds trust with potential customers browsing for design and installation.
