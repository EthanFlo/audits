Secondary Category

A secondary category on a Google Business Profile (GBP) is an additional business classification that helps Google understand the full range of products or services a local business offers beyond its primary specialization. While the primary category defines the core nature of the business, secondary categories provide crucial context, enabling the business to appear in search results for related, but not central, customer queries. They are supporting descriptors that broaden a business's relevant audience without diluting its main identity. Secondary categories significantly impact a business's visibility on Google Maps and local search results because they tell Google which other user searches the business is relevant for. Without appropriate secondary categories, a business might miss out on appearing in the "local pack" or Google Maps for important niche services, directly affecting customer discovery and potential leads. For example, a a restaurant primarily categorized as "Italian Restaurant" might also offer "Pizza Delivery" or "Catering Service," and these secondary designations capture valuable specific searches. Properly chosen categories enhance the likelihood of a business matching a broader spectrum of user intents, leading to increased organic traffic and customer engagement. Consider a business operating in Seattle, Washington. If their primary category is "Veterinarian," that clearly states their core service. However, they might also offer specialized services like "Pet Groomer," "Pet Boarding Service," or even "Veterinary Pharmacy." By adding these as secondary categories, the Seattle veterinary clinic ensures that when a local resident searches for "pet grooming near me" or "pet boarding Seattle," their business has a better chance of appearing, even if the user didn't specifically search for a veterinarian at that moment. This strategic categorization expands their reach beyond their main offering. A common mistake is selecting too many secondary categories or choosing categories that are not genuinely reflective of the services offered, which can confuse Google and potential customers. Businesses sometimes try to "game the system" by adding irrelevant categories in an attempt to rank for more keywords, but this often backfires, leading to diminished trust and relevance signals. Another misconception is thinking that secondary categories carry the same weight as the primary category, while important, they support the main classification, not replace it. Furthermore, some businesses neglect to review and update their categories over time as their services evolve, missing opportunities. To effectively utilize secondary categories for your Google Business Profile:
  • Be Specific and Relevant: Only select categories that accurately describe services you genuinely provide and want to attract customers for.
  • Prioritize Customer Intent: Think about the specific terms customers would use when searching for your services. Use those as a guide.
  • Research Competitors: See what categories successful competitors are using, but always ensure they apply to your business too.
  • Review Google's Category List: Google provides a predefined list of categories, stick to these and do not create custom ones.
  • Monitor and Update: Regularly review your chosen categories, especially if your business introduces new services or discontinues old ones.
