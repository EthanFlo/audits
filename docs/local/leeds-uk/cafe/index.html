The three fixes on 80% of audited cafe profiles

  • Your Cafe's Story is Too Short

    Many Leeds cafes use only a few sentences to describe their unique selling points, leaving their Google Business Profile description well under the 400-character mark, let alone Google's 750-character limit. This is a missed opportunity to showcase your artisan coffee, vegan brunch options, or the cosy atmosphere that makes your cafe special. A compelling, keyword-rich description helps potential customers in Leeds understand exactly why they should choose your spot over others.

  • Overlooking Key Secondary Categories

    Often, cafes only select 'Cafe' as their primary category, completely missing out on crucial secondary categories that help them appear in more specific searches. For a cafe in Leeds, adding options like 'Coffee Shop,' 'Brunch Restaurant,' or 'Bakery' can dramatically expand your reach. These specific categories tell Google exactly what services you offer, connecting you with customers searching for more than just a generic 'cafe' experience.

  • A Picture is Worth a Thousand Coffees (But You Need More)

    Another common issue is a scarcity of owner-uploaded photos, often fewer than the recommended 30. Potential customers want to see your vibrant smashed avocado on toast, the skilled baristas crafting lattes, your inviting outdoor seating area, or even the locally sourced pastries on display. High-quality, diverse imagery not only attracts attention but also builds trust and anticipation, making your Leeds cafe irresistible before they even step foot inside.

Many Leeds residents and visitors are on the move, whether commuting to work, shopping, or exploring new areas. Their cafe searches are often spontaneous and location-driven, happening on their phones as they walk past or before they leave home. A fully optimised profile with rich descriptions and categories means your cafe is precisely matched with these 'on-the-go' searches, capturing immediate interest and directing foot traffic from nearby offices, universities, or tourist spots directly to your door.

For cafes, visual appeal and atmosphere are paramount to customer decision-making. People rarely choose a coffee shop based purely on text; they want to see the inviting interior, the quality of the food, and the overall vibe. In a city like Leeds, where social media and word-of-mouth are strong, having an extensive gallery of high-quality, diverse photos on your Google Business Profile serves as a critical first impression. It allows customers to 'experience' your cafe virtually before committing to a visit, which is especially important for attracting new patrons who might be unfamiliar with your specific location.
