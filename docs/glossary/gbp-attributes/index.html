GBP Attributes

GBP Attributes are specific features, amenities, and service options that businesses can select and display on their Google Business Profile (GBP). These predefined tags, provided directly by Google, help accurately describe a business to potential customers, appearing prominently in Google Search results and Google Maps listings. They categorize essential details like accessibility, service offerings, and payment options, ensuring users quickly grasp what a business offers before clicking through to learn more.

These attributes are critically important for local SEO and directly influence how a business ranks and appears in local search results. Google utilizes attributes to better understand a business's offerings, matching it more precisely with user search queries that include specific needs or preferences. Properly utilized attributes can significantly increase visibility, improve click-through rates by attracting highly qualified leads, and enhance the overall user experience by providing relevant information upfront, distinguishing a business from its local competitors.

Consider a bustling Seattle coffee shop, for instance. By correctly selecting attributes like "Free Wi-Fi," "Outdoor Seating," "Curbside Pickup," "Wheelchair Accessible Entrance," and "Dog-friendly," the coffee shop immediately communicates a wealth of information. A customer searching for "coffee shop with outdoor seating Seattle" or "wheelchair accessible coffee near me" will see this business highlighted, making it a preferred choice over competitors who might offer similar services but have not declared these specific attributes. This clarity helps potential patrons make informed decisions quickly.

A common mistake businesses make is overlooking or ignoring GBP Attributes, assuming they are minor details with little impact. Another misconception is believing attributes are only relevant for brick-and-mortar stores or specific industries like restaurants. Service-based businesses, for example, can use attributes like "Online Appointments" or "Free Estimates" effectively. Furthermore, businesses sometimes select attributes inaccurately or fail to update them when offerings change, which can lead to customer frustration, negative reviews, and ultimately, a damaged local reputation when user expectations are not met.

To effectively manage GBP Attributes:

  • Regularly review and update them: Ensure your attributes accurately reflect your current offerings and any changes.
  • Be honest and accurate: Misleading attributes will lead to poor customer experiences and negative feedback.
  • Prioritize customer-facing attributes: Focus on features that differentiate your business or address common customer needs, such as "Delivery" or "Accepts Credit Cards."
  • Observe competitors: Check what attributes your local competitors are using, and ensure you are not missing crucial ones that align with your business.
  • Encourage customer reviews: Specific customer feedback mentioning attributes (e.g., "Loved the outdoor seating!") can further validate your selections.
  • Utilize Google Business Profile Insights: Review performance reports to understand how users are discovering your business and what features they might be looking for.
