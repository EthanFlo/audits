The three fixes on 80% of audited cafe profiles

  • Underutilised Business Description

    Many Glasgow cafes miss the chance to fully describe their unique offerings. Google allows 750 characters, yet I often find profiles using less than half. This is prime real estate to highlight your artisan coffee, vegan brunch options, or dog-friendly policy, setting you apart from the bustling West End competition and clearly communicating your unique selling points to potential customers scrolling by.

  • Missing Key Secondary Categories

    While most cafes have "Cafe" as their primary category, many neglect crucial secondary ones. For a Glasgow cafe, adding categories like "Coffee Shop," "Brunch Restaurant," or "Bakery" significantly broadens your reach. This ensures you appear in more specific local searches, capturing customers looking for exactly what you offer beyond a generic "cafe" search, enhancing your visibility across different search intents.

  • Insufficient Visual Storytelling

    A picture is worth a thousand words, especially for enticing food and drink. I frequently see Glasgow cafe profiles with fewer than 30 high-quality, owner-uploaded photos. This means missing opportunities to showcase your latte art, inviting interiors, bustling atmosphere, outdoor seating, or mouth-watering pastry displays. Robust visual content is essential for attracting new customers and making your profile irresistible.

Why these matter specifically for cafes in Glasgow, UK

Glasgow's cafe scene is incredibly vibrant and competitive, from the bustling city centre to the charming West End and the Southside. Commute patterns and lunchtime rushes in Glasgow create specific search behaviour. People are often searching for "cafe near me" or "coffee shop with WiFi" on the go.
