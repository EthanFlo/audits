30-point Google Business Profile checklist for locksmiths

General advice for optimizing your Google Business Profile (GBP) can feel overwhelming and directionless. "Improve your profile" is a nice sentiment, but it doesn't tell a busy locksmith *exactly* what to do when they have a spare half-hour between calls. That's where a precise, actionable checklist becomes invaluable, transforming vague goals into a series of conquerable tasks.

This 30-point checklist isn't just a list of suggestions; it's a meticulously ordered sequence designed for maximum impact. Imagine a quiet Tuesday afternoon with no urgent calls – this is your go-to guide to systematically enhance your online visibility, ensuring potential customers searching for "emergency locksmith" or "auto locksmith" find *your* business first. Work through each item diligently, one by one.

The three fixes on 80% of audited locksmith profiles

Business Info

  • ☐ Verify your primary business name precisely matches legal documentation.
  • ☐ Ensure your physical street address is exact and consistently formatted.
  • ☐ Confirm your local phone number is active and accepts customer calls.
  • ☐ Add your accurate website URL, linking directly to your locksmith services page.
  • ☐ Set your business hours accurately, including 24/7 availability if applicable.

Categories

  • ☐ Select "Locksmith" as your primary business category.
  • ☐ Add "Emergency Locksmith" as a secondary category if you offer urgent services.
  • ☐ Include "Auto Locksmith" if you specialize in car key replacement or entry.
  • ☐ Add "Safe and Vault Opening Service" if you open safes.
  • ☐ Review all categories annually to ensure they reflect your current offerings.

Photos

  • ☐ Upload a clear, high-resolution photo of your storefront or service vehicle.
  • ☐ Add at least three interior/exterior photos showcasing your professional equipment.
  • ☐ Include photos of your team members in uniform providing locksmith services.
  • ☐ Upload before-and-after photos of lock repairs or installations.
  • ☐ Post photos of specific tools or unique services, like safe opening equipment.

Services

  • ☐ List "Emergency Lockout Service" with a detailed description.
  • ☐ Detail "Automotive Key Duplication" and "Car Remote Programming."
  • ☐ Add "Residential Lock Installation" and "Commercial Security Upgrades."
  • ☐ Include niche services like "Safe Opening" or "Access Control Systems."
  • ☐ Regularly update service descriptions with new technologies or offerings.

Reviews

  • ☐ Respond professionally to every new Google review, positive or negative.
  • ☐ Encourage satisfied customers to leave reviews after service completion.
  • ☐ Address negative reviews promptly, offering solutions or clarifications.
  • ☐ Acknowledge positive reviews, thanking customers for their trust.
  • ☐ Flag any spam or irrelevant reviews for Google to remove.

Posts

  • ☐ Create weekly Google Posts announcing new services or promotions.
  • ☐ Share informational posts about home security tips or new lock technology.
  • ☐ Post updates on holiday hours or 24/7 availability for Emergency Locksmith.
  • ☐ Announce special offers for Auto Locksmith services or key duplication.
  • ☐ Use relevant high-quality images and clear calls to action in every post.

Why these matter specifically for locksmiths in Checklist

After meticulously working through all 30 points on this checklist, your job isn't done – it's just beginning. Give your Google Business Profile about two weeks to allow Google's algorithm to re-index and reflect your comprehensive updates. Then, dive into your Google Business Profile Insights to measure the impact: look for increases in searches, map views, website clicks, and phone calls. This data is crucial for understanding what's working. Repeat this entire checklist process every quarter, ensuring your profile remains sharp, current, and maximally optimized for attracting new locksmith business.
