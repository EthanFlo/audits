30-point Google Business Profile checklist for dermatologists

This 30-point Google Business Profile checklist for dermatologists cuts through general advice, providing specific, actionable steps to enhance your clinic's online visibility. Generic tips often lead to incomplete optimization, leaving opportunities missed. This specialized checklist is designed to guide you through a precise sequence, ensuring every critical element of your profile is addressed with the unique needs of a dermatology practice in mind.

Imagine a slow afternoon at the clinic: instead of catching up on emails, you systematically work through this list. Each point is a direct instruction, removing guesswork and inefficiency. By following these steps, you'll not only improve your ranking and patient engagement but also project an image of professionalism and comprehensive care that resonates with potential patients actively searching for dermatological services.

The three fixes on 80% of audited dermatologist profiles

Business Info

  • ☐ Verify exact business name matching your official practice registration.
  • ☐ Ensure your primary phone number is direct and frequently monitored for patient calls.
  • ☐ Update your precise clinic street address for accurate local map results.
  • ☐ Specify accurate clinic hours, including any lunch closures or extended evening availability.
  • ☐ Add your practice's website URL, ideally linking to your dermatology services page.

Categories

  • ☐ Set "Dermatologist" as your primary Google Business Profile category.
  • ☐ Add "Cosmetic Surgeon" if you offer extensive cosmetic dermatology procedures.
  • ☐ Include "Medical Spa" if offering aesthetic services like injectables or advanced facials.
  • ☐ Specify "Skin Cancer Screening Service" if this is a key offering.
  • ☐ Consider "Pediatric Dermatologist" if that's a dedicated sub-specialty for your practice.

Photos

  • ☐ Upload a clear, professional photo of your clinic's exterior during daylight hours.
  • ☐ Include high-resolution images of your comfortable waiting room and private consultation rooms.
  • ☐ Add professional headshots of all practicing dermatologists and key support staff.
  • ☐ Show appropriate before-and-after photos for cosmetic dermatology (with consent, if applicable).
  • ☐ Post images of specialized equipment, such as lasers or dermoscopes, used in treatments.

Services

  • ☐ List "Skin Cancer Screening" as a prominent, detailed service with a clear description.
  • ☐ Detail "Cosmetic Dermatology" services like injectables, lasers, chemical peels, and body contouring.
  • ☐ Elaborate on "Acne Treatment" including various therapeutic approaches for all ages.
  • ☐ Add "Pediatric Dermatology" to specify care for children's skin conditions.
  • ☐ Specify "Medical Dermatology" for conditions like eczema, psoriasis, rosacea, and rashes.

Reviews

  • ☐ Respond professionally and promptly to all new Google reviews, both positive and negative.
  • ☐ Encourage satisfied patients to leave a review via a direct link or QR code.
  • ☐ Address negative feedback constructively, offering offline resolution pathways for patient concerns.
  • ☐ Regularly monitor review sentiment for common patient praises or recurring concerns about your practice.
  • ☐ Highlight positive reviews that specifically mention individual dermatologists or successful treatments.

Posts

  • ☐ Create a weekly post about a common skin condition (e.g., "Understanding Eczema & Its Triggers").
  • ☐ Announce new cosmetic dermatology treatments, products, or clinic technologies.
  • ☐ Share seasonal skin care tips, like summer sun protection or winter hydration strategies.
  • ☐ Promote special offers on services such as laser hair removal, Botox, or chemical peels.
  • ☐ Post about clinic news, new staff joining, or community involvement related to skin health.

Why these matter specifically for dermatologists in Checklist

Once all 30 points on this checklist are meticulously addressed, your work isn't done, but the initial heavy lifting is. The crucial next step is to measure the impact: diligently monitor your Google Business Profile Insights over the coming weeks. Pay attention to changes in views, searches, and actions like website visits or calls. Allow at least 14 days for Google's algorithms to fully process and reflect your updates before making further major changes, then repeat this process by revisiting each point to ensure ongoing accuracy and optimization.
