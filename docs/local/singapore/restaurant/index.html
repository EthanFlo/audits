The three fixes on 80% of audited restaurant profiles

  • Missing Flavour in Your Business Description

    Many Singapore restaurants use only a few sentences to describe their unique offerings. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet I often find profiles with descriptions barely scratching 200-300 characters. This missed opportunity means you're not conveying your signature dishes, the ambiance, or your culinary philosophy to potential diners, making your establishment sound generic when it truly isn't.

  • Overlooking Key Secondary Categories

    Most restaurants correctly pick their primary category, like "Restaurant." However, a common audit finding is the complete absence of crucial secondary categories that significantly enhance discoverability. You could be missing out on niche searches by not adding specific types such as "Italian Restaurant," "Seafood Restaurant," or "Vegetarian Restaurant," which directly impacts who sees your business.

  • Insufficient Visual Appeal: Too Few Photos

    In a visually driven city like Singapore, diners eat with their eyes first. I frequently observe restaurant profiles with fewer than 10-15 owner-uploaded photos, far from the optimal 30+. Missing are high-quality shots of your signature mains, inviting interior spaces during peak hours, the vibrant bar area, or even close-ups of freshly prepared desserts and handcrafted cocktails.

Why these matter specifically for restaurants in Singapore

  • Singapore’s F&B scene is incredibly dense. From hawker centers to Michelin-starred establishments, a potential diner has hundreds of choices within a small radius. A half-optimized Google Business Profile means you’re invisible in a crowded market where every detail counts for standing out against a highly competitive set of neighbours.

  • With efficient public transport and short distances, Singaporeans are less reliant on being within walking distance. They often search for a specific cuisine or dining experience, regardless of the immediate vicinity. A fully optimized profile ensures you capture these intentional searches, making commuters willing to travel a few extra stops for your distinct offering.

  • Singaporean diners are savvy and often use specific, long-tail search queries like "best Hokkien Mee in Katong" or "romantic rooftop bar near Marina Bay." If your profile lacks detailed descriptions, relevant secondary categories, or enticing photos, you simply won't appear for these highly qualified, conversion-ready searches that define local restaurant discovery.

  • Furthermore, in a culture that values presentation and quality, high-resolution, current photos are paramount. A profile with outdated or few images erodes trust and diminishes perceived quality, especially when competing with neighbours who meticulously showcase their culinary art and inviting ambiance online.
