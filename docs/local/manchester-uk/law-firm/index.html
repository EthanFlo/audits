The three fixes on 80% of audited law firm profiles

  • Underutilised Business Description

    Many Manchester law firms miss the chance to fully describe their practice. With Google allowing 750 characters, leaving your description brief means you're not showcasing key services or your unique approach. This limits opportunities to include crucial keywords clients use when searching for solicitors, making your firm harder to find for specific legal needs.

  • Missing Secondary Service Categories

    Most law firms select only "Law Firm" as their primary category, but Google Business Profile allows several specific secondary categories. Often, profiles lack crucial additions like "Personal Injury Lawyer," "Family Law," or "Estate Planning Solicitor." This oversight prevents Google from accurately matching your firm with highly specific client searches, severely limiting relevant visibility.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    A visually sparse Google Business Profile gives clients little to connect with. We often find profiles with fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. This means crucial images like exterior and interior office shots, barrister headshots, client meeting areas, or even local Manchester landmarks related to your practice are absent, reducing trust and engagement.
