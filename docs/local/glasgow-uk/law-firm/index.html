The three fixes on 80% of audited law firm profiles

  • Business Description is Under 400 Characters

    Many Glasgow law firms use a brief, uninspired Google Business Profile description. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet most are well under 400. This is a missed opportunity to showcase your firm's specific practice areas, its unique approach to client care, and your deep roots in the Glasgow community. A comprehensive description helps clients understand exactly how you can assist them before they even visit your website.

  • Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Your firm might have "Law Firm" as its primary category, which is correct, but many neglect to add essential secondary categories. Our audits frequently find firms missing vital specialisations. Properly adding categories like "Personal Injury Solicitor," "Family Law Solicitor," or "Wills & Probate Solicitor" helps Google understand your full scope of services, ensuring you appear in more specific, high-intent local searches across Glasgow.

  • Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

    A surprising number of Glasgow law firms have uploaded fewer than 30 high-quality, owner-managed photos to their profile. This usually means potential clients are missing out on seeing your welcoming office interior, professional team headshots, the exterior of your building, or even pictures of your legal team engaging with local Glasgow initiatives. More photos build trust and provide a valuable visual impression.

A complete, professional, and visually rich Google Business Profile instils confidence and trust even before the first call. It demonstrates your professionalism and capacity to handle their specific legal issue, making it more likely they choose your Glasgow law firm over a less visible or less comprehensive listing.
