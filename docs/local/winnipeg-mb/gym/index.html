The three fixes on 80% of audited gym profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short to Shine

    Many Winnipeg gym profiles I audit have a business description that's barely scratching the surface, often under 400 characters. Google allows up to 750. This means you are missing crucial space to highlight your unique offerings like specialized equipment, class types, membership benefits, and your gym's atmosphere. A rich, keyword-optimized description helps potential members understand your value before they even click through to your website.

  • Untapped Potential: Missing Secondary Categories

    One common oversight on many Winnipeg gym profiles is the failure to utilize secondary Google Business Profile categories. Beyond "Gym," you might also be a "CrossFit Gym," "Yoga Studio," or offer "Personal Trainer" services. Each additional relevant category expands the types of searches your business can appear in, capturing a wider audience looking for specific fitness niches you already serve.

  • Your Profile is Visually Lacking: Too Few Photos

    Visual appeal is critical for attracting new members. I frequently find gym profiles with fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, often missing key visuals. Potential members want to see your clean locker rooms, the range of cardio equipment, specific class setups like spin or yoga, your certified trainers in action, and the welcoming reception area. High-quality, diverse photos build trust and excitement, encouraging more visits.

Why these matter specifically for gyms in Winnipeg, MB

  • Winnipeg's fitness scene is surprisingly dense, especially within neighbourhoods like Osborne Village, St. Boniface, and the Exchange District. With numerous gyms, studios, and fitness centres competing for local attention, a fully optimized Google Business Profile isn't just a bonus, it's essential for standing out. Neglecting your profile means competitors with better-maintained listings will simply outrank you.

  • Commute patterns in Winnipeg also play a significant role. Many residents choose a gym based on proximity to their home or work, often preferring a location they can easily access before or after rush hour. An incomplete or inaccurate GBP listing, missing location details or proper categories, means you miss out on "gyms near me" searches when someone is searching from their office downtown or their home in Transcona.

  • Customer search behavior for gyms is highly specific. People aren't just looking for "gym"; they search for "CrossFit Inkster," "yoga classes River Heights," or "personal trainer Fort Garry." If your profile doesn't clearly articulate these specific services through rich descriptions and accurate secondary categories, you're invisible to these high-intent local searches across Winnipeg.

  • Furthermore, in a city known for its variable weather, the convenience factor of finding the right gym quickly is paramount. An audit ensures your hours, amenities, and photos accurately reflect what members can expect, reducing friction for new sign-ups and making your gym the obvious choice over less-detailed competitors, no matter the season.
