The three fixes on 80% of audited landscaper profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many Glasgow landscapers underutilise their Google Business Profile description, often sticking to a few generic sentences. Google allows up to 750 characters, yet most profiles barely hit 300-400. This is a missed opportunity to showcase your unique specialities, highlight your commitment to Glasgow clients, or mention key service areas like West End gardens or Southside patios. An expanded description allows you to integrate more keywords that potential customers are searching for, directly impacting your local search ranking.

  • Missing Key Secondary Service Categories

    Beyond "Landscaper," Google Business Profile offers specific categories that can significantly broaden your visibility. I often find Glasgow landscaping businesses failing to add crucial secondary categories like "Lawn Care Service," "Hardscape Contractor," or "Tree Service." Without these, you're invisible to customers specifically searching for those services. Properly adding these categories ensures your profile appears for a wider range of relevant searches, capturing more targeted leads looking for your exact specialism.

  • Not Enough High-Quality Owner Photos

    Visuals are paramount for landscapers, yet many profiles have fewer than 10-15 owner-uploaded photos, often stock images or low-quality snaps. Your Google Business Profile should be a dynamic portfolio, showcasing before-and-after transformations, diverse garden designs, team members at work, and examples of hardscaping or planting projects across Glasgow. High-quality, numerous images build trust and help potential clients visualise your expertise, making them far more likely to choose your services over a competitor's.
