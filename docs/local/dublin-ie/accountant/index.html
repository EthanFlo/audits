The three fixes on 80% of audited accountant profiles

Underutilised Business Description

Many Dublin accountants use a bare-bones Google Business Profile description. Google allows up to 750 characters, but I often find profiles with under 400. This misses a crucial opportunity to detail your specialisms, like contractor tax, payroll for SMEs, or audit services. A comprehensive description helps clients understand your value before even visiting your site, improving your chances of ranking for specific, valuable long-tail queries.

Missing Secondary Service Categories

Limiting your profile to just "Accountant" severely restricts your visibility. I frequently see profiles missing critical secondary categories. To truly dominate searches in Dublin, you need to explicitly list services like "Tax preparation service," "Bookkeeping service," and "Payroll service." These specific categories help Google understand your full scope, ensuring you appear in diverse searches from businesses and individuals seeking precise financial solutions.

Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

First impressions matter, especially in a professional service. Many accountant profiles in Dublin feature fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, often just a logo or exterior shot. You're missing chances to showcase professional headshots of your team, modern office interiors, client meeting spaces, and even local Dublin landmarks that reinforce your local presence. High-quality, diverse imagery builds trust and helps potential clients feel more comfortable choosing your firm.
