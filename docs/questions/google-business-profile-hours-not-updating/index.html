Google Business Profile Hours Not Updating? Fix

If your Google Business Profile hours are not updating, the most effective solution usually involves re-publishing your changes directly within the Google Business Profile manager or diligently checking for any conflicting information and pending edits.

The Primary Fix: Re-publishing and Syncing

Often, the most straightforward reason for hours not updating is that changes were made but not fully saved, published, or properly synced across Google's various platforms. The Google Business Profile interface requires a deliberate save or apply action to push updates live, even if they appear correct on your screen.

  1. Access Your Google Business Profile: Go to business.google.com or search for your business name directly on Google Search or Maps while logged into your managing account. Look for the "Edit profile" button.
  2. Navigate to Hours: From the editing interface, select "Hours". This will display your current regular hours.
  3. Make or Verify Changes: Adjust any incorrect times. Even if they appear accurate, sometimes simply toggling a day off and then back on, or re-entering the times, can trigger the system to recognize a change.
  4. Apply the Changes: After making your adjustments, look for and click the "Apply" or "Save" button. This crucial step pushes your edits to Google's system.
  5. Monitor for Updates: Allow Google some time to process. While often instant, it can occasionally take a few minutes or up to 24 hours for the update to fully propagate across Google Search, Maps, and other services.

Addressing Pending Changes and Conflicting Information

Google's ecosystem can sometimes present conflicting information or pending changes that prevent your manual updates from appearing. This can stem from user suggestions (anyone can "Suggest an edit" on Google Maps), data aggregated from other online sources, or even old information cached within Google's system. It is essential to review and manage these potential conflicts.

  1. Check for Pending Edits: Within your Google Business Profile dashboard, look for a section or notification regarding "Pending edits" or "Suggestions." Review any proposed changes to your hours.
  2. Accept or Reject: If a suggestion is accurate, accept it. If it is incorrect, reject it. Actively managing these suggestions tells Google which information to prioritize.
  3. Consistency Across Platforms: Ensure your hours are consistent on your website, social media, and other online directories. Discrepancies can lead Google's algorithm to question the accuracy of your Business Profile data.

Understanding Special Hours and Holiday Schedules

A very common reason for apparent hour discrepancies, particularly around holidays or specific events, is the confusion between "regular hours" and "special hours." Regular hours are your standard weekly schedule, while special hours are specific, temporary overrides for holidays, special events, or seasonal changes. Neglecting to set special hours means your regular hours will display, even if your business is actually closed or operating on a modified schedule.

Example: Consider "Brew & Grind Coffee," a popular coffee shop that typically operates from 6 AM to 6 PM, Monday through Friday. If Christmas Eve falls on a Tuesday, and Brew & Grind plans to close early at 2 PM, then be completely closed on Christmas Day, these specific changes need to be entered as special hours. Failure to do so means Google will show their regular 6 AM - 6 PM hours for Christmas Eve and Christmas Day, despite the actual operational changes.

  1. Access Special Hours: From your Google Business Profile editing interface, select "Hours", then look for an option like "Add special hours" or "Holiday hours."
  2. Select the Date: Choose the specific date(s) for which you need to set special hours (e.g., December 24th, December 25th).
  3. Enter Specific Times: For each selected date, input the precise opening and closing times. If the business is closed all day, select the "Closed" option for that date.
  4. Save and Apply: Just like with regular hours, ensure you click the "Apply" or "Save" button to publish these special hours.

Common Pitfalls and Troubleshooting Steps

Beyond the primary methods, several other factors can interfere with your Google Business Profile hours updating correctly. These often relate to technical glitches, account access, or Google's processing delays, making diagnosis a bit more challenging.

  • Browser Cache and Cookies: Clear your browser's cache and cookies, then try accessing and updating your profile again. Using an incognito or private browsing window can also help bypass caching issues.
  • Incorrect Google Account: If you manage multiple Google accounts or business profiles, ensure you are logged into the correct Google Account associated with the specific Business Profile you are trying to edit.
  • Profile Verification Status: An unverified or recently unverified Google Business Profile may have limited editing capabilities or slower update propagation. Confirm your profile is fully verified and in good standing.
  • Google's Processing Time: While most updates are quick, some can take longer. Factors like high traffic or complex edits can extend the waiting period.
  • Third-Party Integrations: If you use a third-party service that syncs data with Google Business Profile, ensure there is not a conflict or an override occurring from that system.

Contacting Google Support

If you have followed all the troubleshooting steps and your Google Business Profile hours still refuse to update after a reasonable waiting period (e.g., 24-48 hours), it might be time to escalate the issue directly to Google support. They have access to backend tools and diagnostics that can identify specific problems with your profile.

  1. Prepare Information: Gather all relevant details: your business name, address, website, the specific hours you are trying to set, screenshots of the problem (if possible), and a list of steps you have already taken.
  2. Access Support: From your Google Business Profile dashboard, look for a "Support" or "Help" option. This usually leads to a help center where you can find articles or initiate contact via email or chat.
  3. Explain Clearly: When you contact support, clearly explain the issue, detailing what hours you are trying to set, what is currently displayed, and how long the problem has persisted. Reference the troubleshooting steps you have already performed.
