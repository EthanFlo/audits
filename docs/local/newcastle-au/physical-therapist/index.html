The three fixes on 80% of audited physical therapist profiles

  • Your Business Description is Undercooked

    Many Newcastle physical therapists use a brief, generic business description, barely scratching the surface of the 750 characters Google allows. This is a missed opportunity to detail your specialisations like concussion therapy, women's health physiotherapy, or dry needling. A well-crafted description with relevant keywords not only informs potential patients but also signals to Google your exact services, boosting relevance for specific searches across areas like Adamstown or Lambton.

  • Overlooking Crucial Secondary Categories

    Beyond 'Physical Therapist', many clinics miss adding vital secondary categories. For example, 'Sports Physiotherapist' is essential for treating athletes. Other crucial but often overlooked categories include 'Pain management clinic' for chronic pain sufferers, or 'Rehabilitation centre' for post-operative care. In Newcastle, patients seek specialists, so missing these categories means missing out on targeted local searches for specific treatments.

  • A Visually Sparse Profile

    Your Google Business Profile should be a visual showcase, yet many Newcastle physical therapy practices have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos. Patients want to see modern equipment, spacious treatment rooms, and friendly reception areas. Are you showcasing your hydrotherapy pool in Wallsend, or your state-of-the-art gym in Broadmeadow? Missing photos of your team, specific services, or clinic exterior leaves potential patients with a sterile impression, failing to build trust and convey your professional environment.
