The three fixes on 80% of audited veterinarian profiles

  • Your Google Business Profile Description is Too Short

    Google allows up to 750 characters for your business description, yet many Seattle veterinarians use less than 400. This is a missed opportunity to showcase your unique services, compassionate approach, and specialized care. A detailed description allows you to include keywords for pet dentistry, emergency services, or specific animal care, helping Google better understand what you offer beyond a generic "veterinarian" listing and attracting pet owners searching for precise needs.

  • Missing Crucial Secondary Categories

    Your primary category is likely "Veterinarian," but Google Business Profile allows for multiple secondary categories that refine your profile. I frequently find practices in Seattle omitting vital categories such as "Emergency Vet," "Animal Hospital," or "Pet Surgery." Without these, you are invisible to pet owners specifically searching for these critical services, limiting your reach and making it harder for Google to match your clinic with diverse search queries.

  • Insufficient Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Most veterinarian profiles I audit have fewer than 30 high-quality, owner-uploaded photos. Pet owners want to see more than just a logo. They look for images of your clean exam rooms, welcoming waiting areas, friendly staff interacting with animals, specialized equipment, and even your clinic's exterior. Missing are reassuring photos of your team providing care or showing the comfortable environment you offer, which builds trust and encourages visits.

  • Seattle's competitive landscape for veterinary services is dense. From bustling neighborhoods like Ballard to family-oriented areas like West Seattle, there are numerous practices vying for attention.   • Commute patterns and convenience are paramount for Seattle pet owners. With traffic congestion and geographical barriers like Lake Union or the Puget Sound, pet owners prioritize finding a reputable vet close by, especially in emergencies. An optimized Google Business Profile ensures you rank prominently in "near me" searches, preventing potential clients from having to navigate significant city travel and instead choosing your accessible practice.

  • Customer search behavior for veterinarians in Seattle is highly specific. Pet owners aren't just searching for "vet"; they often include specialized terms like "cat friendly vet Seattle," "dog teeth cleaning," or "after hours pet clinic." Rich descriptions, appropriate secondary categories, and a strong visual presence on your GBP directly address these detailed queries, building trust and converting searchers into clients who feel confident in your specific services before they even call.
