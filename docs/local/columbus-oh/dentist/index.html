The three fixes on 80% of audited dentist profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many Columbus dentists use a Google Business Profile description that barely scratches the surface, often falling well under the 750-character limit and sometimes even below 400. This means you're missing a critical opportunity to highlight your unique patient approach, advanced technologies, or the specific comfort measures you offer. An incomplete description fails to inform potential patients about what truly sets your practice apart, causing them to scroll past without a second glance.

  • Missing Key Secondary Categories

    Often, dentists only list "Dentist" as their primary category, completely overlooking crucial secondary categories. In our audits, we frequently find practices missing "Cosmetic Dentist," "Pediatric Dentist," or "Emergency Dental" — even when they offer these services. Without these precise categories, Google struggles to match your practice with specific patient searches, significantly reducing your visibility for high-value services that patients in Columbus are actively seeking.

  • Not Enough Owner-Uploaded Photos

    Most dentist profiles we review have fewer than 30 owner-uploaded photos, far below the recommended quantity for robust engagement. Patients want to see more than just your logo. They're looking for images of your welcoming reception area, modern treatment rooms, a friendly team, and even patient success stories. A lack of diverse, high-quality photos makes your practice appear less inviting and trustworthy compared to competitors who showcase their full patient experience.

Patients don't just search for "dentist near me"; they're looking for "pediatric dentist Columbus," "cosmetic dentist German Village," or "emergency dental OSU campus."
