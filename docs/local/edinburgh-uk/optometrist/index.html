The three fixes on 80% of audited optometrist profiles

1. Underutilised Business Description

Many Edinburgh optometrists miss a crucial opportunity by keeping their Google Business Profile description under 400 characters, despite Google allowing up to 750. This means you're not fully detailing specialist services like dry eye clinics, specific lens options, or even your unique patient care philosophy. A comprehensive description helps potential patients understand your full range of expertise, differentiating your practice from the competition before they even visit your website.

2. Missing Secondary Categories

Often, optometrist profiles are listed with only "Optometrist" as their primary category. This neglects the power of secondary categories to capture highly specific searches. We frequently find practices missing key additions like "Eye Exam," "Contact Lens Fitter," and "Pediatric Optometrist." Including these ensures your practice appears when parents search for children's eye care or individuals need specialised contact lens advice in Edinburgh.

3. Fewer Than 30 Owner-Uploaded Photos

Many local optometry practices fall short of the recommended 30+ owner-uploaded photos. This often means missing crucial visuals that build trust and familiarity. We see profiles lacking clear images of your reception area, modern examination rooms, your friendly team, specific eyewear collections, or even unique diagnostic equipment. These photos are vital for showcasing your practice's professional environment and inviting atmosphere to prospective patients.
