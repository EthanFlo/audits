The three fixes on 80% of audited locksmith profiles

  • Your Business Description is Too Short

    Many Denver locksmiths use less than half of the 750 characters Google allows for their business description. This is a massive missed opportunity to showcase your specialties, whether it’s emergency car lockouts near DIA, residential rekeying in Cherry Creek, or commercial security upgrades downtown. A sparse description fails to capture valuable keywords and unique selling points, leaving your profile bland and less informative than a competitor's robust summary.

  • Neglecting Secondary Service Categories

    Simply listing 'Locksmith' as your sole category severely limits your reach. Google Business Profile allows for multiple secondary categories, which are critical for matching diverse customer searches in Denver. Most audited profiles miss optimizing for essential services like 'Emergency Locksmith,' 'Auto Locksmith,' and 'Safe Opening Service.' Each missing category means you're invisible to potential clients specifically searching for those highly-profitable, specialized services.

  • Lack of Engaging Visuals

    Many locksmith profiles in Denver fall short on owner-uploaded photos, often having fewer than 30. High-quality images build trust and show professionalism. I often find profiles missing pictures of their mobile vans, the team in action, completed residential lock installations, commercial security systems, or even clear photos of their service vehicles parked in front of Denver landmarks. These visuals humanize your brand and give potential clients a reason to choose you over a faceless competitor.
