30-point Google Business Profile checklist for real estate agents

Here is your 30-point Google Business Profile checklist for real estate agents:

Generic advice for optimizing your Google Business Profile often falls short because it lacks the actionable detail real estate agents truly need. You're busy showing properties, negotiating deals, and cultivating leads; you don't have time for vague suggestions. What you need is a precise roadmap – a sequential, step-by-step guide that eliminates guesswork and tells you exactly what to do.

This 30-point checklist provides that specificity. Think of it as your blueprint for a powerful GBP presence, designed to be tackled on a slow afternoon or spread across a few focused sessions. Each item is a concrete action, tailored for real estate professionals, ensuring your profile works harder to attract buyers, sellers, and property management clients in your local market.

The three fixes on 80% of audited real estate agent profiles

Business Info

  • ☐ Verify business name, address, and phone number (NAP) exactly as it appears elsewhere online.
  • ☐ Set accurate business hours, including special holiday hours if applicable for showings.
  • ☐ Write a compelling business description highlighting your real estate specialization and unique selling points.
  • ☐ Add your real estate license number to the "Licenses" section for compliance and trust.
  • ☐ Ensure your primary website link goes directly to your agent profile or listings page.

Categories

  • ☐ Set "Real Estate Agent" as your primary business category for maximum visibility.
  • ☐ Add "Buyer's Agent" as a secondary category if you frequently represent purchasers.
  • ☐ Include "Seller's Agent" as another category if assisting clients with property sales.
  • ☐ Select "Property Management Company" if offering services beyond buying/selling homes.
  • ☐ Review all selected categories to ensure they accurately reflect *all* your real estate services.

Photos

  • ☐ Upload a professional headshot as your logo or primary profile photo for recognition.
  • ☐ Add at least five high-quality photos of local landmarks or popular neighborhood spots.
  • ☐ Showcase your team with a group photo to add a personal, trustworthy touch.
  • ☐ Post interior and exterior photos of a recently sold listing (with permission) as a portfolio.
  • ☐ Include a video tour of a representative property or a short client testimonial video.

Services

  • ☐ List "Buyer Representation" with a clear description of your services for homebuyers.
  • ☐ Detail "Seller Representation" outlining your marketing strategies and selling process.
  • ☐ Create a service for "Property Valuation" or "Comparative Market Analysis (CMA)".
  • ☐ Add "Open House Management" or "New Construction Sales" if these are core offerings.
  • ☐ Include specialized services like "First-Time Homebuyer Assistance" or "Investment Property Sourcing".

Reviews

  • ☐ Respond to every new Google review promptly and professionally, positive or negative.
  • ☐ Ask satisfied clients directly for a Google review after closing a successful deal.
  • ☐ Create a direct link to your Google review page for easy sharing with clients.
  • ☐ Acknowledge specific details from positive reviews to show genuine appreciation.
  • ☐ Address negative reviews constructively, offering to resolve issues offline where appropriate.

Posts

  • ☐ Create a "What's New" post announcing a recent local listing or sale achievement.
  • ☐ Publish an "Offer" post detailing a special deal or free consultation for new clients.
  • ☐ Share an "Event" post about an upcoming open house, webinar, or community gathering.
  • ☐ Write a "COVID-19 update" post if specific safety protocols are relevant for showings.
  • ☐ Use a "Product" post to highlight a specific property or a unique real estate service.

Why these matter specifically for real estate agents in Checklist

Once you've diligently worked through all 30 points on this checklist, the work isn't entirely done, but the heavy lifting is. The next crucial step is to monitor your progress. Utilize your Google Business Profile Insights dashboard to track key metrics like views, searches, and actions taken on your profile. Give the platform time to index your changes – typically about 14 days – before making further significant adjustments. After this period, review your performance, identify areas for improvement, and then repeat this checklist process, refining and updating your GBP to maintain a competitive edge.
